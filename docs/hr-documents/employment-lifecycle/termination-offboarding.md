## 1. Purpose

This document outlines procedures for processing employee separations, ensuring consistent treatment, legal compliance, smooth transitions, and protection of company interests.

---

## 2. Types of Separations

### 2.1 Voluntary Separations

**Resignation:**
- Employee-initiated departure
- Should provide written notice
- Standard notice: 2 weeks (4 weeks for management)

**Retirement:**
- Departure for retirement
- Advance notice appreciated for planning
- May be eligible for retirement benefits

### 2.2 Involuntary Separations

**Termination for Cause:**
- Performance issues
- Policy violations
- Misconduct

**Termination Without Cause:**
- Position elimination
- Organizational restructuring
- Business reasons

**Layoff/Reduction in Force:**
- Business-driven workforce reduction
- May involve severance
- WARN Act compliance (if applicable)

### 2.3 Other Separations

**End of Contract:**
- Temporary or contract worker end date

**Mutual Separation:**
- Agreed-upon departure

**Job Abandonment:**
- Three consecutive no-call/no-show days
- Considered voluntary resignation

## 3. Voluntary Resignation Process

### 3.1 Notice Requirements

| Role | Expected Notice |
|------|-----------------|
| Individual Contributor | 2 weeks |
| Manager | 3 weeks |
| Director+ | 4 weeks |
| Executive | 4-8 weeks |

Shorter notice accepted; longer notice appreciated.

### 3.2 Submitting Resignation

**Employee Steps:**
1. Notify manager verbally
2. Submit written resignation (email acceptable)
3. Include last day of work
4. Manager notifies HR

**Written Resignation Should Include:**
- Intent to resign
- Last day of work
- Reason (optional)
- Transition assistance offer

### 3.3 Manager Response

Upon receiving resignation:
1. Accept resignation
2. Notify HR immediately
3. Discuss transition plan
4. Begin knowledge transfer
5. Submit termination request in Workday

### 3.4 Counteroffer Policy

Acme Corporation generally does not make counteroffers:
- Underlying issues often remain
- May create precedent
- Exceptions require VP and HR approval

### 3.5 Working During Notice Period

**Expectations:**
- Work normally through last day
- Complete handoff activities
- Train replacement if possible
- Maintain professional conduct

**PTO During Notice:**
- Generally not approved
- Manager discretion for pre-planned PTO
- Not for extending last day

**Immediate Release:**
- Company may release employee before notice period ends
- Pay through original last day or notice period (whichever policy applies)
- Typically used for sensitive roles

## 4. Involuntary Termination Process

### 4.1 Pre-Termination Requirements

**Documentation Review:**
- HR reviews performance documentation
- Confirm progressive discipline followed (if applicable)
- Verify no protected activity concerns
- Legal review for sensitive cases

**Approvals Required:**

| Level | Approvers |
|-------|-----------|
| IC1-IC3 | Manager + Director + HR |
| IC4+ | Director + VP + HR |
| Manager | VP + HR |
| Director+ | SVP + CHRO |
| Executive | CEO + Board (as applicable) |

### 4.2 Termination Meeting

**Preparation:**
- Schedule private meeting room
- Have HR present
- Prepare final pay information
- Prepare separation documents
- Arrange for property return
- Coordinate IT access revocation

**Meeting Conduct:**
- Be direct and professional
- Keep message brief and clear
- Do not debate the decision
- Provide written notice
- Explain next steps
- Allow questions
- Maintain dignity

**Sample Script:**
> "[Name], thank you for meeting with us. Unfortunately, we've made the decision to end your employment with Acme, effective today. [Brief reason if appropriate]. HR will walk you through the next steps, including final pay and benefits information. We appreciate your contributions and wish you well."

### 4.3 Immediate Actions

**During/After Meeting:**
- IT disables system access
- Building access revoked
- Collect company property
- Provide final pay information
- Escort from building (if required)
- Notify team (per communication plan)

## 5. Layoffs and Reductions in Force

### 5.1 Decision Process

Layoffs require:
- Business justification
- Selection criteria (objective, non-discriminatory)
- Legal review
- Executive approval
- WARN Act analysis (if 100+ employees affected)

### 5.2 Selection Criteria

May include:
- Position elimination (role-based)
- Performance ratings
- Skills and competencies
- Business needs
- Must not discriminate on protected characteristics

### 5.3 WARN Act Compliance

If applicable (100+ employees, mass layoff):
- 60 days advance notice required
- Notice to employees, state, local government
- Penalties for non-compliance

### 5.4 Severance

Severance may be offered for layoffs:
- Based on years of service
- Requires signed separation agreement
- Typically 1-2 weeks per year of service
- Minimum 2 weeks, maximum 26 weeks
- Benefits continuation information (COBRA)

## 6. Final Pay

### 6.1 Timing

Final pay timing varies by state:

| State | Termination Type | Final Pay Due |
|-------|-----------------|---------------|
| California | Involuntary | Immediately |
| California | Voluntary | 72 hours (or immediate if 72 hours notice given) |
| Texas | All | Next regular payday |
| New York | All | Next regular payday |
| Colorado | All | Immediately or next payday depending on circumstances |

### 6.2 Components

Final paycheck includes:
- Wages through last day worked
- Accrued, unused PTO (per state law/policy)
- Earned commissions
- Expense reimbursements
- Deductions for:
  - Benefits (prorated)
  - 401(k) contribution
  - Any amounts owed to company (where legal)

### 6.3 Delivery

- Direct deposit (if enrolled)
- Check mailed to address on file
- In-person pickup (some states require)

## 7. Benefits Continuation

### 7.1 Health Insurance

**COBRA:**
- Continuation of group health coverage
- Up to 18 months (36 for certain qualifying events)
- Employee pays full premium + 2% administrative fee
- COBRA notice provided within 14 days
- 60 days to elect coverage
- Coverage retroactive to termination

**Cost:** Employee pays 102% of premium (employer + employee portions)

### 7.2 Life Insurance

- Company-paid coverage ends on termination date
- Conversion to individual policy available
- Must apply within 31 days

### 7.3 Disability Insurance

- Coverage ends on termination date
- No continuation option

### 7.4 401(k)

**Options:**
- Leave funds in Acme plan (if balance over $7,000)
- Roll over to new employer plan
- Roll over to IRA
- Cash out (taxes and penalties may apply)

**Vesting:**
- Your contributions: Always 100% vested
- Company match: Per vesting schedule

### 7.5 Stock Options

- Vested options: Exercise within 90 days of termination (or per plan terms)
- Unvested options: Forfeited
- Review your grant agreement for specific terms

## 8. Property Return

### 8.1 Company Property to Return

- Laptop computer
- Mobile phone (if company-issued)
- ID badge and keys
- Parking pass
- Credit cards
- Files and documents
- Any other company equipment

### 8.2 Return Process

**On-Site Terminations:**
- Return property during exit meeting
- IT processes device return
- Sign property return form

**Remote Employees:**
- Shipping materials provided
- Return within 5 business days
- Prepaid shipping label provided

### 8.3 Failure to Return

- Employees responsible for lost/unreturned items
- Cost may be deducted from final pay (where legal)
- May affect final pay in some states

## 9. Exit Interview

### 9.1 Purpose

Exit interviews help Acme:
- Understand reasons for leaving
- Identify organizational issues
- Improve retention
- Gather feedback on management

### 9.2 Process

**Voluntary Separations:**
- Scheduled by HR during notice period
- Typically 30-45 minutes
- In-person, phone, or video
- Optional but encouraged

**Involuntary Separations:**
- Generally not conducted
- May be offered in some circumstances

### 9.3 Topics Covered

- Reason for leaving
- Job satisfaction
- Management effectiveness
- Work environment
- Suggestions for improvement
- Would you recommend Acme? Why/why not?

### 9.4 Confidentiality

- Feedback is aggregated and anonymized
- Specific concerns shared with leadership only
- HR determines appropriate follow-up

## 10. Transition Activities

### 10.1 Knowledge Transfer

Departing employees should:
- Document current projects and status
- Update SOPs and documentation
- Train successor (if hired)
- Transfer key relationships
- Provide system access information
- Complete pending approvals

### 10.2 Communication

**Internal:**
- Manager announces departure to team
- Timing coordinated with employee
- Professional and positive tone

**External:**
- Transition client relationships
- Introduce successor
- Update external contacts

### 10.3 Handoff Checklist

- [ ] Current project status documented
- [ ] Files organized and accessible
- [ ] Pending items listed
- [ ] Successor briefed
- [ ] Key contacts notified
- [ ] Recurring meetings transferred
- [ ] Distribution lists updated
- [ ] Auto-reply set for email

## 11. Post-Termination Obligations

### 11.1 Confidentiality

Departing employees must:
- Return all confidential information
- Not disclose confidential information
- Delete company data from personal devices
- Ongoing obligations per signed agreements

### 11.2 Non-Compete (If Applicable)

If you signed a non-compete:
- Review terms of your agreement
- Understand restricted activities
- Understand geographic and time limitations
- Consult legal counsel if questions

### 11.3 Non-Solicitation (If Applicable)

Standard terms may include:
- No soliciting Acme employees (typically 12 months)
- No soliciting Acme clients (typically 12 months)
- Review your specific agreement

### 11.4 Cooperation

Former employees agree to:
- Cooperate with pending matters
- Assist with litigation if needed
- Respond to reasonable requests
- Reasonable compensation for time

## 12. Severance Agreements

### 12.1 When Offered

Severance may be offered for:
- Layoffs and position eliminations
- Mutual separations
- Settlement of disputes
- Generally not for termination for cause

### 12.2 Typical Components

- Severance pay (weeks of salary)
- COBRA subsidy (sometimes)
- Outplacement services
- Reference agreement
- Non-disparagement clause
- Release of claims

### 12.3 Consideration Period

Employees have time to review:
- Under 40: As specified in agreement
- 40 and over: 21 days minimum (OWBPA requirement)
- Group layoffs (40+): 45 days minimum
- 7-day revocation period after signing

### 12.4 Legal Review

Employees encouraged to:
- Review agreement carefully
- Consult with attorney
- Ask questions before signing

## 14. Rehire Eligibility

### 14.1 Categories

**Eligible for Rehire:**
- Voluntary resignation in good standing
- Layoff/RIF
- Satisfactory performance history

**Not Eligible for Rehire:**
- Termination for cause
- Policy violations
- Resignation in lieu of termination
- Job abandonment

### 14.2 Rehire Process

- Apply through normal hiring process
- Previous employment noted
- Manager and HR review eligibility
- No guaranteed return to previous level/salary

### 14.3 Service Credit

If rehired:
- Within 12 months: Prior service may count
- After 12 months: Service resets
- Benefits eligibility per plan terms
