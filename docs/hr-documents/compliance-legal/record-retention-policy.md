## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines for retaining, storing, and disposing of company records. Proper record retention ensures legal compliance, supports business operations, protects the company in litigation, and manages storage costs.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All employees, contractors, and agents
- All records in any format (paper, electronic, audio, video)
- Records created, received, or maintained by Acme Corporation
- All locations and business units

---

## 3. Definitions

**Record:** Any document, data, or information created or received in the course of business that provides evidence of transactions, decisions, or activities. Includes emails, reports, contracts, financial data, personnel files, etc.

**Retention Period:** The minimum time a record must be kept before disposition.

**Disposition:** The final action on a recordâ€”either destruction or transfer to archives.

**Legal Hold:** A suspension of normal destruction procedures when records may be relevant to litigation, investigation, or audit.

**Active Record:** A record in current use for business operations.

**Inactive Record:** A record no longer needed for daily operations but within retention period.

---

## 4. General Principles

### 4.1 Retention Standards

- Retain records for required periods
- Dispose of records when retention period expires (unless legal hold)
- Follow consistent procedures
- Maintain records in accessible format

### 4.2 Record Categories

Records are categorized by:
- **Legal requirements:** Laws and regulations mandating retention
- **Business needs:** Operational necessity
- **Historical value:** Company history and institutional knowledge

### 4.3 When Retention Period Starts

Retention period typically begins:
- At record creation
- At end of contract/agreement
- At end of employment
- At end of fiscal year
- At project completion

Specific triggers noted in retention schedule.

## 6. Legal Holds

### 6.1 What Is a Legal Hold?

A legal hold suspends normal destruction when records may be relevant to:
- Current or anticipated litigation
- Government investigation
- Regulatory inquiry
- Audit

### 6.2 When to Issue Legal Hold

Legal issues a hold when:
- Litigation is filed or threatened
- Investigation is initiated
- Subpoena or discovery request received
- Government inquiry begins
- Other circumstances warrant preservation

### 6.3 Legal Hold Process

**Step 1:** Legal identifies relevant records and custodians

**Step 2:** Legal issues hold notice to custodians

**Step 3:** Custodians confirm receipt and compliance

**Step 4:** Records preserved until hold released

**Step 5:** Legal monitors compliance

**Step 6:** Legal releases hold when appropriate

### 6.4 Employee Responsibilities

When you receive a legal hold notice:
- Stop destroying relevant records immediately
- Preserve all potentially relevant records
- Confirm receipt of the notice
- Ask questions if unsure what to preserve
- Notify Legal before destroying any records

**Failure to comply with a legal hold can result in:**
- Adverse legal consequences for the company
- Sanctions by courts
- Disciplinary action for the employee

## 7. Record Storage

### 7.1 Physical Records

**Active Records:**
- Stored in office or nearby accessible location
- Organized for easy retrieval
- Protected from damage

**Inactive Records:**
- Transferred to records storage facility
- Properly labeled and inventoried
- Retrievable when needed

**Storage Requirements:**
- Secure access
- Climate controlled
- Fire and water protection
- Regular inventory

### 7.2 Electronic Records

**Storage Requirements:**
- Approved company systems only
- Regular backups
- Access controls
- Encryption where appropriate
- Organized file structure

**Prohibited Storage:**
- Personal devices (without approval)
- Personal cloud services
- Unapproved external drives
- Personal email accounts

### 7.3 Email Management

- Use company email for business communications
- File important emails appropriately
- Delete routine emails regularly
- Do not use email as document storage system
- Subject to legal holds when applicable

## 8. Record Destruction

### 8.1 When to Destroy

Records should be destroyed when:
- Retention period has expired
- No legal hold in place
- No ongoing business need
- Approved for destruction

### 8.2 Destruction Methods

**Paper Records:**
- Shredding (cross-cut preferred)
- Pulping
- Incineration
- Certified destruction service

**Electronic Records:**
- Secure deletion
- Degaussing
- Physical destruction of media
- Certified data destruction

### 8.3 Destruction Documentation

Maintain destruction logs including:
- Record type and description
- Destruction date
- Method of destruction
- Name of person authorizing

### 8.4 What NOT to Destroy

Never destroy records:
- Subject to legal hold
- Before retention period expires
- Needed for ongoing business
- Under investigation
- Without proper authorization

## 11. Exceptions

### 11.1 Requesting Exceptions

To retain records beyond scheduled period:
- Submit written request to Legal
- Explain business need
- Obtain approval before retention period expires

To destroy records before scheduled period:
- Generally not permitted
- Requires Legal approval
- Must document justification

### 11.2 Emergency Situations

In emergencies (fire, flood, etc.):
- Prioritize employee safety
- Document damaged records
- Report to Legal and Records Management
- Determine if records can be reconstructed
