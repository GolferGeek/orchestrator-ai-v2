## 3. Employee Responsibilities

### 3.1 All Employees Must:

- Follow all safety rules, procedures, and instructions
- Use required personal protective equipment (PPE)
- Report hazards, incidents, and near-misses immediately
- Maintain clean and organized work areas
- Know emergency procedures for their work area
- Complete required safety training
- Not engage in horseplay or unsafe behavior

### 3.2 Managers Must:

- Ensure employees are trained on safety procedures
- Enforce safety rules consistently
- Investigate incidents and near-misses
- Correct hazards promptly
- Lead by example
- Include safety in performance discussions
- Conduct regular safety observations

## 4. Hazard Identification and Reporting

### 4.1 Types of Hazards

**Physical Hazards:** Slips, trips, falls, ergonomic issues, noise, electrical

**Environmental Hazards:** Poor air quality, temperature extremes, lighting

**Chemical Hazards:** Cleaning products, toner, batteries

**Biological Hazards:** Illness, mold, pests

**Security Hazards:** Violence, theft, unauthorized access

### 4.2 Reporting Hazards

Report hazards immediately to:
- Your supervisor
- Facilities: facilities@acmecorp.com, ext. 5550
- Safety Manager: angela.foster@acmecorp.com, ext. 5560
- Safety Hotline: ext. 5555 (for urgent issues)
- Online: safety.acmecorp.com

All hazard reports are investigated within 24 hours. Critical hazards are addressed immediately.

### 4.3 Hazard Correction

Hazards are corrected based on risk:

| Risk Level | Response Time | Examples |
|------------|---------------|----------|
| Critical | Immediate | Exposed wiring, gas leak, fire |
| High | 24 hours | Blocked exits, broken stairs, missing guards |
| Medium | 1 week | Poor lighting, loose carpeting, malfunctioning equipment |
| Low | 30 days | Minor ergonomic issues, cosmetic damage |

## 5. Incident Reporting and Investigation

### 5.1 What to Report

All employees must report:
- Injuries (no matter how minor)
- Illnesses related to work
- Near-misses (incidents that could have caused injury)
- Property damage
- Safety equipment malfunctions
- Security incidents

### 5.2 How to Report

**Emergencies:** Call 911, then Security at ext. 5555

**Non-Emergencies:**
- Notify your supervisor immediately
- Complete an Incident Report Form (available on HR portal)
- Submit within 24 hours of the incident

### 5.3 Investigation Process

All incidents are investigated to:
- Determine root causes
- Identify corrective actions
- Prevent recurrence

Investigations include interviews, evidence review, and analysis. Findings are documented and tracked.

### 5.4 Workers' Compensation

Employees injured on the job may be entitled to workers' compensation benefits. Report injuries to HR within 24 hours. HR will provide necessary forms and information about benefits.

## 6. Emergency Procedures

### 6.1 Emergency Numbers

| Emergency | Number |
|-----------|--------|
| Police/Fire/Medical | 911 |
| Security Operations Center | ext. 5555 |
| Facilities Emergency | ext. 5550 |
| Safety Manager | ext. 5560 |

### 6.2 Fire Emergency

If you discover a fire:
1. Activate the nearest fire alarm pull station
2. Call 911 and Security (ext. 5555)
3. Evacuate using nearest exit
4. Do not use elevators
5. Close doors behind you
6. Go to your designated assembly area
7. Do not re-enter until authorized

If you hear a fire alarm:
1. Stop work immediately
2. Evacuate using nearest exit
3. Close doors behind you
4. Do not use elevators
5. Go to your designated assembly area
6. Follow Floor Warden instructions

### 6.3 Medical Emergency

If someone is injured or ill:
1. Call 911 for life-threatening emergencies
2. Call Security (ext. 5555)
3. Do not move the person unless in danger
4. Provide first aid if trained
5. Stay with the person until help arrives
6. Clear the area for emergency responders

**AED Locations:**
- Austin HQ: Main lobby, 2nd floor break room, 4th floor near elevators
- All locations: Posted on emergency maps

### 6.4 Severe Weather

**Tornado Warning:**
1. Proceed to interior rooms/hallways on lowest floor
2. Stay away from windows
3. Crouch low, protect head and neck
4. Monitor for updates
5. Remain in shelter until all-clear

**Thunderstorm/Lightning:**
- Stay indoors
- Avoid windows
- Do not use landline phones
- Wait 30 minutes after last thunder to resume outdoor activities

### 6.5 Workplace Violence

If you witness violence or threats:
1. Remove yourself from danger
2. Call 911 and Security (ext. 5555)
3. Do not confront the individual
4. Evacuate or shelter in place as appropriate
5. Follow instructions from security/law enforcement

**Active Shooter:**
- **RUN:** Evacuate if safe path exists
- **HIDE:** Find a secure location, lock/barricade doors, silence phones
- **FIGHT:** As last resort, act aggressively against the shooter

### 6.6 Power Outage

1. Remain calm
2. Emergency lighting will activate
3. Save your work (battery backup provides 15 minutes)
4. Do not use elevators
5. Follow instructions from Facilities
6. If extended, you may be asked to go home

### 6.7 Evacuation Assembly Areas

## 7. Fire Prevention

### 7.1 General Requirements

- Know the location of fire extinguishers and alarms
- Keep exits, hallways, and stairwells clear
- Do not block fire doors or prop them open
- Do not overload electrical outlets
- Report frayed cords and faulty equipment
- No open flames without approval
- No personal heaters without approval

### 7.2 Fire Extinguisher Use (PASS Method)

**P** - Pull the pin
**A** - Aim at the base of the fire
**S** - Squeeze the handle
**S** - Sweep side to side

Only attempt to extinguish small fires. Evacuate if:
- Fire is larger than a wastebasket
- Smoke is heavy
- You cannot get to an exit
- Your instincts tell you to leave

### 7.3 Fire Drills

Fire drills are conducted quarterly. All employees must participate. Drills are evaluated for:
- Complete evacuation
- Evacuation time (target: under 5 minutes)
- Proper use of exits
- Assembly area compliance

## 8. Ergonomics

### 8.1 Workstation Setup

Proper workstation setup prevents musculoskeletal injuries:

**Chair:**
- Feet flat on floor or footrest
- Thighs parallel to floor
- Lumbar support positioned at lower back
- Armrests support forearms at desk height

**Monitor:**
- Top of screen at or slightly below eye level
- 20-26 inches from eyes
- Directly in front of you

**Keyboard and Mouse:**
- Elbows at 90-degree angle
- Wrists straight, not bent
- Mouse close to keyboard

**Lighting:**
- Avoid glare on screen
- Position monitor perpendicular to windows
- Use task lighting as needed

### 8.2 Ergonomic Assessments

Request a free ergonomic assessment through HR portal or by contacting:
- Facilities: facilities@acmecorp.com, ext. 5550
- Safety Manager: angela.foster@acmecorp.com, ext. 5560

Assessments include workstation evaluation and equipment recommendations.

### 8.3 Taking Breaks

- Take short breaks every 30-60 minutes
- Look away from screen every 20 minutes (20-20-20 rule)
- Stretch regularly
- Change positions throughout the day
- Use standing desks if available

## 9. Office Safety

### 9.1 Preventing Slips, Trips, and Falls

- Clean up spills immediately
- Report wet floors to Facilities
- Keep walkways clear of cords, boxes, and clutter
- Close file cabinet drawers when not in use
- Use handrails on stairs
- Walk, don't run
- Wear appropriate footwear
- Use step stools, not chairs

### 9.2 Electrical Safety

- Do not overload outlets
- Use surge protectors for electronics
- Report frayed cords and damaged outlets
- Do not use personal appliances without approval
- Unplug non-essential items at day's end
- Keep liquids away from electronics

### 9.3 Material Handling

- Use proper lifting technique (bend knees, keep back straight)
- Get help with heavy items (over 35 lbs)
- Use carts and dollies when available
- Do not block your view when carrying items
- Stack materials safely

### 9.4 Kitchen and Break Room Safety

- Clean up after yourself
- Do not leave food unattended while cooking
- Report malfunctioning appliances
- Keep flammables away from heat sources
- Do not store personal medications in shared refrigerators

## 14. Enforcement

### 14.1 Progressive Discipline

Safety violations are subject to progressive discipline:

1. **First Violation:** Verbal warning and retraining
2. **Second Violation:** Written warning
3. **Third Violation:** Suspension
4. **Fourth Violation:** Termination

Serious violations (willful disregard, endangering others) may result in immediate termination.

### 14.2 Incentives

We believe in positive reinforcement:
- Safety performance in annual reviews
- Department recognition programs
- Safety suggestion rewards
