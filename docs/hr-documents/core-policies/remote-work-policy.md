**Effective Date:** March 15, 2020
**Last Revised:** November 22, 2025
**Version:** 4.0
**Document Owner:** Human Resources Department
**Approved By:** Sarah Mitchell, Chief Human Resources Officer

## 1. Policy Statement

Acme Corporation supports flexible work arrangements that promote work-life balance while maintaining productivity and collaboration. Remote work is a privilege, not an entitlement, and is offered when it aligns with business needs and job requirements.

This policy establishes guidelines for remote work arrangements to ensure consistency, accountability, and continued high performance.

---

## 2. Definitions

**Remote Work:** Working from a location other than an Acme Corporation office on a regular basis.

**Hybrid Work:** A combination of in-office and remote work, typically on a set schedule.

**Fully Remote:** Working remotely 100% of the time, with no regular in-office requirement.

**Occasional Remote Work:** Working from home occasionally (less than 20% of time) with manager approval.

**Remote Work Location:** The primary location where an employee performs remote work, typically their home.

---

## 3. Eligibility

### 3.1 Position Eligibility

Not all positions are suitable for remote work. Eligibility depends on:

**Job Functions:**
- Work can be performed independently
- Minimal need for in-person collaboration or equipment
- Results easily measurable
- No physical presence requirements

**Examples of Eligible Roles:**
- Software developers
- Technical writers
- Financial analysts
- Marketing specialists
- Project managers
- Customer support (with proper setup)

**Examples of Non-Eligible Roles:**
- Facilities staff
- Security personnel
- Lab technicians
- Receptionist
- Warehouse workers
- On-site IT support

### 3.2 Employee Eligibility

Employees must meet the following criteria:
- Completed initial training period (minimum 90 days)
- Performance rating of "Meets Expectations" or above
- No active performance improvement plans
- No attendance or disciplinary issues
- Demonstrated ability to work independently
- Suitable home work environment

### 3.3 Manager Discretion

Managers have discretion to approve or deny remote work based on:
- Team needs and workload
- Client requirements
- Project phases
- Past performance in remote settings
- Business requirements

---

## 4. Remote Work Arrangements

### 4.1 Hybrid Schedule

Most remote-eligible employees work a hybrid schedule:

**Standard Hybrid:** 3 days in-office, 2 days remote
- Required in-office days: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- Remote days: Monday and Friday

**Modified Hybrid:** Alternative schedules may be approved for specific business reasons
- Must include at least 2 in-office days per week
- Schedule must be consistent week-to-week

### 4.2 Fully Remote

Fully remote arrangements are approved for:
- Positions specifically designated as fully remote
- Employees in locations without an Acme office
- Approved accommodations

Fully remote employees must:
- Travel to assigned office quarterly (or as required)
- Attend company events and meetings as required
- Maintain a suitable home office

### 4.3 Requesting Remote Work

To request a remote work arrangement:
1. Discuss with your manager
2. Complete the Remote Work Request Form (HR portal)
3. Submit to manager for approval
4. HR reviews for policy compliance
5. Complete Remote Work Agreement if approved

Requests should be submitted at least 2 weeks before the desired start date.

---

## 5. Remote Work Agreement

### 5.1 Requirements

Approved remote workers must sign a Remote Work Agreement covering:
- Work schedule and availability
- Remote work location
- Equipment and technology requirements
- Performance expectations
- Communication requirements
- Confidentiality and security
- Duration and review process

### 5.2 Duration

Remote work arrangements are:
- Reviewed annually during performance reviews
- Subject to change with 30 days' notice
- Revocable at any time for business reasons or performance issues

### 5.3 Changes

To change an existing remote work arrangement:
- Submit a new request through the HR portal
- Receive manager and HR approval
- Sign an updated Remote Work Agreement

---

## 6. Work Schedule and Availability

### 6.1 Working Hours

Remote employees must:
- Work their scheduled hours (typically 40 hours/week for full-time)
- Be available during core hours (10:00 AM - 3:00 PM local time)
- Respond to communications promptly (within 2 hours during business hours)
- Attend scheduled meetings

### 6.2 Time Zone Considerations

For employees in different time zones:
- Core hours may be adjusted by agreement
- Schedule should overlap at least 4 hours with team members
- Meeting times should rotate to share the burden of off-hours meetings

### 6.3 Schedule Communication

Remote employees must:
- Keep calendar updated with working hours and availability
- Set appropriate status in Slack/Teams
- Communicate schedule changes in advance
- Notify team of any interruptions or unavailability

---

## 7. Workspace Requirements

### 7.1 Home Office Setup

Remote work locations must have:

**Essential:**
- Dedicated workspace (not required to be a separate room)
- Reliable high-speed internet (minimum 50 Mbps download, 10 Mbps upload)
- Quiet environment suitable for video calls
- Proper lighting for video conferences
- Ergonomic setup (chair, desk at proper height)

**Recommended:**
- Separate room with door
- External monitor
- Quality webcam and microphone
- Backup internet option (mobile hotspot)

### 7.2 Equipment

**Company-Provided:**
- Laptop computer
- Required software licenses
- VPN access
- Collaboration tools

**Employee-Provided:**
- Internet service
- Phone (unless business line required)
- Desk and chair
- Additional monitors (some departments provide)

**Equipment Stipend:**
Eligible remote workers receive a one-time $500 home office stipend. See the Equipment Stipend Policy for details.

### 7.3 Workspace Safety

Employees are responsible for maintaining a safe home workspace:
- Free from hazards
- Proper electrical setup (no overloaded outlets)
- Ergonomic to prevent injury
- Secure from unauthorized access

---

## 8. Technology and Security

### 8.1 Required Security Measures

Remote workers must:
- Use company-provided equipment for work
- Connect through VPN when accessing company systems
- Use secure Wi-Fi (WPA2 or higher)
- Enable full-disk encryption
- Keep software updated
- Use company-approved applications
- Lock computer when away

### 8.2 Prohibited Actions

Remote workers must not:
- Use public Wi-Fi for work without VPN
- Allow family members to use work devices
- Download unauthorized software
- Store confidential data on personal devices
- Print sensitive documents at home (unless approved)
- Use personal email for work communications

### 8.3 Data Security

Confidential information requires extra protection:
- No confidential conversations in public spaces
- Secure storage of any printed documents
- Shredding of confidential documents
- Screen positioned away from others' view
- Use of privacy screens when in public

### 8.4 Technical Support

IT support is available for remote workers:
- Help Desk: helpdesk@acmecorp.com, ext. 5000
- Hours: 7:00 AM - 7:00 PM (local time)
- Remote support available for most issues
- On-site support requires office visit

---

## 9. Communication and Collaboration

### 9.1 Communication Expectations

Remote workers must:
- Check email and messages regularly
- Respond within 2 hours during business hours
- Attend all scheduled meetings
- Keep camera on during video meetings (when possible)
- Participate actively in team communications

### 9.2 Tools

Standard collaboration tools include:
- Microsoft Teams (video, chat, collaboration)
- Slack (team communication)
- Zoom (external meetings)
- Outlook (email and calendar)
- SharePoint (document sharing)
- Jira/Confluence (project management)

### 9.3 Meetings

**Meeting Best Practices:**
- Be punctual
- Test technology before important meetings
- Use video when possible
- Mute when not speaking
- Minimize background noise and distractions
- Be fully present (no multitasking)

**Required Attendance:**
Remote workers must attend:
- All scheduled team meetings
- One-on-ones with manager
- Company all-hands meetings
- Required training sessions

### 9.4 In-Office Days

On required in-office days, employees should prioritize:
- Face-to-face collaboration
- Team building activities
- Client meetings
- Training and mentoring
- Complex problem-solving

---

## 10. Performance Management

### 10.1 Expectations

Remote workers are held to the same performance standards as in-office employees. Performance is measured by:
- Achievement of goals and deliverables
- Quality of work
- Meeting deadlines
- Collaboration and communication
- Responsiveness

### 10.2 Manager Responsibilities

Managers of remote workers should:
- Set clear expectations and deliverables
- Conduct regular check-ins (at least weekly)
- Provide timely feedback
- Ensure equitable treatment of remote and in-office employees
- Address performance issues promptly

### 10.3 Employee Responsibilities

Remote workers should:
- Maintain productivity and quality
- Communicate proactively about progress and challenges
- Seek help when needed
- Complete work on time
- Participate in team activities

### 10.4 Performance Issues

Remote work arrangements may be modified or revoked if:
- Performance declines
- Communication is inadequate
- Deadlines are missed
- Collaboration suffers
- Business needs change

## 11. Time Off and Absences

### 11.1 PTO and Sick Leave

Remote workers follow the same PTO and sick leave policies as in-office employees:
- Request PTO through Workday
- Notify supervisor of sick days
- Do not work while on PTO or sick leave

### 11.2 Partial Day Absences

For appointments or personal needs during work hours:
- Communicate with your manager
- Block time on calendar
- Make up time if non-exempt
- Use PTO for extended absences

### 11.3 Sick Children/Dependents

Remote work is not a substitute for childcare or eldercare:
- Employees must have dependent care arrangements during work hours
- Brief interruptions are understandable
- Extended caregiving requires PTO or leave

## 12. Expenses and Reimbursement

### 12.1 Company-Paid Expenses

Acme Corporation provides or pays for:
- Company-owned equipment
- Required software and licenses
- Home office stipend (one-time $500)
- Cell phone stipend ($50/month for eligible employees)

### 12.2 Employee-Paid Expenses

Employees are responsible for:
- Internet service
- Electricity and utilities
- Office furniture beyond stipend
- Personal office supplies

### 12.3 Travel Expenses

When remote workers travel to the office:
- Fully remote workers: Travel expenses reimbursed per Travel Policy
- Hybrid workers: No reimbursement for regular commute
- All workers: Expenses for special meetings/events per Travel Policy

## 13. Compliance and Legal

### 13.1 Wage and Hour Laws

Non-exempt remote workers must:
- Accurately track all time worked
- Take required breaks
- Not work off-the-clock
- Obtain approval for overtime

### 13.2 Workers' Compensation

Injuries occurring in the home workspace during work hours may be covered by workers' compensation. Report any work-related injuries immediately.

### 13.3 Tax Implications

Remote work may have tax implications:
- Multi-state taxation if working from different state
- Home office deductions (consult tax advisor)
- State-specific requirements

Acme Corporation does not provide tax advice. Consult a tax professional for personal situations.

### 13.4 Location Requirements

Employees must notify HR if working from a location other than their approved remote work site for more than 2 weeks:
- Different state or country
- Temporary relocation
- Extended travel

Some locations may not be approved due to tax, legal, or security concerns.

---

## 14. International Remote Work

### 14.1 General Policy

Working remotely from outside the United States requires:
- Advance approval from manager and HR
- Legal review
- Compliance with local laws
- Security assessment

### 14.2 Short-Term International Work

Temporary international remote work (up to 30 days):
- Submit request at least 30 days in advance
- Limited to countries with appropriate infrastructure and security
- Subject to time zone and connectivity requirements

### 14.3 Prohibited Locations

Remote work is prohibited from:
- Countries under U.S. sanctions
- Locations with inadequate internet security
- Areas with travel warnings

---

## 15. Termination of Remote Work

### 15.1 Voluntary Return to Office

Employees may request to return to full-time office work at any time.

### 15.2 Involuntary Termination of Remote Work

Remote work privileges may be revoked for:
- Performance issues
- Attendance or communication problems
- Security violations
- Business needs
- Position changes

30 days' notice will be provided when possible.

### 15.3 Employment Termination

Upon termination of employment:
- Return all company equipment within 5 business days
- Shipping materials provided for equipment return
- Final pay processed per standard procedures
