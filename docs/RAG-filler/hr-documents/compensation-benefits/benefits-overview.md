Employees working 30+ hours per week are eligible for all benefits.

## Health Savings Account (HSA)

### Eligibility
- Must be enrolled in a qualifying high-deductible health plan (Bronze or Silver PPO)
- Cannot be enrolled in Medicare or claimed as a dependent on someone else's taxes

### 2025 Contribution Limits
| Coverage | IRS Limit | Acme Contribution | Your Max |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|----------|
| Individual | $4,300 | $750 | $3,550 |
| Family | $8,550 | $1,500 | $7,050 |
| Catch-up (55+) | Additional $1,000 | - | $1,000 |

### Benefits
- Triple tax advantage: Pre-tax contributions, tax-free growth, tax-free withdrawals for medical expenses
- Funds roll over year to year
- Portable if you leave Acme
- Investment options available once balance exceeds $1,000

## Flexible Spending Accounts (FSA)

### Healthcare FSA
- 2025 contribution limit: $3,200
- Use for medical, dental, vision expenses not covered by insurance
- $660 carryover to following year
- Cannot be combined with HSA

### Dependent Care FSA
- 2025 contribution limit: $5,000 ($2,500 if married filing separately)
- Use for childcare, elder care, preschool, summer day camp
- Use-it-or-lose-it (no carryover)

### FSA Eligible Expenses
- Copays and deductibles
- Prescription medications
- Eyeglasses and contacts
- Dental work
- Hearing aids
- Medical equipment
- Certain OTC medications

## Disability Insurance

### Short-Term Disability (Company-Paid)
- Coverage: 60% of weekly salary (up to $2,500/week)
- Waiting period: 7 days for illness, 0 days for injury
- Duration: Up to 12 weeks
- Automatic enrollment

### Long-Term Disability (Company-Paid)
- Coverage: 60% of monthly salary (up to $15,000/month)
- Waiting period: 90 days
- Duration: Until age 65 or recovery
- Automatic enrollment

### Voluntary Long-Term Disability Buy-Up
- Increase coverage to 66.67% of salary
- Employee-paid through payroll deduction
- Rate based on age and salary

## Leave Programs

### Parental Leave
- 12 weeks paid leave for all new parents
- Applies to birth, adoption, and foster placement
- Must be taken within 12 months of event
- Runs concurrent with FMLA where applicable

### Family & Medical Leave (FMLA)
- Up to 12 weeks unpaid, job-protected leave
- For serious health conditions, family care, military family
- Must have 12 months service and 1,250 hours worked
- Benefits continue during leave (employee pays premiums)

### Bereavement Leave
- Immediate family (spouse, child, parent, sibling): 5 days
- Extended family (grandparent, in-law): 3 days
- Close friend or colleague: 1 day

### Jury Duty
- Up to 10 days paid per year
- Must provide jury summons to manager

### Military Leave
- Per USERRA requirements
- Pay differential for up to 2 weeks of training annually
- Benefits continue per law

## Enrollment

### Initial Enrollment
- Enroll within 30 days of hire date
- Log into benefits.acmecorp.com
- Select coverage and dependents
- Designate beneficiaries

### Annual Open Enrollment
- Typically held in November
- Changes effective January 1
- You will receive communication in October

### Qualifying Life Events
You may make changes outside open enrollment within 30 days of:
- Marriage or divorce
- Birth or adoption
- Death of dependent
- Loss of other coverage
- Change in employment status
- Dependent aging out

Report life events to HR and make changes in Workday.
