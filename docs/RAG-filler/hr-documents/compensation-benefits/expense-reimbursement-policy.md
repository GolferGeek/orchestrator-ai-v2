# Acme Corporation Expense Reimbursement Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024
**Last Revised:** November 22, 2025
**Version:** 2.2
**Document Owner:** Finance Department
**Approved By:** Michael Chen, Chief Financial Officer

---

## 1. Policy Statement

Acme Corporation reimburses employees for reasonable and necessary business expenses incurred while conducting company business. This policy establishes guidelines for expense reporting, approval, and reimbursement.

---

## 2. General Principles

### 2.1 Core Guidelines

- Expenses must be necessary and reasonable for business purposes
- Expenses must be properly documented with receipts
- Expenses must be submitted timely (within 30 days)
- Expenses must be pre-approved when required
- Personal expenses are never reimbursable

### 2.2 Fiscal Responsibility

Employees should:
- Use good judgment as if spending their own money
- Choose cost-effective options
- Avoid extravagance
- Combine trips when possible
- Book travel in advance for best rates

### 2.3 Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following are not reimbursable:
- Personal items and services
- Alcohol (except for approved client entertainment)
- Traffic fines and parking tickets
- Personal travel insurance
- Childcare and pet care
- Personal phone calls beyond reasonable limits
- First-class airfare (without prior approval)
- Movies, mini-bar, and in-room entertainment
- Spa and gym services
- Clothing and personal care items
- Lost or stolen items
- Tips exceeding 20%
- Credit card interest or late fees

---

## 3. Travel Expenses

### 3.1 Air Travel

**Booking Requirements:**
- Book through corporate travel portal (Concur) or approved travel agent
- Book at least 14 days in advance when possible
- Select economy/coach class for domestic flights under 6 hours
- Business class permitted for international flights over 6 hours

**Airfare Limits:**
| Flight Type | Class |
|-------------|-------|
| Domestic (under 6 hours) | Economy |
| Domestic (6+ hours) | Premium Economy (with approval) |
| International (under 6 hours) | Economy |
| International (6+ hours) | Business Class |

**Baggage:**
- One checked bag reimbursable
- Additional bags require justification

### 3.2 Ground Transportation

**Rental Cars:**
- Compact or intermediate class preferred
- Full-size or SUV with justification (group travel, equipment)
- Always accept Collision Damage Waiver (CDW)
- Refuel before returning

**Approved Rental Agencies:** Enterprise, National, Hertz

**Ride-Share/Taxi:**
- Permitted for airport transfers
- Permitted when more cost-effective than rental car
- Document business purpose

**Personal Vehicle:**
- Reimbursed at IRS standard mileage rate: $0.70/mile (2025)
- Log starting and ending odometer readings
- Parking and tolls reimbursed with receipts

### 3.3 Lodging

**Booking Requirements:**
- Use corporate travel portal when possible
- Select standard room at business-class hotel
- Choose hotels near meeting location to minimize transportation

**Nightly Limits (excluding taxes):**
| City Tier | Maximum Rate |
|-----------|-------------|
| Tier 1 (NYC, SF, LA, Boston) | $350 |
| Tier 2 (Chicago, DC, Seattle, Austin) | $275 |
| Tier 3 (Other major cities) | $225 |
| Tier 4 (Suburban/rural) | $175 |

**Approved Hotel Chains:** Marriott, Hilton, Hyatt, IHG (corporate rates available)

**Hotel Amenities:**
- Internet: Reimbursable
- Parking: Reimbursable with receipt
- Room service: Subject to meal limits
- Mini-bar: Not reimbursable
- Movies/entertainment: Not reimbursable

### 3.4 Meals During Travel

**Per Diem Limits (per day):**
| City Tier | Daily Maximum |
|-----------|---------------|
| Tier 1 | $100 |
| Tier 2 | $85 |
| Tier 3 | $70 |
| Tier 4 | $55 |

**Per Meal Limits:**
| Meal | Maximum |
|------|---------|
| Breakfast | $20 |
| Lunch | $25 |
| Dinner | $45 |

**Guidelines:**
- Itemized receipts required for meals over $25
- Tips up to 20% included in limits
- Alcohol with meals: Up to $15 per person (with dinner only)
- Meals included in conference registration reduce per diem

### 3.5 International Travel

**Additional Requirements:**
- Pre-approval required for all international travel
- Business class airfare permitted for flights over 6 hours
- Currency conversion at actual exchange rate
- Keep all receipts in original currency
- Passport and visa fees reimbursable
- Required vaccinations reimbursable

## 4. Business Meals and Entertainment

### 4.1 Client Entertainment

**Approval Thresholds:**
| Amount | Approval Required |
|--------|------------------|
| Under $150 | Manager |
| $150 - $500 | Director |
| $500 - $1,000 | VP |
| Over $1,000 | CFO |

**Requirements:**
- Must have clear business purpose
- Document client names and companies
- Document business topics discussed
- Receipt required for all entertainment
- Alcohol permitted but must be reasonable

### 4.2 Team Meals and Events

**Guidelines:**
- Pre-approval required from manager
- Per-person limit: $75
- Alcohol up to $20 per person
- Document attendees and business purpose

### 4.3 Working Meals

Meals with colleagues during normal business hours:
- Permitted when meeting extends through meal time
- Not permitted for routine lunches without business purpose
- Per-meal limits apply

---

## 5. Technology and Equipment

### 5.1 Mobile Devices

- Company-issued devices: All costs covered
- Personal device stipend: $50/month for eligible employees
- Cases and accessories: Up to $50/year

### 5.2 Home Office Equipment

For approved remote workers:
- One-time stipend: $500
- Annual supplies: $100
- Major equipment requires pre-approval

### 5.3 Software and Subscriptions

- Must be pre-approved by IT and manager
- No reimbursement for personal subscriptions
- Request through IT procurement

---

## 6. Professional Development

### 6.1 Training and Conferences

- Pre-approval required
- Annual budget: $2,500 per employee (varies by department)
- Registration, travel, and materials covered
- Must align with job responsibilities or career development

### 6.2 Professional Memberships

- Job-related memberships reimbursable
- Maximum: $500/year without VP approval
- Examples: Professional associations, industry groups

### 6.3 Certifications

- Job-related certification exams reimbursable
- Study materials: Up to $500
- Retake policy: One retake reimbursed

---

## 7. Other Reimbursable Expenses

### 7.1 Office Supplies

- Purchase through approved vendors when possible
- Receipts required for out-of-pocket purchases
- Must be for business use only

### 7.2 Postage and Shipping

- Shipping of business materials reimbursable
- Use company accounts when possible
- Expedited shipping requires justification

### 7.3 Communication

- Business calls on personal phone: Reimbursable if no stipend
- International calling: Pre-approve or use company calling card
- Internet during travel: Reimbursable

### 7.4 Client Gifts

- Maximum: $100 per client per year
- Prohibited: Cash, gift cards, tobacco, lottery tickets
- Must comply with client's gift policies
- Government clients: May not accept gifts

## 8. Expense Reporting Process

### 8.1 Expense Management System

All expenses submitted through **Concur**

**Access:** concur.acmecorp.com or mobile app

### 8.2 Submission Requirements

**Timeline:**
- Submit expenses within 30 days of incurring
- Month-end cut-off: 5th business day of following month
- Year-end cut-off: January 10 for prior year expenses

**Required Information:**
- Date of expense
- Vendor name
- Amount
- Business purpose
- Attendees (for meals/entertainment)
- Project code (if applicable)

### 8.3 Receipt Requirements

**Receipts Required:**
- All expenses $25 or more
- All meals, entertainment, and travel regardless of amount
- All airfare, hotel, and rental car charges

**Lost Receipts:**
- Complete Lost Receipt Affidavit
- Manager approval required
- Limited to 3 per year
- Audit may request additional documentation

### 8.4 Corporate Credit Card

**Eligible Employees:**
- Managers and above
- Frequent travelers
- Approved by Finance

**Card Policies:**
- Business expenses only
- No cash advances
- No personal charges (even if repaid)
- Submit expenses before statement due date
- Late submissions result in payroll deduction

### 8.5 Approval Workflow

| Report Amount | Approver |
|---------------|----------|
| Under $500 | Manager |
| $500 - $2,500 | Director |
| $2,500 - $10,000 | VP |
| Over $10,000 | CFO |

---

## 9. Reimbursement Timeline

- Reports approved by manager: 3 business days
- Approved reports to Finance: Weekly processing
- Payment via direct deposit: Within 5 business days of Finance approval
- Total typical timeline: 7-10 business days

---

## 10. Auditing and Compliance

### 10.1 Random Audits

Finance conducts random audits of expense reports. Be prepared to:
- Provide additional documentation
- Explain business purpose
- Justify unusual expenses

### 10.2 Red Flags

Reports flagged for review include:
- Unusual patterns or amounts
- Round dollar amounts
- Multiple expenses just under approval thresholds
- Expenses on weekends or holidays without explanation
- Entertainment without documented attendees

### 10.3 Violations

Policy violations may result in:
- Denial of reimbursement
- Revocation of corporate credit card
- Disciplinary action up to termination
- Repayment of improperly claimed expenses
- Potential legal action for fraud

---

## 11. Roles and Responsibilities

### 11.1 Employees

- Understand and follow this policy
- Submit accurate and timely expense reports
- Retain all receipts
- Obtain required pre-approvals
- Respond promptly to auditor inquiries

### 11.2 Managers

- Review and approve reports timely (within 5 business days)
- Ensure expenses are appropriate and properly documented
- Question unusual or excessive expenses
- Approve or deny within spending authority

### 11.3 Finance/Accounts Payable

- Process approved reports timely
- Conduct audits
- Report policy violations
- Provide training and support
- Maintain policy compliance
