## 1. Policy Statement

Acme Corporation provides paid time off (PTO) to support work-life balance and employee well-being. PTO may be used for vacation, personal time, illness, or any other purpose. This policy describes PTO eligibility, accrual, usage, and administration.

---

## 2. Eligibility

### Full-Time Employees
Employees regularly scheduled to work 40 hours per week are eligible for full PTO accrual.

### Part-Time Employees
Employees regularly scheduled to work 20-39 hours per week accrue PTO on a prorated basis.

### Temporary and Contract Employees
Not eligible for PTO unless required by state law.

### Accrual Start Date
PTO begins accruing on your first day of employment. Accrued PTO is available for use after completing 90 days of employment (except for illness, which is available immediately).

---

## 3. PTO Accrual

### Accrual Schedule

| Years of Service | Annual PTO (Full-Time) | Accrual Per Pay Period | Maximum Balance |
|-----------------|------------------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| 0-2 years | 15 days (120 hours) | 4.62 hours | 160 hours |
| 3-5 years | 20 days (160 hours) | 6.15 hours | 200 hours |
| 6-10 years | 25 days (200 hours) | 7.69 hours | 240 hours |
| 11+ years | 30 days (240 hours) | 9.23 hours | 280 hours |

### How Accrual Works
- PTO accrues each pay period (bi-weekly)
- Accrual is based on hours paid (regular hours, PTO, holidays)
- Unpaid leave does not accrue PTO
- New tier begins on the anniversary of hire date

### Accrual Cap
Once you reach your maximum balance, accrual stops until you use PTO. Plan your time off to avoid losing accrual. The company does not pay for lost accrual due to reaching the cap.

### Service Date for Accrual
Your service date is your original hire date. If you were rehired:
- Within 12 months: Prior service counts
- After 12 months: Service date resets (subject to state law)

---

## 4. Using PTO

### Approved Uses
PTO may be used for any purpose, including:
- Vacation and travel
- Personal errands and appointments
- Mental health days
- Family events
- Illness (self or family member)
- Medical appointments
- Religious observances
- Inclement weather (when office is open)

### Minimum Increment
PTO may be taken in increments of:
- Non-exempt employees: 15 minutes
- Exempt employees: Half day (4 hours)

### Scheduling PTO

**Planned Time Off:**
- Request through Workday
- Submit requests as far in advance as possible
- Guidelines:
  - 1-2 days: 1 week notice
  - 3-5 days: 2 weeks notice
  - 1+ week: 30 days notice

**Unplanned Time Off (Illness/Emergency):**
- Notify your supervisor before your scheduled start time
- Enter in Workday upon return
- Manager may request documentation for absences exceeding 3 days

### Manager Approval
- Managers approve based on business needs
- Approval is not guaranteed for any request
- Popular vacation times may have limitations
- First-come, first-served for same-date requests
- Seniority may be considered for peak periods

### Blackout Periods
Some departments have blackout periods when PTO is restricted:
- Finance: Month-end, quarter-end, year-end close
- Sales: End of quarter
- Retail: Holiday shopping season
- IT: Major system implementations

Your manager will communicate any department-specific blackout periods.

---

## 5. PTO and Holidays

### Holidays Falling During PTO
If a company holiday occurs during your scheduled PTO, you receive holiday pay instead of using PTO.

### Day Before/After Holidays
Request for days adjacent to holidays should be made early, as these are popular times. Normal approval process applies.

---

## 6. PTO and Leaves of Absence

### FMLA and Medical Leave
- PTO may run concurrently with FMLA
- Employees may be required to use accrued PTO during FMLA (varies by state)
- See Leave Policies for details

### Workers' Compensation
PTO cannot be used while receiving workers' compensation benefits for the same time period.

### Short-Term Disability
- Waiting period: May use PTO during 7-day waiting period
- Benefit period: Cannot use PTO while receiving STD benefits

### Military Leave
PTO use is optional during military leave.

---

## 7. Carryover and Year-End

### Carryover Policy
- **Maximum Carryover:** 40 hours to the following year
- **Use-It-Or-Lose-It:** Hours above 40 are forfeited on January 1
- **No Payout for Forfeited PTO**

### Planning for Year-End
- Review your balance in November
- Schedule remaining PTO before year-end
- Unused hours above 40 are lost on January 1

### Exceptions
In limited circumstances, additional carryover may be approved if:
- Business needs prevented you from taking scheduled PTO
- Request must be made to HR by December 1
- Maximum exception: Additional 40 hours
- Must be used by March 31

---

## 8. PTO Balance Management

### Checking Your Balance
- **Workday:** View current balance and future accruals
- **Pay Stub:** Shows current balance
- **HR Portal:** PTO calculator

### Negative PTO Balance
Employees may not take PTO in excess of their balance, except:
- With manager and HR approval
- Maximum advance: 40 hours
- Must repay through future accrual
- If employment ends with negative balance, amount deducted from final pay (where legal)

---

## 9. PTO and Termination

### Voluntary Resignation
- Provide standard notice (2 weeks minimum)
- PTO cannot be used during notice period without approval
- Accrued, unused PTO is paid out in final paycheck (per state law)

### Involuntary Termination
- Accrued, unused PTO is paid out per state law
- Texas: Payout per company policy (paid out)
- California: Always paid out
- Other states: Varies

### PTO Payout Calculation
Payout = Accrued unused hours ร Current hourly rate

For salaried employees: Hourly rate = Annual salary รท 2,080

### No PTO Payout for Forfeited Hours
Hours forfeited at year-end are not paid out upon termination.

---

## 10. State-Specific Requirements

### California
- No maximum accrual cap (use accrual cap as guideline only)
- All accrued PTO paid out at termination
- No use-it-or-lose-it

### Colorado
- Up to 48 hours of accrued PTO may be used for sick purposes
- All accrued PTO paid out at termination

### Other States
Various states have specific requirements. HR ensures compliance with all applicable state laws.

---

## 11. Sick Leave (Where Required)

### States with Mandatory Sick Leave
In states requiring separate sick leave (e.g., California, New York, Arizona), employees may use PTO for sick purposes, and the company provides a separate sick leave balance as required by law.

### Sick Leave Accrual (Where Applicable)
- Accrual: 1 hour per 30 hours worked
- Maximum accrual: Per state law (typically 40-48 hours)
- Can use PTO after sick leave exhausted

### Documentation
For absences exceeding 3 consecutive days, medical documentation may be requested.

### Floating Holidays
- 2 floating holidays per year
- Use for religious observances, birthdays, or any personal occasion
- Request through Workday like regular PTO
- Must be used within the calendar year (no carryover)
- Prorated for mid-year hires

### Holiday Pay Eligibility
To receive holiday pay, employees must:
- Be regularly scheduled to work the holiday
- Work or be on approved PTO the scheduled day before and after the holiday
- Not be on unpaid leave

### Working on Holidays
If you are required to work on a holiday:
- Non-exempt: 1.5x pay for hours worked + holiday pay
- Exempt: Alternate day off within 30 days

## 13. Time Off for Part-Time Employees

### PTO Accrual (Part-Time)
Part-time employees (20-39 hours/week) accrue PTO prorated based on scheduled hours:

| Scheduled Hours | Accrual Multiplier |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| 32-39 hours/week | 80% |
| 24-31 hours/week | 60% |
| 20-23 hours/week | 50% |

### Holiday Pay (Part-Time)
Part-time employees receive holiday pay for their normally scheduled hours on holidays.

---

## 14. Time Off Without Pay

### When Unpaid Time May Be Approved
If PTO is exhausted, unpaid time off may be approved for:
- Extended illness (beyond FMLA/medical leave)
- Family emergencies
- Personal circumstances
- Manager and HR approval required

### Impact of Unpaid Time
- No PTO accrual during unpaid time
- Benefits may be affected if extended
- May impact performance reviews and bonuses

## 15. Administration

### Responsibilities

**Employees:**
- Request PTO in advance
- Maintain awareness of balance
- Enter time off in Workday
- Notify manager of unplanned absences

**Managers:**
- Review and approve/deny requests timely
- Ensure adequate coverage
- Address patterns of concern
- Apply policy consistently

**Human Resources:**
- Administer PTO policy
- Ensure legal compliance
- Address questions and disputes
- Maintain records

### Records Retention
PTO records are retained for 4 years after the year in which they were earned.
