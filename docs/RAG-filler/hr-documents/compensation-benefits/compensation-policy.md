## 1. Policy Statement

Acme Corporation is committed to providing competitive, equitable, and performance-based compensation to attract, retain, and motivate talented employees. Our compensation philosophy is designed to reward performance, support career growth, and ensure internal equity while remaining competitive in the market.

---

## 2. Compensation Philosophy

### 2.1 Guiding Principles

**Market Competitive:** We target the 50th to 75th percentile of relevant labor markets, adjusting for role criticality and talent scarcity.

**Performance-Based:** Compensation recognizes and rewards individual and organizational performance.

**Internally Equitable:** We maintain fair pay relationships between roles based on job value, skills, and responsibilities.

**Legally Compliant:** We comply with all federal, state, and local compensation laws.

**Transparent:** We communicate our compensation philosophy and practices openly.

### 2.2 Total Rewards Approach

Compensation at Acme includes:
- Base salary
- Annual bonus/incentive compensation
- Equity awards (for eligible employees)
- Benefits (health, retirement, time off)
- Career development opportunities
- Recognition programs

## 4. Base Salary

### 4.1 Determining Base Salary

Base salary is determined by:
- Job level and pay grade
- Market data for the role
- Individual qualifications and experience
- Internal equity with similar roles
- Geographic location

### 4.2 Pay Ranges for 2025

Sample pay ranges by level (U.S. nationwide):

| Level | Minimum | Midpoint | Maximum |
|-------|---------|----------|---------|
| IC1 | $52,000 | $65,000 | $78,000 |
| IC2 | $64,000 | $80,000 | $96,000 |
| IC3 | $80,000 | $100,000 | $120,000 |
| IC4 | $104,000 | $130,000 | $156,000 |
| IC5 | $136,000 | $170,000 | $204,000 |
| M1 | $96,000 | $120,000 | $144,000 |

*Engineering and technical roles may have higher ranges. Ranges vary by location.*

### 4.3 Geographic Differentials

Pay is adjusted for cost of labor in different markets:

| Location Tier | Adjustment |
|--------------|------------|
| Tier 1 (SF, NYC, Seattle) | +15% to +25% |
| Tier 2 (Austin, Boston, Denver) | Base ranges |
| Tier 3 (Other metro areas) | -5% to -10% |
| Tier 4 (Lower cost areas) | -10% to -15% |

### 4.4 New Hire Salary

New hires are typically placed between minimum and midpoint based on:
- Relevant experience
- Education and certifications
- Current compensation
- Market conditions
- Internal equity

Offers above midpoint require HR and executive approval.

## 5. Salary Reviews

### 5.1 Annual Merit Increases

Merit increases are awarded based on performance:

| Performance Rating | Typical Increase Range |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| Exceptional | 5% - 8% |
| Exceeds Expectations | 3% - 5% |
| Meets Expectations | 2% - 3% |
| Needs Improvement | 0% |
| Unsatisfactory | 0% |

Actual increases depend on:
- Individual performance rating
- Position in pay range
- Budget availability
- Market conditions

### 5.2 Timing

- Annual reviews occur in January
- Increases are effective March 1
- Employees must be employed before October 1 to be eligible

### 5.3 Promotions

Promotions typically include:
- 8% - 15% salary increase (varies by level change)
- Movement to new pay grade
- May occur at any time based on business need

### 5.4 Market Adjustments

Market adjustments may be made:
- When market data shows significant pay gaps
- To address retention risks
- To correct internal equity issues

Market adjustments are separate from merit increases.

## 6. Variable Compensation

### 6.1 Annual Bonus Program

Non-sales employees at IC3 and above are eligible for annual bonuses:

| Level | Target Bonus (% of salary) |
|-------|---------------------------|
| IC3 | 5% |
| IC4 | 8% |
| IC5 | 12% |
| M1 | 10% |
| M2 | 15% |
| M3 | 20% |
| M4 | 25% |
| E1 | 35% |
| E2 | 50% |

Bonus payout depends on:
- Company performance (50% weight)
- Department/team performance (25% weight)
- Individual performance (25% weight)

Bonuses are paid in March for the prior year.

### 6.2 Sales Compensation

Sales roles have separate commission plans:
- Account Executives: Base + uncapped commission (50/50 target mix)
- Sales Engineers: Base + commission (70/30 target mix)
- Sales Managers: Base + team commission + bonus

Details in individual Sales Compensation Plans.

### 6.3 Spot Bonuses

Spot bonuses recognize exceptional contributions:
- Range: $500 - $5,000
- Manager approval required
- HR approval for amounts over $2,500
- Limited to significant achievements beyond normal job duties

## 7. Equity Compensation

### 7.1 Stock Option Program

Eligible employees may receive stock options:

**Eligibility:** IC4+ and M2+ levels

**Initial Grants:**
| Level | Typical Grant (options) |
|-------|------------------------|
| IC4 | 2,000 - 4,000 |
| IC5 | 5,000 - 10,000 |
| M2 | 3,000 - 6,000 |
| M3 | 8,000 - 15,000 |
| M4 | 15,000 - 30,000 |
| E1+ | Per individual negotiation |

**Vesting:** 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff (25% at year 1, then monthly)

### 7.2 Refresh Grants

Annual refresh grants for strong performers:
- Reviewed during annual compensation cycle
- Based on performance and market competitiveness
- Typically 25% - 50% of initial grant level

### 7.3 RSU Program

In addition to options, some roles receive Restricted Stock Units (RSUs):
- Typically for senior hires
- Same vesting schedule as options
- Subject to applicable tax treatment

## 8. Pay Equity

### 8.1 Commitment

Acme Corporation is committed to pay equity regardless of:
- Gender
- Race or ethnicity
- Age
- Disability
- Any protected characteristic

### 8.2 Analysis

We conduct annual pay equity analyses to:
- Identify unexplained pay gaps
- Understand root causes
- Develop remediation plans
- Track progress over time

### 8.3 Remediation

When pay gaps are identified:
- Adjustments made outside merit cycle
- Funded from separate budget
- Documented and tracked

### 8.4 Transparency

We comply with all pay transparency laws:
- Salary ranges included in job postings (where required)
- Pay ranges available to current employees
- No retaliation for discussing pay

## 9. Overtime and FLSA Compliance

### 9.1 Exempt vs. Non-Exempt

**Exempt Employees:**
- Salaried, not eligible for overtime
- Must meet FLSA duties and salary tests
- Typically IC3+ and all management levels

**Non-Exempt Employees:**
- Hourly or salaried non-exempt
- Eligible for overtime at 1.5x regular rate
- Must accurately track all time worked

### 9.2 Overtime Pay

Non-exempt employees:
- Paid overtime for hours over 40/week
- Must obtain advance approval for overtime
- Not permitted to work off-the-clock

### 9.3 State Requirements

Some states have additional requirements:
- California: Daily overtime after 8 hours
- Other states: Various requirements

HR ensures compliance with all applicable laws.

## 12. Special Situations

### 12.1 Acting/Interim Roles

Employees temporarily in higher-level roles:
- May receive temporary pay increase (typically 5-10%)
- Increase effective while in acting role
- Returns to original pay when assignment ends

### 12.2 Demotions

Voluntary or involuntary demotions:
- Salary adjusted to appropriate level in new pay grade
- Typically reduced 10-15%
- May be placed at current salary if within new range

### 12.3 Transfers

Lateral transfers:
- Generally no salary change
- Geographic differential may apply if location changes

### 12.4 Rehires

Former employees rehired:
- Salary based on current market and qualifications
- Previous salary not guaranteed
- Prior service may be considered for benefit eligibility
