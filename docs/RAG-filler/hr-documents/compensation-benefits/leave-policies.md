## Overview

Acme Corporation provides various leave programs to support employees during important life events, medical needs, and family responsibilities. This document outlines all leave types, eligibility requirements, and procedures.

## 1. Family and Medical Leave (FMLA)

### 1.1 Eligibility

To qualify for FMLA leave, employees must:
- Have worked for Acme for at least 12 months
- Have worked at least 1,250 hours in the 12 months before leave
- Work at a location with 50+ employees within 75 miles

### 1.2 Amount of Leave

Eligible employees receive up to 12 workweeks of unpaid, job-protected leave per 12-month period.

**Military Caregiver Leave:** Up to 26 workweeks to care for a covered servicemember.

### 1.3 Qualifying Reasons

**Employee's Own Health:**
- Serious health condition that prevents performing job functions

**Family Care:**
- Care for spouse, child, or parent with serious health condition
- Birth and bonding with newborn
- Placement of child for adoption or foster care

**Military Family:**
- Qualifying exigency related to military deployment
- Care for covered servicemember with serious injury or illness

### 1.4 Definition of Serious Health Condition

A serious health condition includes:
- Inpatient care (overnight hospital stay)
- Continuing treatment by a healthcare provider
- Chronic conditions requiring periodic treatment
- Permanent or long-term conditions
- Conditions requiring multiple treatments

### 1.5 Intermittent Leave

FMLA leave may be taken:
- Continuously (full weeks at a time)
- Intermittently (blocks of time as needed)
- Reduced schedule (fewer hours per day or week)

Intermittent leave must be medically necessary. Employees must make reasonable efforts to schedule treatment to minimize workplace disruption.

### 1.6 Notice Requirements

**Foreseeable Leave:**
- 30 days advance notice when possible
- As soon as practicable if 30 days not possible

**Unforeseeable Leave:**
- As soon as practicable (usually same day or next business day)

### 1.7 Certification

Employees must provide medical certification within 15 calendar days. Acme may:
- Request recertification every 30 days (or longer based on condition)
- Request second or third opinions at company expense
- Request fitness-for-duty certification before return

### 1.8 Pay During FMLA

FMLA leave is unpaid, but employees:
- Must use accrued PTO concurrently (company policy)
- May use short-term disability for qualifying conditions
- May receive state-paid family leave benefits (where available)

### 1.9 Benefits During FMLA

- Health insurance continues on same terms
- Employee must continue paying their share of premiums
- If payment not made within 30 days, coverage may be cancelled

### 1.10 Return from FMLA

Employees returning from FMLA are entitled to:
- Same position or equivalent position
- Same pay, benefits, and terms of employment

## 2. Parental Leave

### 2.1 Eligibility

All full-time employees who become parents through:
- Birth of a child
- Adoption of a child
- Foster placement of a child

No minimum tenure requirement.

### 2.2 Amount of Leave

**Paid Parental Leave:** 12 weeks at 100% pay

### 2.3 Timing

Leave must be taken:
- Within 12 months of birth, adoption, or placement
- In one continuous block (intermittent use not permitted)
- Concurrently with FMLA where applicable

### 2.4 Multiple Births or Placements

The 12-week benefit is per event, not per child. Twins, triplets, or siblings adopted together = one 12-week leave.

### 2.5 Both Parents at Acme

If both parents work at Acme, each is entitled to 12 weeks of paid parental leave. Leave may be taken at the same time or different times.

### 2.6 How to Request

1. Notify manager and HR as soon as pregnancy or adoption is known
2. Submit parental leave request in Workday at least 30 days before leave
3. Provide documentation (birth certificate, adoption papers)

### 2.7 Return from Parental Leave

Employees are guaranteed return to same or equivalent position. Notify HR at least 2 weeks before planned return.

## 3. Bereavement Leave

### 3.1 Immediate Family

**5 paid days** for death of:
- Spouse or domestic partner
- Child (including stepchild, foster child)
- Parent (including stepparent, in-law)
- Sibling (including step-sibling)

### 3.2 Extended Family

**3 paid days** for death of:
- Grandparent
- Grandchild
- Son-in-law or daughter-in-law
- Brother-in-law or sister-in-law

### 3.3 Other Relationships

**1 paid day** for death of:
- Close friend
- Colleague
- Other significant relationship (at manager discretion)

### 3.4 Additional Time

If additional time is needed:
- Use PTO for additional days
- Unpaid time may be approved
- FMLA may apply in some circumstances

### 3.5 Travel Time

If travel is required, additional unpaid or PTO time may be approved.

### 3.6 Documentation

Documentation is not typically required but may be requested for extended leave.

### 3.7 How to Request

1. Notify your manager as soon as possible
2. Enter in Workday upon return
3. Include relationship and dates

## 4. Jury Duty Leave

### 4.1 Paid Leave

Employees receive full pay for up to 10 days of jury service per calendar year.

### 4.2 Extended Jury Service

For jury service exceeding 10 days:
- Use PTO for additional days
- Or take unpaid leave
- Extended leave may qualify for unpaid leave of absence

### 4.3 Employee Responsibilities

- Provide copy of jury summons to manager and HR
- Report to work when not needed for jury duty
- Keep manager informed of expected duration
- Provide documentation of service if requested

### 4.4 Jury Compensation

Employees may keep any compensation received from the court.

## 5. Military Leave

### 5.1 USERRA Rights

Acme Corporation complies with the Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act (USERRA).

### 5.2 Paid Military Leave

Employees receive up to 2 weeks (10 working days) of paid leave per calendar year for:
- Annual training
- Weekend drills (beyond normal weekend)
- Other military obligations

### 5.3 Unpaid Military Leave

Extended active duty:
- Unpaid leave for duration of service (up to 5 years cumulative)
- Job protection upon return
- Benefits continuation options available

### 5.4 Pay Differential

Employees called to active duty for more than 30 days may receive pay differential:
- Difference between military pay and Acme pay
- For up to 12 months

### 5.5 Reemployment Rights

Returning service members are entitled to:
- Reemployment in same or comparable position
- Restoration of seniority, pay, and benefits
- Must apply for reemployment within required timeframe:
  - Service less than 31 days: Next work day
  - Service 31-180 days: Within 14 days
  - Service 181+ days: Within 90 days

### 5.6 Benefits During Military Leave

- Health insurance: COBRA or 24-month continuation
- 401(k): Catch-up contributions allowed upon return
- PTO: Continues to accrue during paid leave portion

## 7. Domestic Violence Leave

### 7.1 Eligibility

Leave available for employees who are victims of:
- Domestic violence
- Sexual assault
- Stalking

Also available for employees whose family members are victims.

### 7.2 Approved Purposes

Leave may be used for:
- Medical attention
- Counseling
- Legal proceedings
- Safety planning
- Relocation

### 7.3 Amount of Leave

Per applicable state law. Where no state law exists:
- Up to 5 days unpaid leave per year
- PTO may be used

### 7.4 Confidentiality

All information regarding domestic violence leave is strictly confidential.

## 8. Personal Leave of Absence

### 8.1 Eligibility

Full-time employees with 1+ year of service may request personal leave for:
- Extended travel
- Educational pursuits
- Personal matters not covered by other leave types
- Family care beyond FMLA

### 8.2 Duration

- Minimum: 2 weeks
- Maximum: 12 weeks
- Extension may be granted in exceptional circumstances

### 8.3 Approval

Personal leave requires approval from:
- Manager
- HR
- Department VP (for leave over 4 weeks)

Approval is at the company's discretion based on business needs.

### 8.4 Pay and Benefits

Personal leave is unpaid. During leave:
- PTO does not accrue
- Benefits may be continued at employee's expense
- Service credit pauses (for tenure calculations)

### 8.5 Return from Leave

Position is not guaranteed for personal leave. However, the company will make reasonable efforts to return you to the same or similar position.

## 9. Medical Leave of Absence (Non-FMLA)

### 9.1 Eligibility

For employees not eligible for FMLA or who have exhausted FMLA leave.

### 9.2 Duration

Up to 12 weeks per rolling 12-month period (in addition to any FMLA leave used).

### 9.3 Requirements

- Medical certification required
- Must provide updates every 30 days
- Fitness-for-duty certification required before return

### 9.4 Coordination with Disability

Medical leave may run concurrently with:
- Short-term disability
- Long-term disability
- Workers' compensation

### 9.5 Benefits

- Health insurance may continue at employee cost
- Other benefits per plan terms

## 10. Requesting Leave

### 10.1 General Process

1. **Notify your manager** - Verbal or written notice
2. **Contact HR** - For leaves over 3 days
3. **Submit in Workday** - Enter leave request
4. **Provide documentation** - As required by leave type
5. **Receive determination** - HR provides written response

### 10.2 Documentation Requirements

| Leave Type | Documentation Required |
|------------|----------------------|
| FMLA | Medical certification form |
| Parental | Birth certificate, adoption papers |
| Bereavement | Usually not required |
| Jury Duty | Copy of summons |
| Military | Copy of orders |
| Medical (Non-FMLA) | Medical certification |
| Personal | Written request explaining reason |

## 11. Return to Work

### 11.1 Notification

Notify HR and your manager at least 2 business days before returning from leave.

### 11.2 Fitness-for-Duty Certification

Required for return from:
- FMLA leave for employee's own serious health condition
- Medical leave of absence
- Workers' compensation leave

### 11.3 Reasonable Accommodations

If you need accommodations upon return, contact HR to discuss options. See ADA Accommodation Policy.

### 11.4 Failure to Return

Employees who fail to return from leave:
- Must contact HR immediately
- May be subject to termination
- May be required to reimburse health insurance premiums paid during leave

## 12. Protection from Retaliation

Acme Corporation prohibits retaliation against employees who:
- Request or take approved leave
- File complaints about leave rights
- Testify in leave-related proceedings

Report any retaliation to HR or the Ethics Hotline.
