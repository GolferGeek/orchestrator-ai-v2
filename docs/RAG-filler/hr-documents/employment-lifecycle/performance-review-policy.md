# Acme Corporation Performance Review Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024
**Last Revised:** November 22, 2025
**Version:** 2.3
**Document Owner:** Human Resources Department
**Approved By:** Sarah Mitchell, Chief Human Resources Officer

---

## 1. Purpose

Acme Corporation's performance management process is designed to:
- Set clear expectations and goals
- Provide regular feedback
- Support employee development
- Recognize and reward strong performance
- Address performance issues
- Inform compensation decisions

---

## 2. Philosophy

We believe in:
- **Continuous Feedback:** Regular conversations, not just annual reviews
- **Goal Alignment:** Individual goals connected to company objectives
- **Development Focus:** Growth opportunities for all employees
- **Fair Evaluation:** Consistent, objective assessment criteria
- **Two-Way Dialogue:** Input from both managers and employees

---

## 4. Goal Setting

### SMART Goals

All goals should be:
- **S**pecific: Clear, well-defined objectives
- **M**easurable: Quantifiable success criteria
- **A**chievable: Realistic given resources and timeframe
- **R**elevant: Aligned with team and company objectives
- **T**ime-bound: Clear deadline or timeframe

### Goal Categories

Employees typically set 4-6 goals across categories:

| Category | Description | Weight |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| Business/Results | Key deliverables and outcomes | 60-70% |
| Development | Skills, knowledge, career growth | 15-20% |
| Culture/Values | Behaviors, collaboration, citizenship | 15-20% |

### Goal Setting Process

1. **Company Goals Announced:** January (by CEO)
2. **Department Goals Set:** February (by department leaders)
3. **Individual Goals Created:** March (employee drafts)
4. **Manager Review:** March (alignment discussion)
5. **Goals Finalized:** April 1 deadline in Workday

### Goal Updates

Goals can be updated throughout the year when:
- Business priorities change significantly
- Role responsibilities change
- Goals are achieved early
- Goals become unachievable due to external factors

Discuss with your manager and document changes in Workday.

## 5. Continuous Feedback

### Weekly/Bi-Weekly 1:1s

Managers and employees should meet regularly (minimum bi-weekly) to discuss:
- Progress on goals
- Priorities and blockers
- Feedback (both ways)
- Development and career
- Support needed

### Informal Feedback

Beyond scheduled 1:1s:
- Provide feedback close to the event
- Be specific and actionable
- Balance positive and constructive
- Focus on behaviors and impact
- Document significant feedback

### 360 Feedback (Optional)

Some roles may include 360 feedback:
- Collected from peers, direct reports, stakeholders
- Used for development purposes
- Not used as primary rating input

---

## 6. Mid-Year Check-In

### Purpose

The mid-year check-in (July) provides a structured opportunity to:
- Review progress against goals
- Discuss challenges and wins
- Adjust goals if needed
- Identify support requirements
- Preview year-end expectations

### Process

1. **Employee Preparation:** Update goal progress in Workday
2. **Manager Review:** Review progress and prepare discussion points
3. **Check-In Meeting:** 45-60 minute conversation
4. **Documentation:** Manager documents key discussion points

### No Rating

The mid-year is a progress check, not a rating. No formal performance rating is given.

## 7. Annual Performance Review

### Self-Assessment

**Timeline:** Opens October 15, due November 1

**Content:**
- Progress on each goal with evidence
- Key accomplishments
- Challenges and how you addressed them
- Examples of demonstrating company values
- Development progress
- Summary self-rating (optional)

**Tips:**
- Start gathering accomplishments throughout the year
- Use specific examples and data
- Be honest about challenges
- Highlight impact and results

### Manager Assessment

**Timeline:** November 1-30

**Content:**
- Assessment of each goal
- Overall performance summary
- Strengths and accomplishments
- Areas for improvement
- Demonstration of company values
- Proposed performance rating

### Peer Feedback (Where Applicable)

- Managers may request feedback from key collaborators
- Used to inform assessment
- Confidential to the extent possible

## 8. Performance Ratings

### Rating Scale

| Rating | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Exceptional** | Significantly exceeds expectations across all goals; demonstrates outstanding contribution; role model for others |
| **Exceeds Expectations** | Consistently exceeds expectations on most goals; strong performance with notable accomplishments |
| **Meets Expectations** | Achieves most goals; solid, reliable performance; meets the requirements of the role |
| **Needs Improvement** | Falls short on key goals; performance gaps require attention; improvement plan may be needed |
| **Unsatisfactory** | Does not meet minimum expectations; significant performance issues; immediate improvement required |

### Rating Distribution Guidelines

To ensure differentiation and fairness, we target:

| Rating | Target Distribution |
|--------|-------------------|
| Exceptional | 5-10% |
| Exceeds Expectations | 20-25% |
| Meets Expectations | 55-65% |
| Needs Improvement | 5-10% |
| Unsatisfactory | 0-2% |

These are guidelines, not quotas. Actual distribution should reflect true performance.

### Rating Criteria

Ratings consider:
- Goal achievement
- Quality of work
- Behaviors and how results were achieved
- Collaboration and teamwork
- Demonstration of company values
- Contribution to team and company success

## 9. Calibration

### Purpose

Calibration ensures consistent, fair ratings across the organization by:
- Comparing performance across similar roles
- Identifying and correcting bias
- Ensuring rating consistency
- Discussing borderline cases
- Validating rating distribution

### Process

**Level 1 (Department):**
- Managers present their ratings
- Leaders discuss and compare
- Ratings adjusted if needed

**Level 2 (Division/Function):**
- Department ratings rolled up
- Cross-department comparison
- Senior leader review

**Level 3 (Company):**
- Executive review
- Final distribution approval
- Exception approvals

### Calibration Participants

| Level | Participants |
|-------|--------------|
| Manager | Proposes initial rating |
| Director | Reviews and calibrates across managers |
| VP | Reviews and calibrates across directors |
| CHRO | Reviews overall distribution and exceptions |

## 10. Performance Conversations

### Scheduling

Review conversations should occur in January, after calibration.

### Discussion Guide

**Opening (5 minutes):**
- Set positive tone
- Explain purpose and process

**Review Discussion (30-40 minutes):**
- Review accomplishments and strengths
- Discuss areas for improvement
- Share final rating and rationale
- Discuss compensation impact (if applicable)

**Development (10-15 minutes):**
- Career aspirations
- Development goals
- Support and resources needed

**Next Steps (5 minutes):**
- Summarize key points
- Agree on follow-up actions
- Set expectations for coming year

### Tips for Managers

- Be prepared and organized
- Start with positives
- Be specific with examples
- Listen to employee perspective
- Focus on behaviors, not personality
- Discuss development opportunities
- End on a constructive note

### Tips for Employees

- Come prepared with your view
- Listen openly to feedback
- Ask clarifying questions
- Share your perspective respectfully
- Focus on actionable improvements
- Discuss your career goals

## 11. Performance and Compensation

### Merit Increases

Annual merit increases are based on performance rating:

| Rating | Typical Increase Range |
|--------|----------------------|
| Exceptional | 5% - 8% |
| Exceeds Expectations | 3% - 5% |
| Meets Expectations | 2% - 3% |
| Needs Improvement | 0% |
| Unsatisfactory | 0% |

Actual increases depend on budget, position in range, and market factors.

### Annual Bonus

Bonus multipliers by rating:

| Rating | Individual Multiplier |
|--------|---------------------|
| Exceptional | 125% - 150% |
| Exceeds Expectations | 100% - 125% |
| Meets Expectations | 75% - 100% |
| Needs Improvement | 0% - 50% |
| Unsatisfactory | 0% |

### Equity Refresh

Refresh grants consider:
- Performance rating
- Equity position relative to peers
- Retention risk
- Strategic importance of role

## 12. Managing Performance Issues

### When Performance Falls Short

**Informal Coaching:**
- Regular feedback and guidance
- Document conversations
- Set clear expectations
- Provide support and resources

**Formal Warning:**
- Written documentation of concerns
- Specific improvement requirements
- Timeline for improvement
- Consequences of not improving

**Performance Improvement Plan (PIP):**
- Formal written plan
- Specific, measurable goals
- 30-60-90 day timeline typically
- Regular check-ins
- HR involvement
- Possible outcomes: successful completion or termination

### PIP Components

1. **Performance Concerns:** Specific issues with examples
2. **Expectations:** Clear standards to be met
3. **Goals:** Measurable objectives
4. **Support:** Resources and assistance provided
5. **Timeline:** Duration and checkpoints
6. **Consequences:** What happens if goals aren't met

## 13. Promotions

### Criteria

Promotions are based on:
- Sustained high performance (typically 2+ ratings of Exceeds or higher)
- Demonstration of next-level competencies
- Business need for the role
- Manager recommendation
- Leadership approval

### Process

1. Manager nominates employee
2. Documentation of readiness
3. Review by skip-level and HR
4. Calibration with peers
5. Final approval
6. Communication to employee

### Promotion Cycles

Primary promotion cycles are:
- Q1 (with annual review)
- Q3 (mid-year for exceptional cases)

Off-cycle promotions require VP and HR approval.
