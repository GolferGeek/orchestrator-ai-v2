## Before Your First Day

### What to Expect

About one week before your start date, you'll receive:
- **Welcome Email** from your manager
- **Onboarding Portal Access** - Complete pre-hire paperwork
- **IT Setup Form** - Equipment and access preferences
- **Benefits Enrollment Information**

### Pre-Hire Paperwork

Complete these documents in the onboarding portal:
- [ ] Personal information form
- [ ] Tax forms (W-4, state forms)
- [ ] Direct deposit authorization
- [ ] Emergency contact information
- [ ] Background check authorization (if not already completed)
- [ ] I-9 Employment Eligibility Verification (Section 1)

### What to Bring on Day 1

- Government-issued photo ID (for I-9 verification)
- Social Security card or other I-9 acceptable document
- Voided check or bank information (for direct deposit)
- Any questions you have!

## Your First 30 Days

### Goals

By the end of your first month, you should:

**Understand the Company:**
- Know Acme's products and services
- Understand the competitive landscape
- Know the organizational structure
- Understand company goals and strategy

**Know Your Role:**
- Understand your job responsibilities
- Know how your role contributes to the team and company
- Have clear goals for your first 90 days
- Understand how your performance is measured

**Build Relationships:**
- Know your team members and their roles
- Identify key stakeholders
- Have regular check-ins with your manager
- Start building your network

**Be Productive:**
- Complete all required training
- Contribute to team projects
- Ask questions and seek feedback
- Document learnings

### 30-Day Checklist

- [ ] Complete all required training
- [ ] Enroll in benefits
- [ ] Attend team meetings
- [ ] Have 1:1s with manager (weekly)
- [ ] Meet with key stakeholders
- [ ] Understand team goals and projects
- [ ] Set up development environment (technical roles)
- [ ] Complete 30-day check-in with HR
- [ ] Review and understand performance expectations

## Your First 90 Days

### 60-Day Milestones

- Contributing independently to projects
- Understanding workflows and processes
- Building relationships across teams
- Receiving and incorporating feedback
- Identifying areas for improvement or growth

### 90-Day Milestones

- Fully ramped in your role
- Making meaningful contributions
- Developing expertise in key areas
- Established working relationships
- Clear development goals set with manager
- Completed probationary period

### Performance Check-Ins

| Timing | Meeting | Purpose |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Week 1 | Daily check-in with manager | Answer questions, provide support |
| Week 2-4 | Weekly 1:1 with manager | Progress, feedback, questions |
| Day 30 | 30-day review | Formal check-in, address concerns |
| Day 60 | 60-day review | Mid-probation review |
| Day 90 | 90-day review | End of probation, goal setting |

### What Success Looks Like at 90 Days

**You're performing well if you:**
- Complete assigned tasks with minimal guidance
- Ask thoughtful questions
- Take initiative appropriately
- Build positive relationships
- Receive positive feedback
- Understand how your work fits the bigger picture
- Meet or exceed initial goals

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: When do I get my first paycheck?**
A: You'll be paid on the first regular pay date at least one week after your start date. Pay dates are every other Friday.

**Q: When can I enroll in benefits?**
A: You have 30 days from your start date to enroll. Benefits are effective the first of the month following 30 days of employment.

**Q: How do I request time off?**
A: Through Workday. You can use PTO after 90 days (except for illness, which is available immediately).

**Q: Who do I contact if I have questions?**
A: Start with your manager or buddy. For HR questions, contact onboarding@acmecorp.com.

**Q: What if my equipment doesn't work?**
A: Contact IT Help Desk at helpdesk@acmecorp.com or ext. 5000.

**Q: When is my 90-day review?**
A: Your manager will schedule it around your 90-day anniversary.

**Q: Can I work from home?**
A: Depends on your role and manager approval. See Remote Work Policy.
