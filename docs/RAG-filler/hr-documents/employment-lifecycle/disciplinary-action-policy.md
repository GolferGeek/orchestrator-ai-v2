# Acme Corporation Disciplinary Action Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2023
**Last Revised:** November 22, 2025
**Version:** 2.0
**Document Owner:** Human Resources Department
**Approved By:** Sarah Mitchell, Chief Human Resources Officer

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes a fair and consistent approach to addressing employee conduct and performance issues. Our goal is to correct problems, improve performance, and maintain a productive work environment while protecting the rights of all employees.

---

## 2. Policy Statement

Acme Corporation uses progressive discipline to address performance and conduct issues. However, the company reserves the right to skip steps or proceed directly to termination depending on the severity of the issue. Employment remains at-will.

---

## 3. Scope

This policy applies to all employees. It covers:
- Performance deficiencies
- Policy violations
- Misconduct
- Attendance issues
- Behavioral problems

Certain employees may be covered by additional guidelines (e.g., union employees, employees with contracts).

---

## 4. Grounds for Discipline

### 4.1 Performance Issues

- Failure to meet job standards
- Poor quality of work
- Missed deadlines
- Failure to follow instructions
- Inadequate job knowledge
- Lack of productivity

### 4.2 Attendance Issues

- Excessive absenteeism
- Chronic tardiness
- No call/no show
- Patterns of absence
- Leaving work without permission

### 4.3 Policy Violations

- Violation of company policies
- Safety violations
- Security breaches
- Confidentiality violations
- Harassment or discrimination
- Drug or alcohol violations

### 4.4 Misconduct

- Dishonesty or falsification
- Insubordination
- Theft or fraud
- Violence or threats
- Damage to property
- Inappropriate behavior
- Conflict of interest
- Misuse of company resources

## 5. Progressive Discipline

### 5.1 Overview

Progressive discipline typically follows these steps:

| Step | Action | Documentation |
|------|--------|---------------|
| 1 | Verbal Warning | Documented in manager's notes |
| 2 | Written Warning | Formal written notice |
| 3 | Final Written Warning | Formal notice, often with PIP |
| 4 | Termination | Separation |

### 5.2 Step 1: Verbal Warning

**When Used:**
- First occurrence of minor issues
- Initial performance concerns
- Early attendance problems

**Process:**
- Private conversation with employee
- Clear statement of the issue
- Expected improvement
- Consequences of continued issues
- Documentation in manager's files

**Duration:** Typically remains relevant for 6 months

### 5.3 Step 2: Written Warning

**When Used:**
- Repeated minor issues after verbal warning
- More significant first-time issues
- Continued performance concerns

**Process:**
- Meeting with employee (may include HR)
- Written documentation of issue
- Prior warnings referenced
- Specific expectations for improvement
- Timeline for improvement
- Consequences clearly stated
- Employee signs acknowledging receipt

**Duration:** Remains in file for 12 months

### 5.4 Step 3: Final Written Warning

**When Used:**
- Continued issues after written warning
- Serious first-time violations
- Pattern of problems

**Process:**
- Meeting with employee and HR
- Written notice of final warning
- May include Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)
- Clear statement that termination will follow if no improvement
- Specific, measurable expectations
- Short timeline (typically 30-60 days)

**Duration:** Remains in file permanently

### 5.5 Step 4: Termination

**When Used:**
- No improvement after final warning
- Gross misconduct (immediate termination)
- Severe policy violations
- Completion of PIP without success

**Process:**
- Review with HR and legal (as appropriate)
- Approval from appropriate leadership level
- Termination meeting with HR present
- Provision of final pay information
- Return of company property
- Exit logistics

## 6. Immediate Termination Offenses

Certain conduct may result in immediate termination without progressive discipline:

- **Violence** - Physical assault, fighting, threats of violence
- **Weapons** - Possession of weapons on company property
- **Theft** - Stealing company property, client property, or from colleagues
- **Fraud** - Falsification of records, expense fraud, time theft
- **Gross Insubordination** - Refusing direct lawful orders from management
- **Harassment** - Severe harassment, especially after prior warning
- **Discrimination** - Serious discriminatory acts
- **Drug/Alcohol** - Being under the influence at work, possession/sale of drugs
- **Confidentiality** - Major breach of confidentiality or data security
- **Safety** - Gross safety violations endangering others
- **Criminal Activity** - Conviction of job-related crime

## 7. Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

### 7.1 Purpose

A PIP is a formal plan to help employees improve performance before termination. It provides:
- Clear performance expectations
- Specific goals and timelines
- Support and resources
- Consequences if goals aren't met

### 7.2 When Used

- Sustained performance issues
- After written or final warning
- When improvement is possible with support
- For valued employees worth investing in

### 7.3 PIP Components

**Statement of Concerns:**
- Specific performance deficiencies
- Examples with dates
- Impact on team/company
- Prior coaching provided

**Performance Expectations:**
- Clear, measurable goals
- Specific standards to meet
- Resources and support provided
- Training or development offered

**Timeline:**
- Duration (typically 30, 60, or 90 days)
- Check-in schedule (weekly or bi-weekly)
- Evaluation dates

**Consequences:**
- Successful completion: Return to normal status
- Unsuccessful: Termination or demotion
- Extensions rarely granted

### 7.4 PIP Process

1. **Draft:** Manager drafts PIP with HR input
2. **Review:** HR and legal review (as needed)
3. **Meeting:** Present PIP to employee
4. **Acknowledge:** Employee signs PIP
5. **Execute:** Regular check-ins and coaching
6. **Evaluate:** Final assessment at end of period
7. **Conclude:** Success celebration or termination

### 7.5 Employee Rights During PIP

- Understand expectations and criteria
- Receive regular feedback
- Ask questions and seek clarification
- Request reasonable support
- Document their efforts and progress

### 7.6 PIP Statistics

Most PIPs result in either:
- Successful improvement (employee returns to good standing)
- Termination (goals not achieved)
- Resignation (employee chooses to leave)

## 8. Documentation

### 8.1 Importance

Documentation is critical for:
- Fair treatment of employees
- Consistent application of policy
- Legal protection
- Tracking patterns
- Supporting decisions

### 8.2 What to Document

- Date, time, location of incidents
- Specific facts (not opinions)
- Witnesses present
- Employee's explanation
- Prior related issues
- Action taken
- Follow-up expectations
- Employee acknowledgment

### 8.3 Where to Document

- Verbal warnings: Manager's notes, may share with HR
- Written warnings: HR file, Workday
- Final warnings and PIPs: HR file, Workday, legal review
- Terminations: Complete file maintained by HR

### 8.4 Retention

- Active employees: In personnel file
- Terminated employees: 7 years after termination
- Per records retention policy

## 9. Investigation Process

### 9.1 When Investigation Is Needed

- Serious misconduct allegations
- Harassment or discrimination claims
- Complaints involving multiple parties
- Potential legal exposure
- Criminal allegations

### 9.2 Investigation Steps

1. **Receive Complaint:** Document initial report
2. **Assess:** Determine if investigation needed
3. **Plan:** Identify scope, timeline, investigator
4. **Investigate:** Interview parties and witnesses
5. **Gather Evidence:** Documents, emails, records
6. **Analyze:** Assess credibility, reach conclusions
7. **Report:** Document findings and recommendations
8. **Action:** Take appropriate disciplinary action
9. **Follow-Up:** Monitor for retaliation

### 9.3 Confidentiality

Investigations are confidential to the extent possible. Information shared only with those who need to know.

### 9.4 Administrative Leave

During investigations:
- Employee may be placed on paid administrative leave
- Not a disciplinary action
- Protects investigation integrity
- Protects parties involved

## 10. Employee Rights

### 10.1 Due Process

Employees have the right to:
- Be informed of concerns
- Provide their perspective
- Have representation (union, if applicable)
- Review documentation
- Appeal decisions

### 10.2 Consistency

Discipline will be applied consistently regardless of:
- Position or tenure
- Protected characteristics
- Personal relationships

### 10.3 Confidentiality

Disciplinary matters are confidential. Information shared only as needed.

### 10.4 Non-Retaliation

Employees will not face retaliation for:
- Reporting concerns in good faith
- Participating in investigations
- Exercising their rights

---

## 11. Appeals Process

### 11.1 How to Appeal

Employees may appeal disciplinary decisions:

1. **Discuss with Manager:** First attempt resolution
2. **Escalate to HR:** Submit written appeal within 5 business days
3. **HR Review:** HR reviews with leadership
4. **Decision:** Response within 10 business days
5. **Final Appeal:** VP-level review if needed

### 11.2 Grounds for Appeal

- Factual errors
- Procedural violations
- Inconsistent treatment
- Mitigating circumstances not considered
- New evidence

### 11.3 Appeal Outcomes

- Discipline upheld
- Discipline modified
- Discipline removed

## 12. Roles and Responsibilities

### Employees

- Understand and follow policies
- Meet performance expectations
- Communicate concerns promptly
- Participate in improvement efforts
- Exercise appeal rights if needed

### Managers

- Address issues promptly
- Document thoroughly
- Apply discipline consistently
- Partner with HR
- Support improvement efforts
- Treat employees fairly

### Human Resources

- Advise on policy and process
- Review disciplinary actions
- Ensure consistency and fairness
- Conduct investigations as needed
- Maintain documentation
- Support appeals process

### Leadership

- Model expected behavior
- Support consistent enforcement
- Review serious cases
- Approve terminations

---

## 13. Special Situations

### 13.1 Probationary Employees

Employees in their first 90 days:
- May be terminated without progressive discipline
- Manager should still document concerns
- HR should be consulted

### 13.2 Multiple Issues

When an employee has multiple unrelated issues:
- Each issue tracked separately
- Cumulative effect considered
- Pattern may warrant escalated action

### 13.3 Former Issues

- Discipline older than 12 months typically not considered
- Patterns of behavior may be considered
- Serious issues (e.g., harassment) remain relevant longer

---

## 14. Legal Considerations

### 14.1 At-Will Employment

This policy does not change at-will status. Employment can be terminated at any time for any lawful reason.

### 14.2 Protected Activity

Discipline may not be based on:
- Protected class membership
- FMLA or ADA use
- Whistleblower activity
- Workers' compensation claims
- Union activity

### 14.3 Documentation Review

For terminations, HR and legal review documentation to ensure:
- Consistent treatment
- Non-discriminatory basis
- Proper documentation
- Appropriate process
