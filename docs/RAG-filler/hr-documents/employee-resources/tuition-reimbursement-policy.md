**Version:** 2.0

## 1. Purpose

Acme Corporation supports employee development through educational assistance. This policy provides financial support for employees pursuing higher education that benefits both their career growth and the company.

---

## 2. Eligibility

### 2.1 Employee Requirements

To be eligible for tuition reimbursement, you must:
- Be a regular full-time employee
- Have completed at least 1 year of continuous service
- Be in good standing (performance rating of "Meets Expectations" or higher)
- Not be on a Performance Improvement Plan
- Remain employed throughout the course of study

### 2.2 Timing

- Eligibility begins after 1 year of employment
- Request pre-approval before course begins
- Must be employed on last day of course to receive reimbursement

---

## 3. Eligible Programs and Courses

### 3.1 Eligible Programs

**Degree Programs:**
- Associate's degree
- Bachelor's degree
- Master's degree (including MBA)
- Doctoral degree
- Professional degrees (JD, MD)

**Professional Certifications:**
- Industry-recognized certifications
- Professional licenses
- Job-related certificate programs

### 3.2 Requirements

Programs must be:
- From an accredited institution
- Related to your current job or career path at Acme
- Pre-approved by your manager and HR

### 3.3 Accreditation

Institution must be accredited by:
- Regional accrediting agency recognized by U.S. Department of Education
- National accrediting agency for vocational/technical programs
- International equivalent for non-U.S. programs (requires additional approval)

### 3.4 Examples of Eligible Programs

| Current Role | Eligible Programs |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Software Engineer | Computer Science, Data Science, MBA |
| Marketing Manager | Marketing, Business, Communications |
| Financial Analyst | Accounting, Finance, MBA, CPA prep |
| HR Professional | HR Management, Psychology, MBA |
| Sales Representative | Business, Communications, MBA |

### 3.5 Not Eligible

- Courses for personal interest unrelated to work
- Audit courses (no credit)
- Courses you've already completed
- Courses at non-accredited institutions
- High school completion (GED)

---

## 4. Reimbursement Amounts

### 4.1 Annual Limits

| Program Type | Annual Maximum |
|--------------|----------------|
| Undergraduate degree | $5,250 |
| Graduate degree | $5,250 |
| Professional certification | $3,000 |

The $5,250 limit aligns with IRS tax-free educational assistance limits. Amounts above this may be taxable.

### 4.2 Covered Expenses

**Covered:**
- Tuition and fees
- Required textbooks
- Required lab fees
- Required software
- Exam fees (for certifications)

**Not Covered:**
- Room and board
- Transportation
- Parking
- Student activity fees
- Optional materials
- Application fees
- Late registration fees
- Retake fees (without approval)

### 4.3 Proration

If you join mid-year or leave during a course:
- Reimbursement prorated based on time employed during course
- Must be employed on last day of course for any reimbursement

---

## 5. Grade Requirements

### 5.1 Minimum Grades

To receive reimbursement, you must achieve:

| Program | Minimum Grade |
|---------|---------------|
| Undergraduate | B (3.0) or equivalent |
| Graduate | B+ (3.3) or equivalent |
| Pass/Fail courses | Pass |
| Certifications | Passing score |

### 5.2 Incomplete Grades

If you receive an incomplete:
- Reimbursement delayed until final grade posted
- Must achieve minimum grade when completed

### 5.3 Failed Courses

If you don't meet the minimum grade:
- No reimbursement for that course
- May retake course, but retake not automatically approved
- Discuss with HR before retaking

## 6. Application Process

### 6.1 Pre-Approval (Required)

Before registering for courses:

**Step 1:** Complete Tuition Reimbursement Request Form
- Available on HR portal
- Include course details, institution, cost estimate
- Explain relevance to job/career

**Step 2:** Obtain Manager Approval
- Manager reviews and approves
- Manager confirms job relevance

**Step 3:** Submit to HR
- HR reviews eligibility and budget
- HR approves or requests more information
- HR provides written approval

**Timeline:** Submit request at least 30 days before course start

### 6.2 During the Course

- Maintain acceptable academic standing
- Continue meeting job requirements
- Notify HR of any changes (course drop, grade issues)

### 6.3 Reimbursement Request

After completing the course:

**Step 1:** Gather Documentation
- Final grade (official transcript or grade report)
- Itemized receipts for tuition and eligible expenses
- Proof of payment

**Step 2:** Complete Reimbursement Request Form
- Available on HR portal
- Attach all documentation

**Step 3:** Submit Within 60 Days
- Submit within 60 days of course completion
- Late submissions may not be reimbursed

**Step 4:** Receive Reimbursement
- HR reviews and approves
- Payment processed within 30 days
- Paid through payroll (may appear on separate check)

## 7. Service Commitment

### 7.1 Commitment Period

After receiving tuition reimbursement, you commit to:
- Remain employed for 2 years from program completion
- Or 1 year for individual courses/certifications

### 7.2 Repayment

If you voluntarily leave or are terminated for cause within the commitment period:

| Time After Completion | Repayment Required |
|-----------------------|-------------------|
| Less than 6 months | 100% |
| 6-12 months | 75% |
| 12-18 months | 50% |
| 18-24 months | 25% |
| After 24 months | 0% |

### 7.3 Exceptions

Repayment not required if:
- Position eliminated (layoff)
- Transfer to another Acme location
- Medical disability
- Other circumstances approved by HR

### 7.4 Repayment Process

- Amount deducted from final pay (where legal)
- Or payment plan arranged
- Sign repayment agreement with each approval

## 8. Tax Considerations

### 8.1 Tax Treatment

**Tax-Free:**
- Up to $5,250 per year is tax-free per IRS Section 127
- Does not appear as taxable income

**Taxable:**
- Amounts over $5,250 are taxable income
- Subject to income tax and FICA
- Reported on W-2

### 8.2 Consult a Tax Professional

Tax implications depend on individual circumstances. Consult a tax professional for advice specific to your situation.

## 9. Work Schedule Considerations

### 9.1 Class Time

- Classes should not conflict with work hours when possible
- Discuss schedule conflicts with your manager
- Use PTO for classes during work hours
- No paid time off specifically for attending classes

### 9.2 Study Time

- Study on your own time
- Work responsibilities come first
- Plan accordingly to balance both

### 9.3 Flexible Arrangements

Some managers may approve flexible schedules to accommodate classes:
- Adjusted start/end times
- Remote work when appropriate
- Not guaranteed; manager discretion

## 10. Special Programs

### 10.1 Executive MBA Programs

Executive MBA programs may be eligible:
- Same annual limits apply
- Pre-approval required
- Multi-year commitment may apply
- Discuss with HR before applying

### 10.2 Accelerated Programs

Intensive or accelerated programs are eligible if they meet all requirements.

### 10.3 Online Programs

Online and distance learning programs are eligible:
- Must be from accredited institution
- Same requirements as in-person programs
- Growing number of quality online options

### 10.4 International Programs

Programs outside the U.S.:
- Require additional approval
- Must be equivalent to accredited U.S. program
- Currency conversion at actual rate

## 11. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I use tuition reimbursement for a degree unrelated to my job?**
A: The program must be related to your current job or a reasonable career path at Acme. Discuss with your manager and HR.

**Q: What if I receive other financial aid?**
A: Acme reimburses your out-of-pocket costs after other aid. Total reimbursement cannot exceed your actual costs.

**Q: Can I take classes during work hours?**
A: You should schedule classes outside work hours when possible. If unavoidable, use PTO and discuss with your manager.

**Q: What happens if I change jobs within Acme?**
A: Pre-approval is based on your role at approval time. Notify HR if your role changes significantly.

**Q: Can I get reimbursement for classes I already completed?**
A: No. Pre-approval is required before course starts.

**Q: Is there a lifetime maximum?**
A: No lifetime maximum, but annual limits apply each calendar year.

**Q: Can I combine this with other Acme training benefits?**
A: Yes. Tuition reimbursement is separate from your professional development budget.
