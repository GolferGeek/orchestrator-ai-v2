## 1. Purpose

Acme Corporation is committed to maintaining a positive work environment where concerns are addressed promptly and fairly. This procedure provides employees with clear channels to raise concerns, complaints, and grievances.

---

## 2. Scope

This procedure covers complaints and grievances related to:
- Working conditions
- Application of company policies
- Treatment by supervisors or colleagues
- Disciplinary actions
- Compensation and benefits
- Safety concerns
- Any other employment-related matters

**Not Covered by This Procedure:**
- Harassment and discrimination (see Anti-Harassment Policy)
- Whistleblower concerns (see Whistleblower Policy)
- Workers' compensation disputes (see Workers' Comp Policy)
- Union grievances (follow collective bargaining agreement)

---

## 3. Definitions

**Complaint:** An informal expression of concern about a work-related matter.

**Grievance:** A formal written allegation that company policy or practice has been violated or unfairly applied.

**Retaliation:** Adverse action against someone for raising a concern in good faith.

---

## 4. Guiding Principles

### 4.1 Open Door Philosophy

- Employees are encouraged to raise concerns early
- Managers should be accessible and receptive
- No concern is too small to discuss
- Address issues before they escalate

### 4.2 Non-Retaliation

- No retaliation for raising concerns in good faith
- Retaliation is a serious violation
- Report any suspected retaliation immediately

### 4.3 Fairness

- Concerns reviewed objectively
- All parties heard
- Consistent application of policies
- Timely resolution

### 4.4 Confidentiality

- Concerns kept confidential to extent possible
- Information shared only on need-to-know basis
- Complete confidentiality cannot always be guaranteed

## 5. Informal Resolution Process

### 5.1 Step 1: Talk to Your Supervisor

**Recommended as First Step**

Most concerns can be resolved through direct conversation:
1. Schedule a private meeting with your supervisor
2. Explain your concern clearly and calmly
3. Listen to their perspective
4. Work together toward resolution
5. Agree on next steps

**If Your Supervisor Is the Problem:**
- Go to your skip-level manager
- Or contact HR directly

### 5.2 Step 2: Discuss with HR

If Step 1 doesn't resolve the issue:
1. Contact HR at hr@acmecorp.com or ext. 4000
2. Explain your concern and what you've tried
3. HR will gather information
4. HR will work with you and management toward resolution
5. Informal resolution documented

**Timeline:** HR responds within 5 business days

### 5.3 Informal Resolution Options

Depending on the situation, resolution might include:
- Clarification of policy or practice
- Mediation between parties
- Coaching or feedback to involved parties
- Process or policy adjustments
- Other appropriate remedies

## 6. Formal Grievance Process

### 6.1 When to File a Formal Grievance

Consider a formal grievance when:
- Informal resolution was unsuccessful
- The issue is serious
- You want a formal record
- You are alleging policy violation
- You are appealing a disciplinary action

### 6.2 Step 1: Submit Written Grievance

**To File:**
1. Complete the Grievance Form (HR portal) or submit in writing
2. Include:
   - Your name, position, and department
   - Date of filing
   - Description of the concern (specific facts)
   - Dates and times of relevant events
   - Names of people involved or witnesses
   - Steps already taken to resolve
   - Resolution you are seeking
3. Submit to HR at hr@acmecorp.com or deliver to HR office

**Timeline:** Submit within 30 days of the incident or when you became aware of it

### 6.3 Step 2: HR Review

Upon receiving your grievance:
1. HR acknowledges receipt within 3 business days
2. HR reviews the grievance
3. HR conducts investigation if needed:
   - Interview you
   - Interview witnesses
   - Review documents
   - Interview subject of complaint
4. HR analyzes findings

**Timeline:** Investigation completed within 15 business days (complex cases may take longer)

### 6.4 Step 3: Decision

After review/investigation:
1. HR prepares findings and recommendation
2. Appropriate manager reviews and decides
3. Written decision provided to you
4. Decision includes:
   - Summary of findings
   - Determination
   - Any remedies or actions
   - Appeal rights

**Timeline:** Decision within 10 business days of completing investigation

### 6.5 Step 4: Appeal (If Needed)

If you disagree with the decision:
1. Submit written appeal within 10 business days
2. Appeal reviewed by next level of management and HR leadership
3. Appeal decision is final

**Appeal to:**
- Director of Employee Relations (first appeal)
- CHRO (final appeal for significant matters)

**Timeline:** Appeal decision within 15 business days

## 7. Specific Complaint Types

### 7.1 Disciplinary Action Appeals

To appeal a disciplinary action:
1. Submit written appeal within 5 business days
2. Include why you believe action was unfair or incorrect
3. HR reviews with management
4. Decision provided within 10 business days

### 7.2 Performance Review Disputes

To dispute a performance review:
1. First discuss with your manager
2. If unresolved, submit written explanation to HR
3. HR facilitates review with skip-level manager
4. Documentation added to file reflecting your perspective

### 7.3 Compensation Concerns

For compensation-related concerns:
1. Discuss with your manager first
2. If unresolved, contact HR or Compensation team
3. Compensation reviews market data and internal equity
4. Response provided within 15 business days

### 7.4 Safety Concerns

For safety concerns:
1. Report immediately to supervisor or Safety
2. Use Safety Hotline: ext. 5555
3. File through OSHA if external filing desired
4. No retaliation for safety reports

### 7.5 Harassment and Discrimination

Harassment and discrimination complaints follow the Anti-Harassment Policy:
- Report to HR or Ethics Hotline
- Investigation per that policy
- Different timeline and process

## 8. Investigation Process

### 8.1 What to Expect

If your grievance requires investigation:
- HR (or designated investigator) will contact you
- Interview will be scheduled
- You can provide documents and witness names
- You'll be asked to maintain confidentiality
- Investigation is thorough but also timely

### 8.2 Your Responsibilities

During the investigation:
- Be truthful and complete
- Provide relevant information
- Maintain confidentiality
- Cooperate fully
- Refrain from retaliation

### 8.3 Investigation Confidentiality

- Information shared only with those who need to know
- You should not discuss the investigation with others
- Protect the integrity of the process

### 8.4 Outcome Communication

You will be informed of:
- That investigation is complete
- General outcome (substantiated, unsubstantiated, inconclusive)
- Any actions affecting you
- Specific discipline of others may not be shared

---

## 9. Remedies

### 9.1 Possible Outcomes

Depending on findings, remedies may include:
- No action (if not substantiated)
- Policy clarification
- Training or coaching
- Mediation
- Policy or process changes
- Disciplinary action against others
- Reversal of action against you
- Other appropriate remedies

### 9.2 What Remedies Cannot Include

- Guarantees of specific discipline for others
- Disclosure of confidential personnel actions
- Changes that violate policy or law
- Actions beyond company authority

---

## 10. Retaliation Protection

### 10.1 What Is Retaliation?

Retaliation includes adverse action because you:
- Filed a complaint or grievance
- Participated in an investigation
- Opposed unlawful practices

**Examples:**
- Termination or demotion
- Reduction in hours or pay
- Unfavorable job assignments
- Exclusion from opportunities
- Negative treatment or hostility

### 10.2 Reporting Retaliation

If you experience retaliation:
- Report immediately to HR
- Or use Ethics Hotline: 1-800-555-ETHIC
- Retaliation claims investigated promptly

### 10.3 Consequences

Retaliation is a serious violation resulting in:
- Disciplinary action up to termination
- Regardless of original complaint outcome

## 14. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I file anonymously?**
A: Anonymous complaints can be made through the Ethics Hotline. However, anonymous complaints may limit our ability to investigate fully.

**Q: What if I'm afraid of retaliation?**
A: Retaliation is prohibited and taken seriously. If you experience retaliation, report it immediately.

**Q: How long does the process take?**
A: Simple matters may resolve within days. Formal grievances typically resolved within 30-45 days.

**Q: Can I bring someone to meetings?**
A: You may request to have HR present. In some cases, you may have a support person.

**Q: What if I'm not satisfied with the outcome?**
A: You may appeal using the appeal process described in this procedure.

**Q: Will my complaint affect my job?**
A: Filing a good-faith complaint will not negatively affect your employment. Retaliation is prohibited.
