**Document Owner:** Legal & Compliance Department
**Approved By:** Robert Chen, General Counsel

## 1. Policy Statement

Acme Corporation is committed to conducting business with integrity and in compliance with all applicable laws and regulations. We encourage employees to report suspected wrongdoing without fear of retaliation. This policy establishes procedures for reporting concerns and protections for those who report in good faith.

---

## 2. Purpose

This policy:
- Encourages reporting of suspected wrongdoing
- Establishes clear reporting channels
- Protects reporters from retaliation
- Ensures prompt investigation of concerns
- Supports our culture of ethics and compliance

---

## 3. Scope

### 3.1 Who Is Covered

This policy protects:
- Employees (full-time, part-time, temporary)
- Contractors and consultants
- Board members
- Former employees
- Job applicants

### 3.2 What Should Be Reported

Report suspected:
- Fraud, embezzlement, or theft
- Financial reporting irregularities
- Securities violations
- Bribery or corruption
- Violations of laws or regulations
- Antitrust violations
- Environmental violations
- Safety violations
- Discrimination or harassment
- Conflicts of interest
- Misuse of company assets
- Data privacy breaches
- Any violation of company policy

### 3.3 What This Policy Does NOT Cover

This policy is not for:
- Routine HR complaints (performance, pay disputes)
- Personal grievances
- Business disagreements
- Matters that should go through normal channels first

Use appropriate HR processes for employment-related concerns that are not policy violations.

## 5. How to Report

### 5.1 Information to Include

When reporting, provide as much detail as possible:
- What happened
- When it happened
- Where it happened
- Who was involved
- How you became aware
- Any supporting evidence (documents, emails, etc.)
- Names of witnesses
- Whether you have reported previously

### 5.2 Anonymous Reporting

You may report anonymously:
- Via Ethics Hotline or online portal
- We will investigate without knowing your identity
- You can provide a follow-up number/code for communication
- Investigation may be limited without ability to ask questions

### 5.3 Confidentiality

We protect reporter identity to the extent possible:
- Shared only on need-to-know basis
- Investigation conducted discreetly
- Your name will not be disclosed without consent
- Exception: May be required by law or legal proceeding

---

## 6. Good Faith Reporting

### 6.1 What Is Good Faith?

Good faith means:
- You reasonably believe the report is true
- You are not making false accusations
- You are not reporting for personal gain or revenge
- You are honest about what you know

### 6.2 False Reports

False reports made with malicious intent:
- Are not protected by this policy
- May result in disciplinary action
- May result in legal consequences

However, making a report that turns out to be unsubstantiated does NOT violate this policy if made in good faith.

---

## 7. Investigation Process

### 7.1 Receipt of Report

When a report is received:
1. Report is logged confidentially
2. Initial assessment within 48 hours
3. Reporter acknowledged (if identity known)
4. Appropriate investigator assigned

### 7.2 Investigation

Investigations are:
- Prompt and thorough
- Fair and impartial
- Conducted by qualified personnel
- Documented appropriately
- Confidential to extent possible

**Investigation Steps:**
1. Review allegations
2. Gather documents and evidence
3. Interview reporter (if possible and appropriate)
4. Interview witnesses
5. Interview subject of complaint
6. Analyze findings
7. Reach conclusions
8. Recommend actions

### 7.3 Timeframe

Most investigations completed within 30-60 days. Complex matters may take longer. Reporter updated on progress as appropriate.

### 7.4 Outcomes

**If Substantiated:**
- Appropriate corrective action taken
- May include discipline up to termination
- Systemic issues addressed
- Process improvements implemented

**If Unsubstantiated:**
- No adverse action against subject
- Documentation maintained confidentially
- Reporter not penalized for good faith report

### 7.5 Communication

- Reporter informed of outcome (general terms)
- Specific disciplinary actions may not be shared
- Appeal process available if reporter disagrees with outcome

## 8. Non-Retaliation

### 8.1 Prohibition on Retaliation

Retaliation against anyone who reports in good faith is strictly prohibited.

**Retaliation includes:**
- Termination or demotion
- Reduction in pay or hours
- Unfavorable job assignments
- Threats or intimidation
- Harassment or ostracism
- Negative performance reviews not based on performance
- Transfer to undesirable position
- Denial of benefits or promotion

### 8.2 Who Is Protected

Protection extends to those who:
- Report suspected wrongdoing
- Participate in investigations
- Refuse to participate in wrongdoing
- Report to government agencies
- Testify in legal proceedings

### 8.3 Reporting Retaliation

If you experience retaliation:
- Report immediately through same channels
- Retaliation claims investigated promptly
- Retaliation treated as serious violation

### 8.4 Consequences of Retaliation

Anyone who retaliates faces:
- Disciplinary action up to termination
- Possible personal liability
- No protection under this policy

## 9. Legal Protections

### 9.1 Federal Whistleblower Protections

Various laws protect whistleblowers:

**Sarbanes-Oxley Act (SOX):**
- Protects employees of public companies
- Reports of securities fraud
- Can't be retaliated against
- Remedies include reinstatement, back pay, damages

**Dodd-Frank Act:**
- SEC whistleblower program
- Financial awards (10-30% of sanctions over $1 million)
- Report to SEC directly
- Anti-retaliation provisions

**False Claims Act:**
- Reports of fraud against the government
- Qui tam lawsuits
- Percentage of recovery to whistleblower

**Other Laws:**
- OSHA (safety)
- SOX (financial)
- AML/BSA (money laundering)
- Environmental laws
- Many state whistleblower laws

### 9.2 Confidentiality of SEC Reports

Under Dodd-Frank, SEC does not disclose whistleblower identity unless:
- Required by law
- Whistleblower consents

### 9.3 Nothing in This Policy Limits Your Legal Rights

This policy does not:
- Require internal reporting before external
- Limit discussions with government agencies
- Prevent you from collecting rewards
- Waive your legal protections
