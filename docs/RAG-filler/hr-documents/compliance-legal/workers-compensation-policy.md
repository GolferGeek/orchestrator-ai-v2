## 1. Purpose

This policy outlines Acme Corporation's workers' compensation program, which provides benefits to employees who suffer work-related injuries or illnesses. Our goal is to ensure prompt medical treatment, fair compensation, and support for employees to return to work safely.

---

## 2. Coverage

### 2.1 Who Is Covered

All Acme Corporation employees are covered by workers' compensation insurance, including:
- Full-time employees
- Part-time employees
- Temporary employees
- Seasonal employees

Coverage begins on the first day of employment.

### 2.2 What Is Covered

Workers' compensation covers injuries and illnesses that:
- Arise out of employment
- Occur in the course of employment

**Examples of Covered Injuries:**
- Slip and fall in the office
- Repetitive strain injury from work tasks
- Injury while using work equipment
- Car accident while traveling for work
- Exposure to hazardous substances at work

### 2.3 What Is NOT Covered

Generally, workers' compensation does NOT cover:
- Injuries during commute to/from work (with exceptions)
- Self-inflicted injuries
- Injuries while intoxicated or using illegal drugs
- Injuries during personal activities at work
- Injuries from fighting (unless in self-defense)
- Injuries while violating company policy
- Pre-existing conditions (unless aggravated by work)

## 3. Benefits

### 3.1 Medical Benefits

Workers' compensation pays for reasonable and necessary medical treatment, including:
- Doctor visits
- Hospital care
- Surgery
- Physical therapy
- Prescription medications
- Medical equipment
- Mileage for medical appointments

**No deductibles or copays** for work-related injuries.

### 3.2 Income Benefits

If unable to work due to a work-related injury:

**Temporary Total Disability (TTD):**
- When completely unable to work temporarily
- Typically 66-70% of average weekly wage
- Subject to state maximum limits
- Waiting period: 7 days (retroactive if disability exceeds 14-21 days)

**Temporary Partial Disability (TPD):**
- When able to work with restrictions at reduced capacity
- Percentage of wage difference between full and restricted duty pay

**Permanent Disability:**
- For lasting impairment
- Amount based on impairment rating
- Varies significantly by state

### 3.3 Death Benefits

If a work-related injury results in death:
- Funeral and burial expenses (up to state limits)
- Survivor benefits for spouse and dependents
- Percentage of deceased worker's wages

### 3.4 State Variations

Benefits vary by state. Texas employees should note:
- Texas is a non-subscription state
- Acme Corporation DOES carry workers' compensation coverage
- Benefits follow Texas Workers' Compensation Act

## 4. Reporting Work Injuries

### 4.1 Employee Responsibilities

**Immediately:**
1. Seek medical attention if needed (call 911 for emergencies)
2. Report the injury to your supervisor
3. If supervisor unavailable, report to HR

**Within 24 Hours:**
1. Complete Incident Report Form
2. Provide details of how, when, and where injury occurred
3. Identify any witnesses

**Ongoing:**
1. Keep HR informed of medical status
2. Provide medical documentation
3. Follow treatment plan
4. Communicate about return to work

### 4.2 Supervisor Responsibilities

**Immediately:**
1. Ensure employee receives appropriate medical care
2. Secure the area if safety hazard exists
3. Notify HR and Safety

**Within 24 Hours:**
1. Complete Supervisor's Incident Report
2. Investigate the incident
3. Identify corrective actions
4. Preserve any evidence

### 4.3 HR Responsibilities

1. File claim with insurance carrier within required timeframe
2. Provide employee with workers' comp information
3. Track claim status
4. Coordinate return to work
5. Maintain documentation

### 4.4 Reporting Deadlines

| Action | Deadline |
|--------|----------|
| Employee reports to supervisor | Immediately / same day |
| Incident Report completed | Within 24 hours |
| Employer reports to carrier | Within 24-48 hours (varies by state) |
| Employee files formal claim | Varies by state (30 days - 2 years) |

**Important:** Late reporting can result in denial of benefits.

## 6. Return to Work

### 6.1 Philosophy

Acme Corporation supports early, safe return to work. Studies show that returning to work (when medically appropriate) aids recovery and benefits employees.

### 6.2 Modified Duty Program

If you cannot perform your regular job, we will try to accommodate:
- **Light duty:** Same job with reduced physical demands
- **Modified duty:** Different tasks within restrictions
- **Transitional work:** Temporary assignments during recovery

### 6.3 Return to Work Process

1. **Medical Release:** Obtain written release from treating physician
2. **Restrictions:** Provide HR with any work restrictions
3. **Assessment:** HR evaluates if restrictions can be accommodated
4. **Return:** Return to regular or modified duty
5. **Monitor:** Ongoing assessment of progress

### 6.4 Work Restrictions

If you have work restrictions:
- Provide written restrictions from your doctor
- Follow restrictions exactly
- Do not perform tasks beyond restrictions
- Report any difficulties to supervisor and HR

### 6.5 Failure to Return

If you fail to return when released:
- Communicate any concerns immediately
- Get documentation if you disagree with release
- Understand that refusing reasonable work affects benefits

## 7. Benefits Coordination

### 7.1 Paid Time Off

- PTO cannot be used simultaneously with workers' comp income benefits
- May use PTO during waiting period (before income benefits begin)
- Benefits coordinate to not exceed regular pay

### 7.2 Health Insurance

- Group health insurance continues during workers' comp leave
- Employee pays their normal contribution
- Coordination of benefits applies if both plans involved

### 7.3 FMLA

- Workers' comp leave may run concurrently with FMLA
- Job protection under FMLA for up to 12 weeks
- Notify HR to coordinate leaves

### 7.4 Short/Long-Term Disability

- Workers' comp is primary; STD/LTD are secondary
- May receive STD/LTD for difference if workers' comp is less
- Specific coordination rules apply

## 8. Fraud Prevention

### 8.1 What Is Workers' Compensation Fraud

Fraud includes:
- Claiming injuries that didn't happen
- Exaggerating injury severity
- Claiming personal injuries as work-related
- Working while receiving total disability benefits
- Delaying return to work intentionally
- Provider billing fraud

### 8.2 Consequences of Fraud

Workers' compensation fraud is a crime:
- Denial of benefits
- Repayment of benefits received
- Termination of employment
- Criminal prosecution
- Fines and imprisonment

### 8.3 Reporting Suspected Fraud

Report suspected fraud to:
- HR: hr@acmecorp.com
- Ethics Hotline: 1-800-555-ETHIC
- Texas Department of Insurance Fraud Unit: 1-888-327-8818

Reports may be made anonymously.

## 9. Employee Rights

### 9.1 Right to Benefits

You have the right to:
- File a workers' compensation claim
- Receive medical treatment
- Receive income benefits if unable to work
- Have your claim processed fairly
- Return to work when medically released

### 9.2 No Retaliation

Acme Corporation prohibits retaliation against employees who:
- Report work injuries
- File workers' compensation claims
- Testify in workers' compensation proceedings

Retaliation should be reported to HR or the Ethics Hotline.

### 9.3 Dispute Resolution

If you disagree with a claim decision:
1. Discuss with HR
2. Contact insurance carrier
3. File dispute with state workers' compensation board
4. Seek legal counsel if needed

### 9.4 Right to Legal Representation

You may hire an attorney to represent you in workers' compensation matters. Attorney fees are typically paid from your settlement/award.
