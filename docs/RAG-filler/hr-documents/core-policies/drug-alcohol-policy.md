## 1. Policy Statement

Acme Corporation is committed to maintaining a safe, healthy, and productive work environment free from the influence of drugs and alcohol. Substance abuse poses serious risks to employee health and safety, job performance, and company reputation.

This policy establishes clear expectations regarding drugs and alcohol and outlines consequences for violations. We also provide support for employees struggling with substance abuse issues.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, temporary workers, and interns. It covers conduct:
- On company premises
- During work hours
- While conducting company business
- At company-sponsored events
- While operating company vehicles or equipment
- Any time an employee represents Acme Corporation

---

## 3. Prohibited Conduct

### 3.1 General Prohibitions

The following are prohibited:

**Illegal Drugs:**
- Use, possession, distribution, sale, or manufacture of illegal drugs
- Being under the influence of illegal drugs
- Possessing drug paraphernalia

**Alcohol:**
- Consuming alcohol during work hours (except at approved events)
- Being under the influence of alcohol while working
- Possessing open containers of alcohol on premises (except at approved events)
- Operating company vehicles or equipment after consuming alcohol

**Legal Drugs (including marijuana):**
- Being impaired by any substance that affects ability to work safely
- Misusing prescription or over-the-counter medications
- Using marijuana at work, regardless of state legality

**Other:**
- Refusing a drug or alcohol test
- Tampering with or adulterating test samples
- Switching samples or using substances to mask results
- Possessing or using synthetic drugs or designer drugs

### 3.2 Prescription and Over-the-Counter Medications

Employees taking medications that may affect their ability to work safely must:
- Inform their supervisor or HR before starting work
- Provide medical documentation if requested
- Follow medical instructions for use
- Not exceed recommended doses

Acme Corporation will work with employees to provide reasonable accommodations as needed.

### 3.3 Marijuana and Cannabis

Although marijuana is legal in some states, it remains a Schedule I controlled substance under federal law. Therefore:
- Use of marijuana is prohibited during work hours
- Employees may not be impaired by marijuana at work
- Employees in safety-sensitive positions may be subject to additional restrictions
- Acme Corporation complies with federal contractor requirements prohibiting marijuana use

## 4. Drug and Alcohol Testing

### 4.1 When Testing Occurs

Acme Corporation conducts drug and alcohol testing in the following circumstances:

**Pre-Employment Testing:**
- All offers of employment are contingent on passing a drug test
- Tests must be completed within 72 hours of accepting an offer
- Failure or refusal results in withdrawal of the offer

**Reasonable Suspicion Testing:**
- When a supervisor has reasonable suspicion that an employee is under the influence
- Reasonable suspicion must be documented
- Two trained supervisors should observe and concur when possible

**Post-Incident Testing:**
- Following workplace accidents resulting in injury or property damage
- Following vehicle accidents involving company vehicles
- Testing should occur within 8 hours for alcohol, 32 hours for drugs

**Random Testing:**
- For employees in safety-sensitive positions
- Performed on an unannounced, random selection basis
- At least 50% of safety-sensitive employees tested annually

**Return-to-Duty and Follow-Up Testing:**
- For employees returning after violating this policy
- Conducted before returning to work
- Follow-up testing for up to 5 years after return

### 4.2 Substances Tested

Standard drug tests screen for:
- Marijuana (THC)
- Cocaine
- Opiates (heroin, codeine, morphine)
- Amphetamines (including methamphetamine)
- Phencyclidine (PCP)
- Benzodiazepines
- Barbiturates
- Methadone
- Propoxyphene
- MDMA (Ecstasy)

Alcohol testing uses breath, blood, or saliva testing.

### 4.3 Testing Procedures

All testing follows established protocols:
- Conducted by certified laboratories
- Chain of custody maintained
- Confirmation testing (GC/MS) for positive results
- Medical Review Officer (MRO) reviews all positive results
- Employees may request retest of split sample

### 4.4 Confidentiality

Test results are treated as confidential medical information:
- Shared only on a need-to-know basis
- Maintained separately from personnel files
- Protected under applicable privacy laws

---

## 5. Consequences of Violations

### 5.1 Positive Test Results

**Pre-Employment:** Offer withdrawn; may reapply after 6 months with proof of rehabilitation.

**Current Employees:**
- First positive test: May be offered opportunity for rehabilitation (see Section 6); return to work contingent on completing treatment and passing return-to-duty test
- Second positive test: Termination
- Refusal to test: Treated as positive test

### 5.2 Other Violations

Employees who violate this policy through conduct other than positive tests (e.g., possession, impairment, distribution) are subject to:
- Immediate removal from the workplace
- Investigation
- Discipline up to and including termination

Distribution or sale of drugs results in immediate termination and referral to law enforcement.

### 5.3 Safety-Sensitive Positions

Employees in safety-sensitive positions may face more stringent consequences:
- Immediate removal from safety-sensitive duties
- More frequent follow-up testing
- Extended monitoring period

Safety-sensitive positions include:
- Employees who operate vehicles or heavy equipment
- Employees who work with hazardous materials
- Employees who have access to sensitive areas or information
- Employees whose impairment could endanger others

## 6. Employee Assistance and Rehabilitation

### 6.1 Employee Assistance Program (EAP)

Acme Corporation provides an Employee Assistance Program to help employees with substance abuse issues. The EAP offers:
- Confidential assessments
- Short-term counseling
- Referrals to treatment programs
- Family support services

**EAP Contact:**
- Phone: 1-800-555-4EAP (24/7)
- Website: eap.acmecorp.com

Services are confidential and free to employees and their families.

### 6.2 Voluntary Self-Referral

Employees who voluntarily seek help for substance abuse before a policy violation may be eligible for:
- Leave of absence for treatment
- Continuation of benefits during treatment
- Return to work after successful completion
- No disciplinary action for self-disclosure

To be eligible, employees must self-refer before:
- Being notified of a drug test
- Being involved in an incident
- Being observed impaired

### 6.3 Rehabilitation Programs

Employees who test positive for the first time may be offered the opportunity to participate in a rehabilitation program. Requirements include:
- Evaluation by a substance abuse professional (SAP)
- Completion of recommended treatment
- Signing a Return-to-Work Agreement
- Passing a return-to-duty test
- Participation in follow-up testing for up to 5 years

During treatment, employees may use:
- Accrued PTO or sick leave
- Unpaid leave (up to 12 weeks under FMLA if eligible)
- Short-term disability (if eligible)

## 7. Company-Sponsored Events

### 7.1 Events with Alcohol

At approved company-sponsored events where alcohol is served:
- Consumption must be moderate and responsible
- Employees must not become intoxicated
- Employees must arrange safe transportation if needed
- Non-alcoholic beverages and food must be available
- Events must have a designated end time

### 7.2 Manager Responsibilities at Events

Managers should:
- Model responsible behavior
- Not pressure employees to drink
- Ensure safe transportation options
- Address inappropriate behavior
- Stop serving alcohol to intoxicated individuals

### 7.3 Business Meals

Moderate alcohol consumption during business meals is permitted if:
- It does not impair judgment or performance
- The employee will not be operating vehicles or equipment
- It is appropriate for the business setting

## 8. Reasonable Suspicion

### 8.1 Indicators of Impairment

Signs that may indicate impairment include:
- Slurred speech
- Unsteady gait or coordination problems
- Bloodshot or glassy eyes
- Unusual behavior or mood swings
- Smell of alcohol or drugs
- Decreased alertness or drowsiness
- Excessive absenteeism or tardiness
- Decline in work performance
- Physical evidence (bottles, paraphernalia)

### 8.2 Supervisor Responsibilities

Supervisors who observe signs of impairment should:
1. Document specific observations (behavior, speech, appearance, odor)
2. Consult with another manager or HR
3. Remove the employee from safety-sensitive duties
4. Meet with the employee privately
5. Arrange for reasonable suspicion testing
6. Arrange safe transportation home
7. Place employee on administrative leave pending results

### 8.3 Training

Supervisors receive training on:
- Recognizing signs of impairment
- Documentation requirements
- Proper procedures for reasonable suspicion
- Legal considerations

## 9. Legal Compliance

### 9.1 Federal Requirements

This policy complies with:
- Drug-Free Workplace Act of 1988 (for federal contractors)
- DOT regulations (for covered positions)
- OSHA requirements

### 9.2 State Laws

Where state laws conflict with this policy, we will comply with applicable law. However, federal contractor requirements may supersede state marijuana laws.

### 9.3 ADA Considerations

The Americans with Disabilities Act protects individuals in recovery from substance abuse who:
- Are no longer using illegal drugs
- Have successfully completed or are participating in rehabilitation
- Are not currently using illegal drugs

Acme Corporation provides reasonable accommodations for employees in recovery.
