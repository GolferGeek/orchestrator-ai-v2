## 1. Policy Statement

Acme Corporation is committed to providing a work environment free from harassment and discrimination. We do not tolerate harassment or discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex (including pregnancy, childbirth, and related medical conditions), national origin, age (40 or older), disability, genetic information, veteran status, sexual orientation, gender identity or expression, marital status, or any other characteristic protected by applicable federal, state, or local law.

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, vendors, clients, visitors, and anyone else who interacts with our workforce. It covers conduct in the workplace, at company-sponsored events, during business travel, and in any other context that may affect the work environment.

---

## 2. Definitions

### 2.1 Harassment

Harassment is unwelcome conduct based on a protected characteristic that:
- Creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment; OR
- Results in an adverse employment decision (such as termination, demotion, or denial of promotion); OR
- Unreasonably interferes with an individual's work performance.

### 2.2 Sexual Harassment

Sexual harassment is a form of harassment that includes:

**Quid Pro Quo Harassment:** When submission to or rejection of sexual conduct is used as the basis for employment decisions.

**Hostile Work Environment:** When sexual conduct unreasonably interferes with work performance or creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment.

Examples of sexual harassment include but are not limited to:
- Unwelcome sexual advances or propositions
- Requests for sexual favors
- Sexual jokes, comments, or innuendo
- Displaying sexually explicit images or materials
- Unwanted physical contact, including touching, hugging, or kissing
- Leering or staring
- Sexual gestures
- Comments about a person's body, appearance, or sexual activity
- Spreading sexual rumors
- Sexual assault

### 2.3 Other Forms of Harassment

Harassment based on any protected characteristic is prohibited. Examples include:
- Racial slurs, epithets, or derogatory comments
- Religious jokes or mockery of religious practices
- Age-related jokes or comments suggesting someone is "too old"
- Mocking accents or national origins
- Derogatory comments about disabilities
- Offensive comments about sexual orientation or gender identity
- Displaying offensive symbols, images, or materials
- Exclusion from activities or opportunities based on protected characteristics

### 2.4 Discrimination

Discrimination is treating someone less favorably in employment decisions because of their protected characteristics. Employment decisions include:
- Hiring and firing
- Compensation and benefits
- Job assignments and promotions
- Training and development opportunities
- Performance evaluations
- Discipline and termination

### 2.5 Retaliation

Retaliation is taking adverse action against someone because they:
- Reported harassment or discrimination in good faith
- Participated in an investigation or proceeding
- Opposed discriminatory practices
- Requested a reasonable accommodation

Retaliation can include termination, demotion, reduction in pay, exclusion from opportunities, negative performance reviews, or any other action that would discourage a reasonable person from reporting concerns.

---

## 3. Prohibited Conduct

### 3.1 Zero Tolerance

Acme Corporation has zero tolerance for:
- Sexual harassment of any kind
- Harassment based on any protected characteristic
- Discrimination in any employment decision
- Retaliation against those who report concerns or participate in investigations

### 3.2 Specific Prohibitions

The following conduct is strictly prohibited:
- Making hiring, promotion, or other employment decisions based on submission to sexual advances
- Creating a hostile work environment through unwelcome conduct
- Making derogatory comments about protected characteristics
- Displaying offensive materials in the workplace or on company systems
- Excluding individuals from opportunities based on protected characteristics
- Taking adverse action against anyone who reports concerns in good faith
- Failing to take appropriate action when aware of harassment or discrimination

### 3.3 Third-Party Harassment

Acme Corporation also prohibits harassment by or toward third parties, including:
- Clients and customers
- Vendors and contractors
- Visitors
- Temporary workers

Employees should report any harassment by third parties immediately.

## 4. Responsibilities

### 4.1 All Employees

All employees are responsible for:
- Treating colleagues and others with dignity and respect
- Refraining from conduct that could be perceived as harassment or discrimination
- Reporting harassment, discrimination, or retaliation they experience or witness
- Cooperating in investigations
- Completing required anti-harassment training

### 4.2 Managers and Supervisors

Managers and supervisors have additional responsibilities:
- Modeling appropriate behavior
- Creating and maintaining a respectful work environment
- Promptly reporting any complaints or concerns to HR
- Taking immediate action to stop observed inappropriate behavior
- Not retaliating against employees who report concerns
- Ensuring employees complete required training
- Monitoring for signs of harassment or discrimination
- Following through on corrective actions

Managers who fail to report or address known harassment may face disciplinary action.

### 4.3 Human Resources

Human Resources is responsible for:
- Administering this policy
- Receiving and investigating complaints
- Maintaining confidentiality to the extent possible
- Recommending appropriate corrective action
- Ensuring non-retaliation
- Providing training and education
- Maintaining records of complaints and investigations
- Reporting to leadership on policy compliance

### 4.4 Executive Leadership

Executive leadership is responsible for:
- Setting the tone for a respectful workplace
- Allocating resources for training and compliance
- Reviewing significant complaints and investigation outcomes
- Ensuring accountability at all levels
- Supporting HR in enforcement

### 5.2 Information to Provide

When making a report, please provide as much information as possible:
- Names of individuals involved
- Dates, times, and locations of incidents
- Description of what happened
- Names of any witnesses
- Any documentation (emails, messages, photos, etc.)
- How the conduct has affected you

### 5.3 Anonymous Reporting

Anonymous reports can be made through the Ethics Hotline or online reporting system. While anonymous reports will be investigated, providing contact information allows for follow-up questions and updates.

### 5.4 Confidentiality

All complaints will be kept confidential to the extent possible. Information will be shared only with those who need to know to conduct an investigation or implement corrective action. However, complete confidentiality cannot be guaranteed.

## 6. Investigation Process

### 6.1 Initial Assessment

Upon receiving a complaint, HR will:
- Acknowledge receipt within 24 hours
- Assess the nature and severity of the allegations
- Determine if immediate protective measures are needed
- Assign an investigator

### 6.2 Investigation Steps

Investigations typically include:
1. Interview with the complainant
2. Review of relevant documents and evidence
3. Interview with the accused
4. Interviews with witnesses
5. Additional follow-up as needed
6. Analysis of findings
7. Documentation of conclusions

### 6.3 Timeline

Most investigations are completed within 30 days. Complex cases may take longer. The complainant and accused will be kept informed of progress.

### 6.4 Interim Measures

During an investigation, interim measures may be implemented to protect the parties involved. These may include:
- Separating the parties
- Modifying work schedules or locations
- Placing the accused on administrative leave
- Providing additional supervision

### 6.5 Outcome

Upon completion of the investigation, HR will:
- Determine whether a policy violation occurred
- Recommend appropriate corrective action
- Communicate the outcome to the complainant and accused
- Document the investigation and outcome
- Follow up to ensure no retaliation occurs

---

## 7. Corrective Action

### 7.1 Range of Actions

If a violation is found, corrective action will be taken. The specific action depends on factors including:
- Severity of the conduct
- Whether it was a single incident or pattern
- Impact on the victim
- Accused's position and history
- Any prior violations

### 7.2 Possible Consequences

Corrective actions may include:
- Verbal warning with documentation
- Written warning
- Mandatory training or counseling
- Performance improvement plan
- Demotion
- Transfer
- Suspension without pay
- Termination

### 7.3 Progressive Discipline

While progressive discipline is typically used, Acme Corporation reserves the right to impose any level of discipline, up to and including termination, for a first offense depending on the severity of the conduct.

---

## 8. Protection from Retaliation

### 8.1 Non-Retaliation Commitment

Acme Corporation strictly prohibits retaliation against anyone who:
- Reports a concern in good faith
- Participates in an investigation
- Opposes discriminatory practices
- Exercises their rights under this policy

### 8.2 What Constitutes Retaliation

Retaliation includes any adverse action that would discourage a reasonable person from reporting concerns, such as:
- Termination or demotion
- Reduction in pay or hours
- Reassignment to less desirable duties
- Exclusion from meetings or opportunities
- Negative performance reviews not based on performance
- Increased scrutiny or criticism
- Social isolation or hostility
- Threats or intimidation

### 8.3 Reporting Retaliation

Anyone who believes they have experienced retaliation should report it immediately using the same channels available for reporting harassment. Retaliation complaints are investigated separately and may result in discipline independent of the underlying complaint.

## 9. Training Requirements

### 9.1 Mandatory Training

All employees must complete anti-harassment training:
- **New Hires:** Within 30 days of start date
- **All Employees:** Annual refresher training
- **Managers:** Additional supervisor-specific training annually

### 9.2 Training Content

Training covers:
- Definition of harassment and discrimination
- Examples of prohibited conduct
- Reporting procedures
- Investigation process
- Non-retaliation policy
- Bystander intervention techniques
- Manager-specific responsibilities (for supervisory training)

### 9.3 Tracking and Compliance

HR tracks training completion. Failure to complete required training may result in disciplinary action, including withholding of bonuses or other consequences.

## 10. Record Keeping

### 10.1 Records Maintained

HR maintains records of:
- All complaints received
- Investigation files
- Corrective actions taken
- Training completion

### 10.2 Retention Period

Records are retained for at least 7 years or as required by law, whichever is longer.

### 10.3 Confidentiality

Records are kept confidential and accessed only by those with a legitimate need to know.

## 11. Legal Protections

### 11.1 External Agencies

Employees have the right to file complaints with external agencies, including:
- **Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC):** www.eeoc.gov, 1-800-669-4000
- **State Civil Rights Agencies**
- **Local Human Rights Commissions**

Filing an internal complaint does not prevent filing with external agencies, and vice versa.

### 11.2 Legal Counsel

Employees have the right to consult with legal counsel at any time during the process.

### 11.3 Workplace Protections

Federal and state laws protect employees from workplace harassment and discrimination, including:
- Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964
- Age Discrimination in Employment Act (ADEA)
- Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)
- Pregnancy Discrimination Act
- State and local anti-discrimination laws
