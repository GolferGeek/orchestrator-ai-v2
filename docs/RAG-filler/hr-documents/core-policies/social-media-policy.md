# Acme Corporation Social Media Policy

**Effective Date:** June 1, 2022
**Last Revised:** November 22, 2025
**Version:** 2.0
**Document Owner:** Marketing & Communications Department
**Approved By:** Michelle Wong, VP of Marketing

---

## 1. Policy Statement

Social media is a powerful tool for communication, collaboration, and brand building. Acme Corporation encourages employees to be brand ambassadors while using social media responsibly. This policy provides guidelines for both professional and personal social media use to protect our employees, our company, and our reputation.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and interns. It covers:
- Personal social media accounts when discussing work-related topics
- Official company social media accounts
- Professional networking sites
- All platforms including LinkedIn, X (Twitter), Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, Reddit, blogs, and emerging platforms

---

## 3. Official Company Social Media

### 3.1 Authorized Spokespersons

Only authorized employees may post on behalf of Acme Corporation:

**Corporate Accounts:**
- Marketing Communications team
- Designated social media managers
- Executive communications team

**Departmental Accounts:**
- HR: Recruiting team for career content
- Engineering: Developer relations team for technical content
- Sales: Marketing-approved account managers

### 3.2 Brand Guidelines

Official posts must follow:
- Brand voice and tone guidelines
- Visual identity standards
- Approval processes for sensitive topics
- Response protocols for customer inquiries

### 3.3 Content Approval

Before posting on official accounts:
- Routine content: Manager approval
- Press releases and announcements: Corporate Communications approval
- Product information: Product Marketing approval
- Legal or controversial topics: Legal and Communications approval

### 3.4 Crisis Communications

During a crisis or sensitive situation:
- Stop all scheduled posts
- Contact Corporate Communications immediately
- Do not respond to inquiries without approval
- Follow the Crisis Communication Protocol

---

## 4. Personal Social Media Use

### 4.1 General Guidelines

When using personal social media:

**Do:**
- Be authentic and genuine
- Think before posting
- Be respectful of others
- Protect confidential information
- Comply with all company policies
- Consider how posts may be perceived

**Don't:**
- Speak on behalf of Acme without authorization
- Share confidential or proprietary information
- Post content that could embarrass yourself or the company
- Engage in arguments or controversial debates as an Acme employee
- Make discriminatory or harassing statements

### 4.2 Identifying Yourself as an Acme Employee

If you identify yourself as an Acme employee on social media:
- Make clear that opinions are your own
- Use a disclaimer when discussing industry topics
- Follow all guidelines in this policy

**Recommended Disclaimer:**
"Opinions expressed are my own and do not represent the views of my employer."

### 4.3 Work-Related Posts

When posting about work:

**Acceptable:**
- Sharing public company news and announcements
- Celebrating team achievements (with permission)
- Posting about industry conferences you attend
- Sharing your professional development
- Congratulating colleagues on promotions

**Not Acceptable:**
- Unannounced product information
- Financial information or forecasts
- Client names or project details without permission
- Internal discussions or decisions
- Criticism of company, management, or colleagues

---

## 5. Confidentiality

### 5.1 Protected Information

Never share on social media:
- Trade secrets and proprietary information
- Client information or relationships
- Financial data, forecasts, or non-public results
- Employee personal information
- Internal communications and emails
- Security protocols and vulnerabilities
- Legal matters and litigation
- Unreleased product information

### 5.2 Photography and Video

Before posting photos or videos from work:
- Get permission from people pictured
- Ensure no confidential information is visible
- Don't photograph secure areas
- Follow client confidentiality requirements

### 5.3 Intellectual Property

Respect intellectual property rights:
- Don't share copyrighted materials without permission
- Don't use company logos for personal accounts
- Credit sources appropriately
- Don't share others' content without attribution

---

## 6. Professional Conduct

### 6.1 Respectful Communication

All social media communications must be:
- Professional and respectful
- Free from harassment, bullying, or discrimination
- Honest and accurate
- Legal and ethical

### 6.2 Prohibited Content

Do not post content that is:
- Discriminatory or harassing
- Defamatory or libelous
- Threatening or violent
- Sexually explicit
- Promoting illegal activities
- Fraudulent or deceptive

### 6.3 Engaging with Others

When engaging in online discussions:
- Be respectful even when disagreeing
- Don't engage in personal attacks
- Walk away from heated arguments
- Report harassment to platform and HR

### 6.4 Political and Controversial Topics

Employees may express personal political views on their own social media accounts. However:
- Don't suggest your views represent Acme
- Don't use company resources for political activities
- Don't engage in discussions that could harm the company's reputation
- Consider whether your activity could affect professional relationships

---

## 7. Social Media at Work

### 7.1 Personal Use During Work Hours

Limited personal social media use is permitted during work hours if:
- It does not interfere with job responsibilities
- It does not violate any other company policy
- It is not excessive

Managers may restrict social media use if it affects productivity.

### 7.2 Using Company Devices

When using company devices for social media:
- Remember activity may be monitored
- Don't store personal passwords on work devices
- Keep personal and professional accounts separate

### 7.3 Wi-Fi and Network Use

Using company network for personal social media:
- Is permitted in moderation
- Must comply with Acceptable Use Policy
- May be monitored
- Should not consume excessive bandwidth

---

## 8. LinkedIn Guidelines

### 8.1 Profile Best Practices

Your LinkedIn profile represents you professionally:
- Keep profile current and accurate
- Use a professional photo
- Accurately describe your role and responsibilities
- Don't share confidential information in your job description

### 8.2 Connections and Networking

When networking on LinkedIn:
- Use discretion when connecting with competitors
- Don't solicit clients for personal ventures
- Be professional in messages and communications
- Consider confidentiality before sharing updates

### 8.3 Company Content

Sharing company content on LinkedIn:
- Share public announcements and news
- Comment thoughtfully on company posts
- Tag Acme Corporation appropriately
- Don't share internal-only content

## 9. Monitoring and Enforcement

### 9.1 Company Monitoring

Acme Corporation reserves the right to:
- Monitor social media for brand mentions
- Review public posts by employees
- Investigate complaints about employee social media use

### 9.2 Reporting Concerns

Report social media concerns to:
- Your manager
- HR: hr@acmecorp.com
- Corporate Communications: communications@acmecorp.com

### 9.3 Consequences

Violations of this policy may result in:
- Coaching and training
- Disciplinary action up to termination
- Legal action in serious cases
- Required removal of content

---

## 10. Legal Considerations

### 10.1 Defamation

Be careful about statements that could be defamatory:
- Verify facts before posting
- Distinguish opinion from fact
- Avoid making false statements about others

### 10.2 Endorsements and Disclosures

FTC guidelines require disclosure when:
- You receive compensation for posts
- You receive free products or services
- There's a material connection to a brand

Use clear disclosures: #ad, #sponsored, "Company provided this product"

### 10.3 Securities Laws

If you have access to material non-public information:
- Never share it on social media
- Don't hint at or tease announcements
- Contact Legal with questions

### 10.4 Protected Activity

This policy does not restrict:
- Protected concerted activity under the NLRA
- Whistleblower communications
- Legal rights to discuss working conditions
- Filing complaints with government agencies

## 11. Social Media for Specific Roles

### 11.1 Executives and Leaders

Senior leaders have additional responsibilities:
- Your posts carry more weight
- Coordinate with Corporate Communications
- Be especially careful about financial and strategic topics
- Model appropriate social media behavior

### 11.2 Recruiters and HR

When using social media for recruiting:
- Follow EEO guidelines
- Don't make discriminatory judgments based on profiles
- Protect candidate privacy
- Use approved recruiting messages

### 11.3 Sales and Client-Facing Roles

Additional guidelines for sales:
- Don't discuss specific deals or clients
- Get approval for testimonials and case studies
- Don't disparage competitors
- Follow client social media policies

### 11.4 Engineers and Technical Staff

Technical employees should:
- Not share proprietary technical details
- Get approval for technical blogs or posts
- Follow open source policies
- Protect security vulnerabilities

---

## 12. Social Media Emergencies

### 12.1 Negative Publicity

If you see negative posts about Acme:
- Don't respond without authorization
- Report to Corporate Communications
- Don't engage in arguments
- Let the official response handle it

### 12.2 Mistakes

If you make a social media mistake:
- Don't try to cover it up
- Contact your manager and Communications
- Follow guidance on correction or deletion
- Learn from the experience

### 12.3 Account Compromise

If your social media account is compromised:
- Secure your account immediately
- Report to IT Security if work-related
- Review recent activity
- Alert followers if necessary
