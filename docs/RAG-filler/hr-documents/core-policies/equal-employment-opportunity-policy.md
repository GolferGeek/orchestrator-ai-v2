# Acme Corporation Equal Employment Opportunity Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2020
**Last Revised:** November 22, 2025
**Version:** 3.0
**Document Owner:** Human Resources Department
**Approved By:** Sarah Mitchell, Chief Human Resources Officer

---

## 1. Policy Statement

Acme Corporation is an equal opportunity employer. We are committed to creating a diverse and inclusive workplace where all individuals are treated with dignity and respect. We prohibit discrimination and harassment against any employee or applicant based on protected characteristics.

Employment decisions at Acme Corporation are based solely on job-related qualifications, merit, and business needs. We actively work to ensure that all employment practices are free from unlawful discrimination.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all aspects of employment, including:
- Recruitment and hiring
- Job assignments and placement
- Compensation and benefits
- Training and development
- Performance management
- Promotions and transfers
- Discipline and termination
- All other terms and conditions of employment

This policy covers all employees, applicants, contractors, vendors, and visitors.

---

## 3. Protected Characteristics

Acme Corporation prohibits discrimination based on:

- **Race** - Including ancestry and ethnic characteristics
- **Color** - Skin color or complexion
- **Religion** - Religious beliefs, practices, observances, or lack thereof
- **Sex** - Including pregnancy, childbirth, lactation, and related conditions
- **National Origin** - Country of origin, ancestry, native language, or accent
- **Age** - 40 years of age or older under federal law
- **Disability** - Physical or mental impairments that substantially limit major life activities
- **Genetic Information** - Family medical history, genetic tests, or genetic services
- **Veteran Status** - Military service, including disabled veterans and Vietnam-era veterans
- **Sexual Orientation** - Heterosexual, homosexual, bisexual, or other orientations
- **Gender Identity** - Gender identity or expression, including transgender status
- **Marital Status** - Single, married, divorced, separated, or widowed
- **Citizenship Status** - Within the limits permitted by law
- **Political Affiliation** - Political beliefs or activities
- **Any Other Protected Characteristic** - Under applicable federal, state, or local law

## 4. Employment Practices

### 4.1 Recruitment and Hiring

We recruit and hire based on qualifications, skills, and experience relevant to the position. Our practices include:

- Writing job descriptions focused on essential functions and qualifications
- Posting positions broadly to reach diverse candidate pools
- Using structured interviews with job-related questions
- Making hiring decisions based on merit and qualifications
- Training hiring managers on bias-free selection
- Documenting hiring decisions and rationale

### 4.2 Compensation

We provide equal pay for equal work. Our compensation practices:

- Base pay on job responsibilities, skills, experience, and performance
- Conduct regular pay equity analyses
- Prohibit pay decisions based on protected characteristics
- Use consistent criteria for raises, bonuses, and incentives
- Ensure transparency in our compensation philosophy

### 4.3 Promotions and Career Development

We provide equal opportunities for advancement. Our practices include:

- Posting internal opportunities to all eligible employees
- Using objective criteria for promotion decisions
- Providing development opportunities to employees at all levels
- Tracking promotion rates across demographic groups
- Addressing any identified disparities

### 4.4 Performance Management

Performance is evaluated fairly and consistently:

- Using job-related, objective criteria
- Providing regular feedback to all employees
- Training managers on bias-free evaluations
- Ensuring consistency across the organization
- Documenting performance issues and expectations

### 4.5 Training and Development

We provide equal access to training and development:

- Making training available to all employees in relevant roles
- Tracking participation across demographic groups
- Addressing barriers to participation
- Investing in diverse leadership development

### 4.6 Discipline and Termination

Discipline is administered fairly and consistently:

- Using progressive discipline except for serious violations
- Applying consistent standards across the organization
- Documenting performance issues and expectations
- Providing employees opportunity to improve
- Basing decisions on job-related factors

## 5. Reasonable Accommodations

### 5.1 Disability Accommodations

Acme Corporation provides reasonable accommodations to qualified individuals with disabilities, unless doing so would impose an undue hardship. Accommodations may include:

- Modified work schedules
- Accessible workspaces and equipment
- Job restructuring
- Modified policies or procedures
- Assistive technology
- Leave for medical treatment
- Reassignment to vacant positions

To request an accommodation, contact HR at hr@acmecorp.com or ext. 4000. HR will engage in an interactive process to identify effective accommodations.

### 5.2 Religious Accommodations

We accommodate sincerely held religious beliefs and practices unless doing so would impose an undue hardship. Accommodations may include:

- Flexible scheduling for religious observances
- Modifications to dress code policies
- Time and space for prayer
- Dietary accommodations
- Holiday schedule adjustments

To request a religious accommodation, contact HR.

### 5.3 Pregnancy Accommodations

We provide reasonable accommodations for pregnancy, childbirth, and related medical conditions, including:

- Modified duties or schedules
- More frequent breaks
- Modified attendance policies
- Temporary transfers to less strenuous positions
- Leave for prenatal care and recovery
- Lactation rooms and break time for expressing milk

## 6. Affirmative Action

### 6.1 Commitment to Diversity

Acme Corporation is committed to affirmative action to ensure equal opportunity. We take proactive steps to:

- Recruit from diverse sources
- Partner with organizations serving underrepresented groups
- Participate in career fairs at diverse colleges and universities
- Mentor and develop diverse talent
- Track and improve diversity metrics
- Hold leaders accountable for diversity goals

### 6.2 Federal Contractor Requirements

As a federal contractor, Acme Corporation complies with:

- **Executive Order 11246** - Prohibiting discrimination and requiring affirmative action
- **Section 503 of the Rehabilitation Act** - Requiring affirmative action for individuals with disabilities
- **Vietnam Era Veterans' Readjustment Assistance Act (VEVRAA)** - Requiring affirmative action for protected veterans

Our Affirmative Action Plan is available for review by contacting HR.

### 6.3 Annual Goals

We establish annual diversity goals and track progress. Current focus areas include:

- Increasing representation of women in technical roles
- Improving representation of underrepresented minorities in leadership
- Meeting federal utilization goals for individuals with disabilities (7%)
- Meeting federal hiring benchmarks for protected veterans (5.2%)

## 8. Reporting and Complaint Procedures

### 8.1 How to Report

Employees who believe they have experienced or witnessed discrimination should report it to:

- Their supervisor (unless the supervisor is involved)
- Human Resources: hr@acmecorp.com, ext. 4000
- Jennifer Williams, Director of Employee Relations: ext. 4010
- Ethics Hotline: 1-800-555-ETHIC (anonymous reporting available)
- Online: ethics.acmecorp.com

### 8.2 Investigation

All complaints are investigated promptly, thoroughly, and impartially. Investigations typically include:

- Interview with the complainant
- Review of relevant documents
- Interview with the accused
- Interviews with witnesses
- Analysis of findings
- Determination and recommendations

### 8.3 Corrective Action

If discrimination is found, appropriate corrective action will be taken, up to and including termination. We also take steps to prevent recurrence.

### 8.4 Confidentiality

Complaints are kept confidential to the extent possible while conducting a thorough investigation.

## 9. Non-Retaliation

### 9.1 Protection

Acme Corporation prohibits retaliation against anyone who:

- Reports discrimination in good faith
- Participates in an investigation or proceeding
- Opposes discriminatory practices
- Requests an accommodation

### 9.2 Reporting Retaliation

Anyone who experiences retaliation should report it immediately using the same channels available for discrimination complaints.

**U.S. Department of Labor - Office of Federal Contract Compliance Programs (OFCCP)**
- Website: www.dol.gov/ofccp
- Phone: 1-800-397-6251
- Filing deadline: 180 days for federal contractors
