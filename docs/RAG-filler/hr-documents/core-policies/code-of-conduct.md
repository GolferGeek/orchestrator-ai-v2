This Code of Conduct outlines the principles and standards that guide our behavior. It applies to everyone at Acme Corporationâ€”from our Board of Directors to our newest team members. No matter your role, you are responsible for upholding these standards every day.

## Section 1: Our Core Values

### 1.1 Innovation
We embrace creative thinking and challenge the status quo. We encourage experimentation and learn from both successes and failures.

### 1.2 Integrity
We are honest and transparent in all our dealings. We keep our promises and take responsibility for our actions.

### 1.3 Collaboration
We work together across teams, departments, and geographies. We share knowledge and support each other's success.

### 1.4 Excellence
We set high standards and strive to exceed them. We take pride in delivering quality work.

### 1.5 Respect
We treat everyone with dignity. We value diverse perspectives and create an inclusive environment where all can thrive.

## Section 2: Workplace Conduct

### 2.1 Professional Behavior

All employees are expected to:
- Treat colleagues, clients, vendors, and visitors with courtesy and respect
- Communicate honestly and professionally
- Meet commitments and deadlines
- Take responsibility for mistakes and work to correct them
- Support company decisions even when you disagree

### 2.2 Harassment-Free Workplace

Acme Corporation prohibits all forms of harassment, including but not limited to:

**Sexual Harassment:**
- Unwelcome sexual advances or requests for sexual favors
- Offensive comments about a person's sex or gender
- Displaying sexually explicit materials
- Unwanted physical contact

**Other Harassment:**
- Slurs, jokes, or derogatory comments based on protected characteristics
- Intimidation or bullying behavior
- Threats of violence
- Exclusion or isolation based on protected characteristics

Any employee who experiences or witnesses harassment should report it immediately to their manager, HR, or the Ethics Hotline.

### 2.3 Diversity and Inclusion

We believe diverse teams produce better results. We are committed to:
- Recruiting and retaining employees from all backgrounds
- Providing equal opportunities for advancement
- Creating an environment where all voices are heard
- Celebrating our differences while working toward common goals

### 2.4 Substance Abuse

Employees must report to work fit for duty. The following are prohibited:
- Being under the influence of alcohol or illegal drugs while working
- Using, possessing, or distributing illegal drugs on company property
- Misusing prescription or over-the-counter medications

Employees taking prescription medications that may affect their ability to work safely must notify HR. Acme Corporation provides an Employee Assistance Program (EAP) for those struggling with substance abuse issues.

### 2.5 Workplace Violence

We have zero tolerance for violence or threats of violence. This includes:
- Physical assault or battery
- Verbal or written threats
- Intimidating or aggressive behavior
- Possession of weapons on company property

All threats should be taken seriously and reported immediately to Security (extension 5555) or the Ethics Hotline.

## Section 3: Conflicts of Interest

### 3.1 General Principles

A conflict of interest occurs when your personal interests interfere with your ability to act in Acme Corporation's best interests. Employees must:
- Avoid situations where personal interests conflict with company interests
- Disclose any potential conflicts to their manager and HR
- Recuse themselves from decisions where they have a conflict

### 3.2 Outside Employment

Employees may hold outside jobs provided they:
- Do not work for competitors
- Do not allow outside work to interfere with Acme responsibilities
- Do not use company time, resources, or information for outside work
- Obtain written approval from their manager and HR

### 3.3 Personal Relationships

Employees must disclose relationships with:
- Vendors, suppliers, or contractors doing business with Acme
- Competitors
- Government officials with oversight over Acme
- Other employees in a direct reporting relationship

### 3.4 Financial Interests

Employees must disclose ownership interests greater than 1% in:
- Companies doing business with Acme
- Competitors
- Companies that may seek to do business with Acme

Board members and executives must disclose all material financial interests annually.

### 3.5 Corporate Opportunities

Employees may not:
- Take for themselves business opportunities discovered through their position
- Use company property, information, or position for personal gain
- Compete with Acme Corporation while employed

## Section 4: Protection of Company Assets

### 4.1 Physical Assets

Company equipment, supplies, and facilities are for business use. Employees must:
- Use company property responsibly
- Report lost, damaged, or stolen property immediately
- Return all property upon separation from employment
- Not remove property from premises without authorization

### 4.2 Intellectual Property

All inventions, ideas, and work products created during employment belong to Acme Corporation. Employees must:
- Protect patents, trademarks, copyrights, and trade secrets
- Not disclose proprietary information without authorization
- Execute any documents needed to perfect the company's IP rights
- Respect the intellectual property of others

### 4.3 Confidential Information

Confidential information includes:
- Client data and contracts
- Financial information
- Business strategies and plans
- Employee personal information
- Technical specifications and source code
- Vendor pricing and terms

Confidential information must be:
- Shared only with those who need it for legitimate business purposes
- Protected from unauthorized access (physical and electronic)
- Not discussed in public places or on unsecured communications
- Properly disposed of when no longer needed

### 4.4 Data Privacy

We respect the privacy of our employees, clients, and partners. Employees must:
- Collect only necessary personal information
- Use personal information only for legitimate business purposes
- Protect personal information from unauthorized access
- Comply with all applicable privacy laws and regulations
- Report any suspected data breaches immediately to IT Security

### 4.5 Records Management

Business records must be:
- Accurate and complete
- Retained according to the Records Retention Policy
- Never destroyed to obstruct investigations
- Properly disposed of when retention periods expire

## Section 5: Fair Dealing

### 5.1 Anti-Corruption and Bribery

Acme Corporation prohibits bribery and corruption in all forms. Employees must not:
- Offer, promise, or give anything of value to improperly influence decisions
- Accept bribes or kickbacks
- Make facilitation payments
- Use third parties to circumvent anti-corruption rules

This applies to dealings with government officials, clients, vendors, and any other parties.

### 5.2 Gifts and Entertainment

**Giving Gifts:**
- Business gifts must be reasonable, appropriate, and lawful
- Gifts to government officials require Legal approval
- Cash or cash equivalents are never appropriate
- Maximum gift value: $100 per recipient per year

**Receiving Gifts:**
- Employees may accept modest gifts and entertainment
- Gifts exceeding $100 in value must be reported to HR
- Gifts that could influence business decisions must be declined
- Cash or cash equivalents must always be declined

**Entertainment:**
- Business meals and entertainment must have a legitimate business purpose
- Host should be present at the event
- Expenses must be accurately recorded

### 5.3 Fair Competition

We compete vigorously but fairly. Employees must not:
- Make false or misleading statements about competitors
- Misappropriate competitors' confidential information
- Engage in price-fixing, bid-rigging, or market allocation
- Enter into agreements that unreasonably restrain trade

### 5.4 Accurate Marketing

All marketing and advertising must be:
- Truthful and not misleading
- Substantiated by evidence
- Compliant with applicable laws and regulations
- Respectful of competitors

## Section 6: Financial Integrity

### 6.1 Accurate Books and Records

All financial records must accurately reflect transactions. Employees must:
- Never create false or misleading entries
- Record transactions in the proper period
- Maintain supporting documentation
- Follow internal controls and approval processes

### 6.2 Expense Reporting

Business expenses must be:
- Legitimate and necessary
- Properly documented with receipts
- Submitted timely (within 30 days)
- Approved by appropriate managers

Submitting false or inflated expense reports is fraud and grounds for termination.

### 6.3 Insider Trading

Employees with access to material non-public information about Acme Corporation or other companies must not:
- Buy or sell securities based on that information
- Share that information with others who might trade
- Recommend securities transactions to others

Violations of insider trading laws can result in severe civil and criminal penalties.

### 6.4 External Reporting

Information provided to external parties, including financial statements, regulatory filings, and press releases, must be:
- Accurate and complete
- Not misleading
- Approved through proper channels
- Compliant with applicable laws and regulations

## Section 7: Government and Community Relations

### 7.1 Government Contracts

Employees working on government contracts must:
- Comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and contract terms
- Ensure accurate certifications and representations
- Protect classified and sensitive information
- Report potential violations through appropriate channels

### 7.2 Political Activities

Acme Corporation encourages civic participation. However:
- Personal political activities must be on your own time and resources
- You may not represent that your political views are those of Acme
- Corporate political contributions require CEO and Legal approval
- Lobbying activities require Legal approval

### 7.3 Working with Government Officials

All interactions with government officials must be:
- Truthful and accurate
- Properly documented
- Approved by Legal when involving contracts or regulations
- Free from improper gifts or payments

### 7.4 Community Involvement

Acme Corporation supports community involvement through:
- Volunteer time off (16 hours per year)
- Matching charitable contributions (up to $1,000 per year)
- Corporate philanthropy programs
- Pro bono services

## Section 8: Reporting and Accountability

### 8.1 Duty to Report

All employees have a duty to report known or suspected violations of:
- This Code of Conduct
- Company policies
- Laws or regulations

Reports may be made to:
- Your manager
- Human Resources
- Legal & Compliance
- Ethics Hotline: 1-800-555-ETHIC (anonymous reporting available)
- ethics@acmecorp.com

### 8.2 Non-Retaliation

Acme Corporation prohibits retaliation against anyone who:
- Reports a concern in good faith
- Participates in an investigation
- Refuses to participate in wrongdoing

Retaliation is a serious violation and will result in disciplinary action.

### 8.3 Investigation Process

All reports are taken seriously and investigated promptly. Investigations are:
- Conducted fairly and objectively
- Kept confidential to the extent possible
- Documented appropriately
- Concluded with appropriate action

### 8.4 Consequences of Violations

Violations of this Code may result in:
- Verbal or written warning
- Performance improvement plan
- Demotion or reassignment
- Suspension
- Termination
- Legal action

The severity of consequences depends on the nature and circumstances of the violation.

## Section 9: Waivers and Amendments

### 9.1 Waivers

Waivers of this Code for employees require approval from the Chief Human Resources Officer and General Counsel. Waivers for executive officers and directors require Board approval and will be disclosed as required by law.

### 9.2 Amendments

This Code may be amended at any time by the Board of Directors. Material changes will be communicated to all employees.
