# Client Confidentiality Policy

**Smith & Associates, PLLC**
**Effective Date: January 1, 2024**
**Last Updated: January 1, 2024**

## Purpose

This policy establishes the firm's standards for protecting client confidential information in accordance with our ethical obligations under the Rules of Professional Conduct.

## Scope

This policy applies to all firm personnel, including:
- Attorneys (partners, associates, of counsel)
- Paralegals and legal assistants
- Administrative staff
- IT personnel
- Contractors and temporary employees
- Interns and law clerks

## Definition of Confidential Information

### What is Protected

Client confidential information includes ALL information relating to the representation of a client, regardless of its source:

- Information provided directly by the client
- Information obtained from third parties about the client's matter
- Attorney work product
- Legal strategies and opinions
- Settlement discussions
- Information learned during investigations
- Documents provided by or about the client
- The fact that someone is a client (in some circumstances)
- Billing records and financial information

### Duration of Protection

Confidentiality obligations:
- Begin when a prospective client first contacts the firm
- Continue indefinitely, even after representation ends
- Survive the client's death
- Cannot be waived by the firm (only by the client or their authorized representative)

## Core Obligations

### General Duty

All personnel must:

1. **Protect** confidential information from unauthorized disclosure
2. **Limit** access to those with a legitimate need to know
3. **Never** discuss client matters in public places
4. **Secure** all documents and electronic files
5. **Report** any suspected breaches immediately

### Physical Security

- Keep client files in locked cabinets when not in use
- Do not leave files visible on desks when away
- Shred documents containing confidential information
- Use privacy screens on monitors in open areas
- Ensure conversations cannot be overheard by unauthorized persons
- Lock office doors when leaving

### Electronic Security

- Use strong passwords (minimum 12 characters)
- Enable two-factor authentication
- Encrypt emails containing confidential information
- Do not use public Wi-Fi for client work without VPN
- Lock computer screens when stepping away
- Never save client files on personal devices
- Use approved cloud storage only (firm-approved systems)
- Report lost or stolen devices immediately

### Communication Security

**Email:**
- Use encryption for sensitive communications
- Verify recipient email addresses before sending
- Include confidentiality disclaimer in signature
- Do not send confidential information to personal email addresses

**Phone/Video:**
- Verify caller identity before discussing matters
- Do not discuss client matters on speakerphone in open areas
- Use private rooms for client calls
- Be aware of surroundings during video calls

**Fax:**
- Verify fax number before sending
- Use cover sheet with confidentiality notice
- Retrieve faxes promptly
- Report misdirected faxes immediately

**Mail:**
- Mark confidential documents appropriately
- Use certified mail for sensitive documents
- Verify mailing addresses

## Working Remotely

### Home Office Requirements

- Work in private space away from family/roommates
- Use firm-provided or approved devices
- Connect via secure VPN
- Do not print confidential documents unless necessary
- Shred home-printed documents properly
- Secure physical files brought home
- Return all files to office promptly

### Public Spaces

- Never work on client matters in public spaces where screens can be viewed
- Do not discuss client matters on phone in public
- Use privacy screens on laptops
- Be aware of surroundings at all times
- Avoid working on confidential matters in airports, coffee shops, etc.

## Social Media and Public Communications

### Prohibited Activities

- Posting about client matters (even anonymously or vaguely)
- Responding to negative reviews with confidential information
- Sharing photos that reveal client information
- Discussing cases on social media platforms
- Posting courtroom or deposition photos

### Permitted Activities

- General legal education content (not case-specific)
- Firm news and announcements (not identifying clients without consent)
- Professional networking (without disclosing confidential information)

## Exceptions to Confidentiality

### Permitted Disclosures

Disclosure is permitted when:

1. **Client consent** – Express or implied authorization
2. **Legal compliance** – Required by law or court order
3. **Prevent harm** – To prevent reasonably certain death or substantial bodily harm
4. **Legal malpractice defense** – To establish a claim or defense in proceedings involving the client
5. **Fee disputes** – To secure payment of fees (limited disclosure)
6. **Conflict checks** – Limited information for conflict checking

### Process for Required Disclosures

If disclosure may be legally required:
1. Notify supervising attorney immediately
2. Attempt to limit scope of disclosure
3. Seek protective order if appropriate
4. Notify client before disclosure (unless prohibited)
5. Document the disclosure

## Inadvertent Disclosure

### Receiving Inadvertently Disclosed Information

If you receive information that appears to have been inadvertently disclosed:
1. Stop reviewing immediately
2. Notify the sending party promptly
3. Follow sending party's instructions
4. Do not use the information
5. Notify supervising attorney

### Making Inadvertent Disclosures

If you inadvertently disclose confidential information:
1. Notify supervising attorney immediately
2. Contact the recipient and request return/destruction
3. Document the incident
4. Notify affected client
5. Take steps to prevent future occurrences

## Former Employees

### Departure Procedures

Upon departure, all personnel must:
- Return all firm and client materials
- Delete client data from personal devices
- Confirm no confidential files remain in possession
- Sign departure acknowledgment
- Continue to honor confidentiality obligations

### Reference Checks

When providing references, do not disclose:
- Specific clients worked for
- Specific matters handled
- Confidential information about the firm

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

All personnel must report suspected violations to:
- Supervising attorney
- Managing Partner
- General Counsel
- Anonymous ethics hotline: ethics@smithassociates.law

### Consequences

Violations of this policy may result in:
- Disciplinary action up to and including termination
- Bar discipline for attorneys
- Civil liability
- Criminal penalties in severe cases

### Non-Retaliation

The firm prohibits retaliation against anyone who reports a suspected violation in good faith.

## Training Requirements

- All new personnel: Confidentiality training within first week
- All personnel: Annual refresher training
- Attorneys: Ethics CLE on confidentiality every 2 years

## Acknowledgment

All personnel must sign an acknowledgment of this policy upon hire and annually thereafter.

## Quick Reference: Common Situations

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Friend asks what case you're working on | Decline to discuss |
| Opposing counsel calls about a matter | Verify identity, then discuss only non-confidential procedural matters |
| Court orders production of privileged documents | Object, seek protective order, notify client |
| Client posts negative online review | Do not respond with confidential information |
| Journalist inquires about case | Refer to Marketing; do not comment |
| Family member works for opposing party | Immediately disclose to supervising attorney |
| Found confidential documents in printer | Return to proper owner; report if can't identify |
| Lost laptop containing client files | Report immediately to IT and managing partner |
