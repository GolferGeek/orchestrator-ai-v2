# Conflict of Interest Policy

**Smith & Associates, PLLC**
**Effective Date: January 1, 2024**
**Last Updated: January 1, 2024**

## Purpose

This policy establishes procedures for identifying, evaluating, and resolving conflicts of interest in accordance with the Rules of Professional Conduct.

## Scope

This policy applies to all attorneys, paralegals, legal assistants, and staff members of the firm.

## Definitions

### Conflict of Interest

A conflict exists when:
1. Representation of one client is directly adverse to another client
2. There is significant risk that representation will be materially limited by responsibilities to another client, former client, third person, or personal interests

### Current Client

Any individual or entity for whom the firm is currently providing legal services or has an open matter.

### Former Client

Any individual or entity for whom the firm previously provided legal services and whose matter is now closed.

### Prospective Client

Any individual or entity who has consulted with the firm about possible representation, even if not retained.

## Conflict Check Procedures

### New Matter Opening

Before opening any new matter:

1. **Intake Coordinator** enters all parties into conflict database:
   - Client name (all variations/aliases)
   - Adverse parties
   - Related entities
   - Key witnesses
   - Insurance carriers

2. **System Search** runs against:
   - Current clients
   - Former clients
   - Prospective clients (last 3 years)
   - Adverse parties in existing matters

3. **Results Review** by designated conflicts attorney within 24 hours

4. **Clearance or Escalation**:
   - No conflicts: Matter may proceed
   - Potential conflict: Escalate to Conflicts Committee

### Ongoing Monitoring

- Conflicts database updated when new parties added to matters
- Quarterly audit of open matters for emerging conflicts
- Immediate report required if conflict discovered during representation

## Conflict Categories

### Absolute Conflicts (Cannot Be Waived)

- Representation of opposing parties in same litigation
- Matters where attorney's interests directly conflict with client
- Situations prohibited by court rules or law

### Waivable Conflicts

With informed consent, confirmed in writing, the firm may proceed when:
- Lawyer reasonably believes competent representation possible
- Representation not prohibited by law
- Clients not directly opposing each other in same proceeding

### Potential Conflicts

Situations requiring evaluation but not necessarily declining representation:
- Former client matters (substantially related test)
- Business transactions with clients
- Family relationships between attorneys and opposing parties

## Waiver Process

### Requirements for Valid Waiver

1. Full disclosure of conflict to all affected clients
2. Explanation of risks and alternatives
3. Reasonable belief representation can be competent
4. Written consent from all affected parties
5. Independent counsel recommendation when appropriate

### Waiver Documentation

All waivers must include:
- Description of the conflict
- Parties involved
- Risks of proceeding
- Alternative options
- Limitations on representation
- Client signatures and dates

### Waiver Retention

Waivers maintained in:
- Client matter file
- Central conflicts file
- Conflicts database notation

## Screening Procedures

### Lateral Hire Screening

When attorney joins from firm representing adverse party:
1. Identify conflicted matters
2. Implement ethical wall procedures
3. Written acknowledgment from screened attorney
4. Notice to affected clients
5. No fee sharing from conflicted matters

### Ethical Wall Requirements

- Physical separation when possible
- Restricted file access (physical and electronic)
- Prohibition on discussion of matter
- Quarterly compliance certification
- Documented procedures

## Confidential Information

### Protection of Former Client Information

- Confidential information from former clients protected indefinitely
- No use of former client confidences against them
- Screening required when substantially related matters

### Prospective Client Information

- Consultation information protected even if not retained
- Limit information obtained during consultation
- Document declination and information received

## Conflicts Committee

### Composition

- Managing Partner (Chair)
- Two senior partners (rotating annually)
- General Counsel

### Responsibilities

- Review escalated conflict determinations
- Approve or deny waiver requests
- Establish screening procedures
- Update conflicts policy
- Training on conflicts issues

### Meeting Schedule

- Weekly during business hours
- Emergency meetings within 24 hours when needed

## Training Requirements

- All attorneys: Annual conflicts training (2 hours CLE)
- New hires: Conflicts orientation within first week
- Staff: Annual training on intake procedures

## Consequences of Violation

Failure to follow conflicts procedures may result in:
- Disciplinary action up to termination
- Malpractice liability
- Bar disciplinary proceedings
- Disqualification from matters

## Reporting

All potential conflicts must be reported immediately to:
- Supervising attorney
- Conflicts Committee Chair
- General Counsel

Good faith reporting is protected; retaliation is prohibited.

---

*This policy supplements, but does not replace, each attorney's individual obligation to identify and address conflicts of interest.*
