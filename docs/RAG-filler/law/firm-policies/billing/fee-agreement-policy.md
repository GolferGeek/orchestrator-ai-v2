## Article I: Purpose and Scope

### Section 1.1: Policy Purpose

This policy establishes comprehensive standards governing all aspects of fee agreements, billing practices, client financial arrangements, and trust account management at Smith & Associates, PLLC. The policy ensures compliance with applicable rules of professional conduct while maintaining transparent, fair, and consistent financial practices across all firm matters.

### Section 1.2: Policy Objectives

#### 1.2.1 Primary Objectives

The Fee Agreement Policy is designed to achieve the following objectives:

1. **Regulatory Compliance**: Ensure all fee arrangements comply with applicable state bar rules, including Rule 1.5 (Fees) and Rule 1.15 (Safekeeping Property)
2. **Client Protection**: Provide clear, understandable fee disclosures that protect client interests
3. **Firm Sustainability**: Maintain fee structures that support firm operations while remaining competitive
4. **Transparency**: Establish billing practices that are clear, detailed, and easily verifiable
5. **Consistency**: Apply uniform standards across all practice areas and attorney levels
6. **Accountability**: Create clear approval hierarchies and oversight mechanisms

#### 1.2.2 Secondary Objectives

1. Minimize fee disputes and client complaints
2. Streamline billing administration and reduce overhead
3. Support accurate financial forecasting and reporting
4. Facilitate timely collections and healthy cash flow
5. Maintain positive client relationships through fair practices

### Section 1.3: Applicability

#### 1.3.1 Covered Personnel

This policy applies to:
- All partners (equity and non-equity)
- All associates (senior and junior)
- Of counsel attorneys
- Contract attorneys
- Paralegals and legal assistants
- Billing and finance staff
- Administrative personnel involved in client billing

#### 1.3.2 Covered Matters

This policy governs:
- All new client engagements
- Existing client matters with fee modifications
- Pro bono matters (for documentation requirements)
- Referral arrangements
- Co-counsel fee sharing arrangements

### Section 2.2: Contingency Fee Arrangements

#### 2.2.1 Permitted Contingency Matters

Contingency fee arrangements are authorized for the following matter types:

**Strongly Encouraged:**
- Personal injury claims (automobile, premises liability, product liability)
- Medical malpractice claims
- Employment discrimination and harassment claims
- Wage and hour violations
- Consumer protection and fraud claims
- Insurance bad faith claims

**Permitted with Partner Approval:**
- Commercial collection matters (minimum claim: $50,000)
- Breach of contract claims (minimum claim: $100,000)
- Business tort claims
- Construction defect claims
- Professional malpractice claims (non-legal)

**Prohibited:**
- Criminal defense matters
- Domestic relations matters (divorce, custody, support)
- Immigration matters
- Probate and estate administration
- Real estate transactions
- Corporate formation and governance

#### 2.2.2 Standard Contingency Fee Schedule

| Stage of Resolution | Standard Rate | Complex Case Rate |
|--------------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Pre-litigation settlement | 33.33% | 35% |
| Post-filing, pre-discovery settlement | 37.5% | 40% |
| Post-discovery, pre-trial settlement | 40% | 42.5% |
| During trial | 42.5% | 45% |
| Post-verdict / Appeal | 45% | 50% |

#### 2.2.3 Contingency Fee Calculation

**Gross Recovery Method** (Standard):
Fee calculated on total recovery before deduction of costs and expenses.

**Net Recovery Method** (Client Option):
Fee calculated after deduction of costs and expenses. When net method is used, contingency percentage may be increased by 5%.

**Example Calculation - Gross Method:**
- Settlement Amount: $100,000
- Contingency Fee (33.33%): $33,330
- Case Expenses: $5,000
- Client Net Recovery: $61,670

**Example Calculation - Net Method (with 5% increase):**
- Settlement Amount: $100,000
- Case Expenses: $5,000
- Net Amount: $95,000
- Contingency Fee (38.33%): $36,413.50
- Client Net Recovery: $58,586.50

#### 2.2.4 Contingency Fee Caps

In accordance with court rules and ethical guidelines:
- Medical malpractice: Maximum 33.33% (statutory cap)
- Workers' compensation: Maximum 20% (statutory cap)
- Social Security disability: Maximum 25% or $7,200 (federal cap)
- Structured settlements: Fee calculated on present value only
- Minor settlements: Subject to court approval; fee may be reduced

#### 2.2.5 Hybrid Contingency Arrangements

The firm offers hybrid arrangements combining reduced hourly rates with contingency components:

**Option A - Reduced Hourly + Success Fee:**
- 50% of standard hourly rate billed monthly
- 15% contingency on gross recovery at conclusion

**Option B - Capped Hourly + Contingency:**
- Standard hourly rate with $25,000 fee cap
- 20% contingency on recovery exceeding $100,000

**Option C - Deferred Hourly + Contingency:**
- Full hourly rate deferred until resolution
- 25% contingency on gross recovery
- Hourly fees deducted from contingency before client distribution

#### 2.3.2 Flat Fee Scope Limitations

All flat fee arrangements include specific scope limitations. The following are explicitly excluded from standard flat fees and billed separately:

1. **Litigation or contested proceedings** arising from the underlying matter

3. **Third-party negotiations** beyond routine document execution
4. **Additional drafts** beyond three (3) revision rounds
5. **Rush processing** (less than standard turnaround time)
6. **Travel** outside the metropolitan area
7. **Court appearances** not specified in the agreement
8. **Expert consultations** (accountants, appraisers, etc.)
9. **Extended research** for novel or complex issues

- **Three-payment option**: 40% at engagement, 30% at midpoint, 30% at completion

## Article III: Billing Practices

### Section 3.1: Time Entry Requirements

#### 3.1.1 Time Entry Standards

All billable time must be recorded in accordance with the following standards:

**Timing Requirements:**
- Time entries must be submitted within 24 hours of work performed
- End-of-day batch entry is discouraged but permitted
- Weekly time entry review required by supervising attorney
- Monthly time entry certification required from all timekeepers

**Minimum Increments:**
- Standard billing increment: 0.1 hours (6 minutes)
- Telephone calls: Minimum 0.1 hours
- Email review/response: Minimum 0.1 hours per substantive exchange
- Brief research inquiries: Minimum 0.2 hours
- Document review: Actual time rounded to nearest 0.1 hours

#### 3.1.2 Time Entry Descriptions

Time entries must include:

**Required Elements:**
1. Date of service
2. Timekeeper identification
3. Matter number
4. Task code (UTBMS compliant)
5. Activity code (UTBMS compliant)
6. Narrative description
7. Time in hours (to 0.1)

**Description Standards:**
- Descriptions must be client-facing appropriate
- Minimum 10 words for entries 0.3 hours or greater
- Specific document names when applicable
- Party names in multi-party matters
- Issue identification in research entries

**Prohibited Description Practices:**
- Single-word descriptions ("Research," "Review," "Drafting")
- Vague references ("Work on matter," "Attention to file")
- Block billing without task breakdown
- Duplicative entries by multiple timekeepers without explanation
- Abbreviations not defined in engagement letter

#### 3.1.3 Block Billing Prohibition

Block billing (combining multiple distinct tasks into a single time entry) is prohibited except:
- Truly integrated tasks performed continuously
- With detailed sub-descriptions for each component
- When total combined time is less than 0.5 hours

**Acceptable Block Entry Example:**
"Review and respond to opposing counsel's letter regarding discovery dispute (0.3); telephone conference with client regarding same (0.4); draft response letter (0.6) - Total: 1.3 hours"

#### 3.2.2 Invoice Content Requirements

All invoices must include:

**Header Information:**

- Invoice number and date

- Client name and billing address
- Invoice period (from/to dates)
- Payment terms and due date

**Fee Section:**
- Itemized time entries by date
- Timekeeper identification and rate
- Task and activity codes
- Subtotal for professional fees

**Expense Section:**
- Itemized expenses by date
- Expense category
- Description
- Amount
- Subtotal for expenses

**Summary Section:**
- Professional fees subtotal
- Expenses subtotal
- Applicable taxes
- Previous balance
- Payments received
- Total amount due

#### 3.2.3 Invoice Review and Approval

Before release, invoices must be reviewed for:
- Time entry accuracy and completeness
- Description clarity and appropriateness
- Rate accuracy
- Expense documentation
- Mathematical accuracy
- Compliance with any billing guidelines

**Approval Authority:**
- Matters under $5,000: Associate review
- Matters $5,000-$25,000: Partner review
- Matters over $25,000: Supervising partner review
- All new client first invoices: Partner review

### Section 3.3: Special Billing Arrangements

#### 3.3.1 Alternative Fee Arrangements

The firm offers the following alternative fee arrangements:

**Fixed Monthly Fee:**

**Fee Caps:**

## Article IV: Trust Account Management

### Section 4.1: IOLTA Account Requirements

#### 4.1.1 Deposit Requirements

All client funds must be deposited to the firm's IOLTA trust account, including:
- Retainers (advance fee payments)
- Settlement proceeds
- Escrow funds
- Client expense deposits
- Third-party funds held for client benefit

#### 4.1.2 Prohibited Deposits

The following may NOT be deposited to the trust account:
- Firm operating funds (except to cover bank fees)
- Earned fees (after billing and payment application)
- Personal funds of any attorney or staff member
- Funds not related to client representation

### Section 4.2: Trust Account Procedures

#### 4.2.1 Deposits

1. All trust deposits made within 24 hours of receipt
2. Deposit slip includes client name and matter number
3. Receipt issued to client within 3 business days
4. Entry recorded in trust accounting system same day

#### 4.2.2 Disbursements

1. Disbursements only for client-authorized purposes
2. Disbursement request form required for all withdrawals
3. Two-signature requirement for disbursements over $10,000
4. Supporting documentation attached to disbursement record
5. Client ledger updated immediately upon disbursement

#### 4.2.3 Monthly Reconciliation

Trust account reconciliation performed monthly:
1. Bank statement reconciliation (by 10th of month)
2. Individual client ledger reconciliation
3. Three-way reconciliation verification
4. Discrepancy investigation and resolution
5. Partner certification of reconciliation

### Section 4.3: Client Notification Requirements

Clients must be notified in writing:
- Upon initial retainer deposit (within 3 business days)
- When trust balance falls below 25% of original retainer
- When trust balance reaches zero
- Monthly trust account statement (upon request)
- Upon any disbursement from trust (within 5 business days)

2. Supervising partner review of matter

Referral fees paid to referring attorneys:
- Maximum 25% of fees earned

#### 8.2.2 Outgoing Referrals

When referring matters to other firms:
- Referral fee negotiated case-by-case

- Written agreement required
