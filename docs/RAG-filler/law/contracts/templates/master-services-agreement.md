## ARTICLE 1: DEFINITIONS AND INTERPRETATION

### Section 1.1: Definitions

For purposes of this Agreement, the following terms shall have the meanings set forth below:

**"Affiliate"** means, with respect to any Party, any entity that directly or indirectly controls, is controlled by, or is under common control with such Party. For purposes of this definition, "control" means the possession, directly or indirectly, of the power to direct or cause the direction of the management and policies of an entity, whether through ownership of voting securities, by contract, or otherwise.

**"Agreement"** means this Master Services Agreement together with all Exhibits, Schedules, and Statements of Work incorporated herein by reference.

**"Business Day"** means any day other than a Saturday, Sunday, or legal holiday in the state of [STATE].

**"Change Order"** means a written document signed by authorized representatives of both Parties that modifies an existing Statement of Work.

**"Client Data"** means all data, information, content, records, files, and materials provided by Client to Service Provider or generated by Service Provider on behalf of Client in the performance of Services.

**"Client Materials"** means any materials, equipment, software, systems, documentation, or other resources provided by Client to Service Provider for use in performing the Services.

**"Confidential Information"** has the meaning set forth in Section 7.1.

**"Deliverables"** means the tangible and intangible work product, including but not limited to reports, documents, software, designs, specifications, data, and other materials to be provided by Service Provider to Client under a Statement of Work.

**"Effective Date"** means the date first written above.

**"Fees"** means the compensation payable by Client to Service Provider for Services as specified in each Statement of Work.

**"Force Majeure Event"** has the meaning set forth in Section 12.1.

**"Intellectual Property Rights"** means all intellectual property rights throughout the world, including patents, copyrights, trademarks, trade secrets, know-how, and any applications therefor.

**"Key Personnel"** means the individuals identified in a Statement of Work as essential to performance of the Services.

**"Losses"** means all losses, damages, liabilities, deficiencies, claims, actions, judgments, settlements, interest, awards, penalties, fines, costs, or expenses of whatever kind, including reasonable attorneys' fees and the cost of enforcing any right to indemnification hereunder.

**"Project Manager"** means the individual designated by each Party to serve as the primary point of contact for day-to-day management of Services.

**"Services"** means the professional services to be performed by Service Provider as described in each Statement of Work.

**"Specifications"** means the functional, technical, and performance requirements for Deliverables as set forth in each Statement of Work.

**"Statement of Work"** or **"SOW"** means a written document executed by both Parties that describes specific Services to be performed, including scope, Deliverables, timeline, Fees, and other terms.

**"Subcontractor"** means any third party engaged by Service Provider to perform any portion of the Services.

**"Term"** has the meaning set forth in Section 3.1.

**"Work Product"** means all Deliverables, inventions, discoveries, improvements, developments, methods, processes, techniques, designs, software code, documentation, and other work created by Service Provider in the course of performing Services.

## ARTICLE 2: SERVICES AND STATEMENTS OF WORK

### Section 2.1: General Framework

#### 2.1.1 Master Agreement Structure

This Agreement establishes the general terms and conditions governing the relationship between the Parties. Specific Services shall be requested and performed pursuant to individual Statements of Work that incorporate and are subject to the terms of this Agreement.

#### 2.1.2 Statement of Work Requirements

Each Statement of Work shall include, at minimum:

1. **Project Title and SOW Number**: Unique identification for tracking purposes
2. **Scope of Services**: Detailed description of Services to be performed
3. **Deliverables**: Specific work product to be delivered
4. **Specifications**: Technical and functional requirements
5. **Acceptance Criteria**: Standards for determining conformity
6. **Timeline and Milestones**: Project schedule with key dates
7. **Fees and Payment Terms**: Compensation structure
8. **Key Personnel**: Individuals assigned to the project
9. **Client Responsibilities**: Client's obligations and contributions
10. **Assumptions and Dependencies**: Conditions underlying the SOW
11. **Signature Blocks**: Authorization by both Parties

#### 2.1.3 Incorporation by Reference

Each Statement of Work shall be deemed incorporated into and made a part of this Agreement. In the event of any conflict between this Agreement and a Statement of Work, the terms of the Statement of Work shall control with respect to the Services described therein, unless the Statement of Work expressly states otherwise.

### Section 2.2: Statement of Work Process

#### 2.2.1 SOW Initiation

Either Party may request Services by submitting a proposed Statement of Work to the other Party. The requesting Party shall provide sufficient detail to enable the other Party to evaluate the request and prepare a response.

#### 2.2.2 SOW Negotiation

The Parties shall negotiate the terms of each proposed Statement of Work in good faith. Neither Party shall be obligated to agree to any Statement of Work.

#### 2.2.3 SOW Execution

A Statement of Work shall become effective only upon execution by authorized representatives of both Parties. Each Statement of Work shall be numbered sequentially (e.g., SOW-001, SOW-002).

#### 2.2.4 Multiple Active SOWs

Multiple Statements of Work may be in effect simultaneously. Each Statement of Work shall be treated as a separate agreement for purposes of performance, payment, and termination, subject to the overarching terms of this Agreement.

### Section 2.3: Performance Standards

#### 2.3.1 Standard of Care

Service Provider shall perform all Services in a professional, workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards and practices. Service Provider represents that it has the skills, experience, and qualifications necessary to perform the Services.

#### 2.3.2 Compliance with Laws

Service Provider shall perform Services in compliance with all applicable federal, state, and local laws, regulations, and ordinances. Service Provider shall obtain and maintain all licenses, permits, and certifications required for performance of Services.

#### 2.3.3 Time of Performance

Time is of the essence with respect to all dates and time periods specified in each Statement of Work. Service Provider shall promptly notify Client of any anticipated delay and its expected duration.

#### 2.3.4 Non-Exclusivity

This Agreement does not create an exclusive relationship. Client may engage other service providers for similar services, and Service Provider may provide similar services to other clients, subject to confidentiality obligations.

### Section 2.4: Change Orders

#### 2.4.1 Change Request Process

Either Party may request changes to an active Statement of Work by submitting a written Change Request describing:

1. The nature of the proposed change
2. Reasons for the change
3. Impact on scope, timeline, and Fees
4. Any assumptions or dependencies

#### 2.4.2 Change Evaluation

Within five (5) Business Days of receiving a Change Request, Service Provider shall provide a written Change Order proposal including:

1. Updated scope description
2. Revised timeline and milestones
3. Fee adjustment (if any)
4. Impact on other active Statements of Work (if any)

#### 2.4.3 Change Order Approval

No change shall be effective until a Change Order is executed by authorized representatives of both Parties. Service Provider shall continue performing Services under the existing Statement of Work until a Change Order is approved.

## ARTICLE 3: TERM AND TERMINATION

### Section 3.1: Agreement Term

#### 3.1.1 Initial Term

This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for a period of [NUMBER] ([X]) years, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article 3 ("Initial Term").

#### 3.1.2 Renewal

Upon expiration of the Initial Term, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive one (1) year periods ("Renewal Terms") unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal at least ninety (90) days prior to expiration of the then-current term.

#### 3.1.3 Statement of Work Term

Each Statement of Work shall specify its own term, which may extend beyond expiration of this Agreement. The terms of this Agreement shall continue to govern any Statement of Work that remains in effect after Agreement expiration.

### Section 3.2: Termination Rights

#### 3.2.1 Termination for Convenience

Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any Statement of Work for convenience upon sixty (60) days' prior written notice to the other Party.

#### 3.2.2 Termination for Cause

Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any Statement of Work immediately upon written notice if:

(a) The other Party materially breaches this Agreement or a Statement of Work and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail;

(b) The other Party becomes insolvent, makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or becomes the subject of any bankruptcy, liquidation, or similar proceeding; or

(c) The other Party engages in fraud, gross negligence, or willful misconduct in connection with this Agreement.

#### 3.2.3 Termination of Individual SOWs

Termination of an individual Statement of Work shall not affect the validity of this Agreement or any other Statement of Work, unless otherwise specified in the termination notice.

### Section 3.3: Effect of Termination

#### 3.3.1 Cessation of Services

Upon termination or expiration:

(a) Service Provider shall cease performing Services, except as reasonably necessary for orderly wind-down;

(b) Service Provider shall deliver to Client all completed and in-progress Work Product;

(c) Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party's Confidential Information;

(d) Client shall pay all Fees earned through the termination date.

#### 3.3.2 Transition Assistance

Upon Client's request and at Client's expense, Service Provider shall provide reasonable transition assistance for a period of up to sixty (60) days following termination to facilitate transfer of Services to Client or another provider.

#### 3.3.3 Survival

The following provisions shall survive termination or expiration: Article 1 (Definitions), Article 5 (Intellectual Property), Article 6 (Compensation) as to amounts due, Article 7 (Confidentiality), Article 8 (Representations and Warranties) for applicable warranty periods, Article 9 (Indemnification), Article 10 (Limitation of Liability), Article 11 (Dispute Resolution), and any other provision that by its nature should survive.

#### 4.3.2 Delay Impact

If Client fails to fulfill its responsibilities and such failure delays performance, the timeline shall be extended accordingly, and Service Provider may be entitled to additional compensation for increased costs.

## ARTICLE 6: COMPENSATION

### Section 6.1: Fees and Rates

#### 6.1.1 Fee Structure

Fees for Services shall be as specified in each Statement of Work and may include:

(a) **Time and Materials**: Fees based on actual time expended at agreed hourly or daily rates, plus approved expenses;

(b) **Fixed Fee**: A predetermined fee for completion of specified Deliverables;

(c) **Milestone-Based**: Fees payable upon achievement of defined milestones;

(d) **Hybrid**: A combination of the above fee structures.

#### 6.1.2 Rate Schedule

Unless otherwise specified in a Statement of Work, the following standard rates shall apply:

| Role | Standard Rate | Discounted Rate |
|------|---------------|-----------------|
| Senior Consultant | $350/hour | $300/hour |
| Consultant | $250/hour | $215/hour |
| Associate Consultant | $175/hour | $150/hour |
| Technical Specialist | $300/hour | $260/hour |
| Project Manager | $275/hour | $235/hour |
| Administrative Support | $100/hour | $85/hour |

#### 6.1.3 Rate Adjustments

Rates may be adjusted annually upon thirty (30) days' prior written notice, effective for new Statements of Work. Rates for existing Statements of Work shall remain unchanged unless modified by Change Order.

### Section 6.2: Expenses

#### 6.2.1 Reimbursable Expenses

Client shall reimburse Service Provider for reasonable, pre-approved, out-of-pocket expenses incurred in performing Services, including:

(a) Travel (airfare at coach class, mileage at IRS rate, hotels at reasonable rates);
(b) Meals during travel (maximum $75 per day);
(c) Materials and supplies specifically requested by Client;
(d) Third-party fees and licenses;
(e) Shipping and courier charges.

#### 6.2.2 Expense Approval

Expenses exceeding $500 individually or $2,500 in aggregate for a Statement of Work require Client's prior written approval.

#### 6.2.3 Documentation

Service Provider shall provide itemized expense reports with supporting receipts.

### Section 6.3: Invoicing and Payment

#### 6.3.1 Invoice Timing

Service Provider shall submit invoices:

(a) Monthly for time and materials engagements, by the tenth (10th) day of the following month;
(b) Upon milestone completion for milestone-based engagements;
(c) As specified in the Statement of Work for fixed fee engagements.

#### 6.3.2 Invoice Content

Each invoice shall include:

1. Invoice number and date
2. Statement of Work reference
3. Billing period
4. Description of Services performed
5. Hours by role and rate (for T&M)
6. Expenses with supporting documentation
7. Total amount due
8. Payment instructions

#### 6.3.3 Payment Terms

Payment is due within thirty (30) days of invoice date. Late payments shall bear interest at the rate of one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is less.

#### 6.3.4 Disputed Invoices

Client shall notify Service Provider in writing of any disputed invoice amounts within fifteen (15) days of invoice date. Client shall pay undisputed amounts by the due date. Parties shall work in good faith to resolve disputes within thirty (30) days.

### Section 6.4: Taxes

#### 6.4.1 Tax Responsibility

Fees do not include sales, use, excise, value-added, or similar taxes. Client shall be responsible for all such taxes, excluding taxes based on Service Provider's income.

#### 6.4.2 Tax Exemption

If Client claims tax exemption, Client shall provide appropriate exemption certificates.

## ARTICLE 7: CONFIDENTIALITY

### Section 7.1: Confidential Information Definition

"Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by either Party ("Disclosing Party") to the other Party ("Receiving Party") that:

(a) Is marked as "confidential," "proprietary," or with similar designation;
(b) Is identified as confidential at the time of disclosure;
(c) A reasonable person would understand to be confidential given its nature and circumstances of disclosure.

Confidential Information includes, without limitation: business plans, financial information, customer data, technical data, trade secrets, software code, designs, specifications, and the terms of this Agreement.

### Section 7.2: Exclusions

Confidential Information does not include information that:

(a) Was publicly available at the time of disclosure through no fault of Receiving Party;
(b) Becomes publicly available after disclosure through no fault of Receiving Party;
(c) Was rightfully in Receiving Party's possession before disclosure;
(d) Is rightfully obtained from a third party without restriction;
(e) Is independently developed without use of Confidential Information.

### Section 7.3: Obligations

#### 7.3.1 Protection Standard

Receiving Party shall:

(a) Use Confidential Information only for purposes of this Agreement;
(b) Protect Confidential Information using the same degree of care it uses for its own confidential information, but not less than reasonable care;
(c) Limit disclosure to personnel with a need to know who are bound by confidentiality obligations;
(d) Not disclose Confidential Information to third parties without prior written consent.

#### 7.3.2 Required Disclosure

If Receiving Party is compelled by law to disclose Confidential Information, it shall:

(a) Provide prompt notice to Disclosing Party (to the extent permitted);
(b) Cooperate with Disclosing Party's efforts to obtain protective treatment;
(c) Disclose only the minimum information required.

### Section 7.4: Duration

Confidentiality obligations shall continue for five (5) years after disclosure or, for trade secrets, for as long as such information remains a trade secret.

### Section 7.5: Return of Materials

Upon termination or Disclosing Party's request, Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all Confidential Information and certify such return or destruction in writing.

### Section 10.1: Limitation on Damages

#### 10.1.2 Cap on Direct Damages

EXCEPT FOR BREACHES OF ARTICLE 7 (CONFIDENTIALITY), INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS UNDER ARTICLE 9, OR GROSS NEGLIGENCE OR WILLFUL MISCONDUCT, EACH PARTY'S TOTAL LIABILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED THE GREATER OF:

(A) THE TOTAL FEES PAID OR PAYABLE BY CLIENT UNDER THE STATEMENT OF WORK GIVING RISE TO THE CLAIM DURING THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS PRECEDING THE CLAIM; OR

(B) ONE MILLION DOLLARS ($1,000,000).

### Section 10.2: Basis of the Bargain

THE LIMITATIONS IN THIS ARTICLE 10 REFLECT THE ALLOCATION OF RISK BETWEEN THE PARTIES AND ARE AN ESSENTIAL BASIS OF THE BARGAIN. THE PARTIES WOULD NOT HAVE ENTERED INTO THIS AGREEMENT WITHOUT THESE LIMITATIONS.

## ARTICLE 11: DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### Section 11.1: Informal Resolution

The Parties shall attempt to resolve any dispute arising under this Agreement through good faith negotiations between their respective Project Managers. If unresolved within fifteen (15) days, the dispute shall be escalated to senior executives.

### Section 11.2: Mediation

If the dispute remains unresolved after thirty (30) days of escalation, the Parties shall submit the dispute to non-binding mediation administered by [MEDIATION ORGANIZATION] before initiating any other proceeding.

### Section 11.3: Arbitration

If the dispute is not resolved through mediation within sixty (60) days, the dispute shall be resolved by binding arbitration administered by [ARBITRATION ORGANIZATION] in accordance with its Commercial Arbitration Rules. The arbitration shall take place in [CITY, STATE], and the award shall be enforceable in any court of competent jurisdiction.

### Section 11.4: Injunctive Relief

Nothing in this Article shall prevent either Party from seeking injunctive or other equitable relief in any court of competent jurisdiction to prevent irreparable harm.

### Section 11.5: Continued Performance

During any dispute, the Parties shall continue to perform their obligations under this Agreement unless terminated in accordance with Article 3.

## ARTICLE 12: GENERAL PROVISIONS

Neither Party shall be liable for delay or failure to perform due to causes beyond its reasonable control ("Force Majeure Event"), including acts of God, war, terrorism, natural disasters, epidemics, government actions, or labor disputes.
