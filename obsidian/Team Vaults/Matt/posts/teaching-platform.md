## Audience Decision Framework

### College Level
**Pros:**
- Structured semester format = deep learning
- Can build curriculum around the platform
- Academic credibility
- Research opportunities

**Cons:**
- Slow adoption process
- Budget/purchasing hurdles
- Less flexibility

**Best For:** Teaching software architecture, distributed systems, AI/ML concepts

### Boot Camp Level
**Pros:**
- Fast-paced learning environment
- Students motivated to learn practical skills
- Can showcase portfolio projects
- Industry connections

**Cons:**
- Short timeframes = surface-level learning
- May need simplified versions

**Best For:** Practical AI/automation skills, career-focused learning

### Individual Level
**Pros:**
- Personalized learning paths
- Direct mentorship opportunity
- Flexible formats (1-on-1, small groups)
- Can charge premium

**Cons:**
- Limited scale
- Marketing challenges
- Time-intensive

**Best For:** High-value coaching, executive education, specialized niches

### Corporate Training
**Pros:**
- Higher budgets
- Immediate practical application
- Recurring revenue potential
- Case studies from real business use

**Cons:**
- Longer sales cycles
- Customization requirements
- Compliance/security considerations

**Best For:** Enterprise AI adoption, team training, upskilling programs

---

## Recommendation: Start with ONE, expand to others

**My suggestion:** Lead with **corporate training** or **boot camp level** for fastest validation, then expand based on what works.

**Why:**
- Corporate = immediate ROI proof
- Boot camp = rapid iteration and feedback
- Both have clearer value propositions than "education in general"
